1. Finding your way around

The left sidebar is your main menu. The top group is the clinical workflow — Dashboard and Patients; below that Clinical Intelligence (reasoning, follow-up, knowledge base) and Research (analytics, studies, safety, pathology, biomarkers, quality, export).

The “Jump to patient…” box in the top bar finds and opens any patient by ID, name or phone from any page. The Home button returns to the dashboard.

2. Registering a patient
  1. Sidebar → Patients → Add Patient (or the “Register New Patient” card on the dashboard).
  2. Complete the guided steps — demographics then clinical/enrolment details. A duplicate check warns if the hospital ID or name already exists.
  3. On the patient page, work down the status strip: Registered → Baseline → Prescription → Follow-up → Outcomes. Add the baseline assessment and record consent.

Tip: fields left blank show up later as information gaps and care gaps on the patient’s reasoning tab, so complete what you can.

3. Recording a visit

Open the patient and use the tabs / action buttons on the patient page:

  • Labs — order a panel and enter results (creatinine/eGFR, UPCR/ACR, serology, etc.).
  • Biopsies — record histology; disease-specific scoring forms are available (IgAN, membranous, lupus, FSGS).
  • Prescription — build a prescription (up to 12 medication lines) and print/save the PDF.
  • Follow-up (Visits) — record a follow-up visit, response, relapse or admission.

Saving new data automatically recomputes the patient’s clinical profile, so the reasoning tab stays current.

4. Clinical reasoning & decision support

The Clinical Reasoning and CDS Plans tabs on the patient page show the leading differential, care pathway, information/care gaps, risk assessment, and suggested management and monitoring.

This is decision support, not a decision. Every suggestion is meant to be reviewed against the guideline reference shown and your own clinical judgement — you remain responsible for the plan.

5. Analysis & dashboards
  • Dashboard — Overview, plus Enrollment, Outcomes and Compliance views of the whole registry.
  • Analytics — cohort analytics including survival (Cox), eGFR-slope, and cumulative-incidence (CIF) result pages.
  • Quality — guideline-adherence and data-completeness measures for continuous improvement.
6. Exporting data

Sidebar → Export. Choose a format:

  • CSV — plain tabular data.
  • Excel — includes a second data dictionary (codebook) sheet.
  • SPSS (.sav) — fully labelled (variable labels, measurement levels, value labels) so it opens analysis-ready.

Exports are de-identified by default. An identified export (with names/IDs) requires the data_manager role — ask your administrator if you need it.

Something not working, or need a backup/restore or a new account? See the Administrator Guide (staff) or ask your site administrator.
