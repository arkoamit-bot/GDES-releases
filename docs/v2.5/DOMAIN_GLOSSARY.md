# GDES Domain Glossary — Ubiquitous Language

**Version:** 2.5  
**Rule:** Every term has exactly one meaning. Every module uses identical terminology.

---

## A

**Active Disease** — Phase where GN is clinically active, typically requiring immunosuppression. Maps to `current_phase="active"`.

**Adverse Event** — Untoward medical occurrence in a patient during treatment. Not necessarily causally related.

**Aggregate** — DDD cluster of domain objects treated as a single unit. Every aggregate has one root entity.

**Audit Log** — Immutable record of who changed what and when. Captured by `AuditLog` model and `AuditedModelViewSet`.

---

## B

**BGD-NNNNN** — Auto-generated unique patient identifier format. Prefix BGD + 5-digit zero-padded sequence.

**Biomarker** — Measurable indicator of disease state (e.g., anti-PLA2R, anti-GBM).

**Biopsy** — Renal tissue sampling with histological, immunofluorescence, and electron microscopy analysis.

**Bounded Context** — DDD boundary within which a particular domain model applies. Each context has its own ubiquitous language.

---

## C

**Care Gap** — Missing investigation, monitoring, or treatment that should be present for standard of care.

**Care Pathway** — 8-stage clinical pathway (assessment → active → remission → relapse → CKD → ESKD → transplant → conservative) with defined transitions.

**Clinical Encounter** — A patient visit or consultation with a healthcare provider.

**Clinical Event** — Significant clinical occurrence (remission, relapse, ESKD onset, death).

**Clinical Insight** — Individual actionable finding generated by the reasoning engine. Categorized as diagnostic, prognostic, therapeutic, monitoring, safety, care gap, or research.

**Clinical Profile** — Per-patient aggregated intelligence combining rule evaluation, trajectory, risk, pathway, evidence, and reasoning chain.

**Cohort** — Group of patients sharing a clinical characteristic for research purposes.

**Conflict (Rule)** — Two active KnowledgeBaseEntry rules for the same disease with contradictory conditions on the same field.

**Consent** — Patient authorization for registry participation, biobanking, or research. Tracks version, status, and withdrawal.

---

## D

**Decision Request** — A request for AI-assisted clinical decision support for a specific patient-encounter combination.

**Decision Result** — The AI-generated recommendation including ranked differential, urgency, next steps, and traceability.

**Differential** — Ranked list of possible diagnoses with scores, matched rule counts, and evidence grades.

**Disease Milestone** — Key event in a patient's disease course: diagnosis, biopsy, remission, relapse, ESKD, treatment started/switched.

**Disease Phase** — Current stage of GN: suspected, active, relapse, remission, CKD, ESKD, post-transplant, conservative.

**Domain Event** — Something of business significance that happened in the domain. Immutable record of a past occurrence.

---

## E

**eGFR** — Estimated Glomerular Filtration Rate. Key measure of kidney function. Computed from serum creatinine.

**Encounter** — See Clinical Encounter.

**ESKD** — End-Stage Kidney Disease. eGFR < 15 mL/min/1.73m² or on dialysis.

**Event Handler** — Function subscribed to a domain event type. Executes business logic in response to the event.

**Evidence Entry** — Published literature reference supporting a knowledge rule. Includes DOI, PMID, evidence level.

**Evidence Grade** — Strength of evidence backing a rule: Level 1 (strong), Level 2 (weak), NG (not graded), OP (expert opinion).

**Evidence Summary** — Aggregated grade distribution across the differential for a ClinicalProfile.

**Explainability Report** — Comprehensive audit of why a clinical recommendation was made, including triggering findings, matched rules, rejected alternatives, and information gaps.

---

## F

**FHIR** — Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources (HL7 R4 standard). External API for EHR integration.

**Follow-up** — Scheduled patient visit for ongoing monitoring.

---

## G

**Guideline Document** — Imported clinical guideline from which rules can be parsed automatically.

**Guideline Source** — Clinical guideline metadata (KDIGO, ASN, ISN, etc.) including version year and effective dates.

---

## I

**Information Gap** — Missing patient data that would improve diagnostic or prognostic confidence (e.g., no biopsy, missing serology).

**Insight** — See Clinical Insight.

**Integration Point** — Defined interface between two bounded contexts (API call, event, service call, FK reference).

---

## K

**Knowledge Base Entry** — A single clinical rule with conditions, weights, evidence grade, guideline references, and status.

**Knowledge Base Version** — Snapshot of a KnowledgeBaseEntry at a point in time. Append-only history.

---

## L

**Lab Result** — Quantitative or qualitative laboratory test result for a patient.

**Lab Test** — Definition of a laboratory assay including code, name, unit, reference range.

---

## M

**Milestone** — See Disease Milestone.

**Multi-Center** — Registry deployment across multiple hospital sites with site-scoped data access.

---

## O

**Operational Intelligence** — Registry-wide compliance metrics, gap trends, and operational dashboards.

**Outcome** — Computed clinical endpoint: remission (complete/partial), relapse, ESKD, death.

**Override** — Clinician action to accept or override an AI-generated recommendation, including alternative diagnosis and rationale.

---

## P

**Pathology Review** — A pathologist's interpretation of a biopsy specimen. Multiple reviews possible per biopsy.

**Pathway** — See Care Pathway.

**Patient** — Central domain entity. A person with suspected or diagnosed glomerular disease enrolled in the registry.

**Phenotype** — Observable clinical presentation derived from patient features.

**Prescription** — Clinician-issued medication order. Versioned and reconciled.

**Protocol** — Research study protocol with eligibility criteria and target enrollment.

**Proteinuria** — Protein in urine. Key disease activity marker. Measured as UPCR, UTP, or dipstick.

---

## R

**Rate Limiter** — Mechanism to cap API request frequency per actor. Currently in-memory; should be Redis-backed.

**Reasoning Chain** — Ordered list of reasoning steps showing how the engine arrived at its conclusions.

**Recommendation** — Actionable suggestion generated by the decision support or clinical reasoning engine.

**Reference Range** — Normal range for a lab test value.

**Registry** — The central patient database and clinical data repository.

**Relapse** — Disease recurrence after a period of remission.

**Remission** — Clinical state where disease activity is suppressed. May be complete or partial depending on disease-specific criteria.

**Repository** — DDD pattern mediating between domain and data mapping layers. Implementation: Django ORM Manager/QuerySet.

**Research Intelligence** — Cohort discovery, protocol matching, and research opportunity detection.

**Risk Assessment** — Evaluation of progression risk, relapse risk, and kidney survival probability.

**Rule** — See Knowledge Base Entry.

**Rule Quality** — Multi-dimensional score (completeness, clarity, evidence, testability) for a knowledge rule.

**Rule Template** — Reusable condition schema for building rules consistently.

---

## S

**Site** — A hospital or clinical center participating in the multi-center registry.

**Study** — A research study with defined protocol, eligibility, and enrollment.

---

## T

**Traceability** — The ability to trace a recommendation back to the specific knowledge rules and patient features that generated it.

**Trajectory** — Assessment of disease course direction: stable, declining, improving, end-stage.

**Treatment Exposure** — A period during which a patient was exposed to a specific drug treatment.

---

## U

**Ubiquitous Language** — DDD principle: a language structured around the domain model, used consistently by all team members and in all code.

**User** — System user with assigned role (data_manager, coordinator, investigator, pathologist, statistician, readonly).

---

## V

**Value Object** — DDD: an object that describes some characteristic or attribute but has no conceptual identity. Immutable and comparable by value.

**Visit** — See Clinical Encounter.
