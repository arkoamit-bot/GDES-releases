## 1. Overview

This document presents 8 clinical cases covering the full spectrum of anti-GBM disease presentations, including classic Goodpasture syndrome with DAH, renal-limited disease in elderly patients, double-positive overlap, trigger-induced disease, treatment complications, paediatric presentation, post-transplant recurrence, and the rare isolated pulmonary variant. Each case follows a standardised format including presentation, investigations, diagnosis, management, and teaching points. The cases are designed to train clinicians to recognise the bimodal age distribution, initiate same-day treatment, and navigate the key decision points in anti-GBM disease management.

## 2. CASE-ANTIGBM-RAP-001: Classic Goodpasture Syndrome — RPGN with DAH

**Classification**: Rapid Presentation  
**Phenotype**: Goodpasture syndrome (pulmonary-renal)  
**Source**: Based on Levy et al. JASN 2001 cohort

### Presentation

A 24-year-old male smoker (15 pack-years) presents to the emergency department with a 5-day history of cough productive of blood-streaked sputum, worsening dyspnoea, and oliguria over 48 hours. He reports fatigue and 4 kg weight loss over the preceding month. On examination he is febrile (38.2 C), tachycardic (110/min), tachypnoeic (28/min), hypoxic (SpO2 85% on room air), and hypertensive (165/95 mmHg). Crackles are heard bilaterally to the mid-zones. Lower extremities show 1+ pitting oedema. Urine is tea-coloured on visual inspection.

### Investigations

| Test | Result | Reference Range |
|------|--------|-----------------|
| Serum creatinine | 6.2 mg/dL | 0.7-1.3 mg/dL |
| eGFR | 11 mL/min/1.73m2 | >90 mL/min/1.73m2 |
| Anti-GBM antibodies | >200 U/mL (strongly positive) | <20 U/mL |
| ANCA (PR3, MPO) | Negative | Negative |
| Haemoglobin | 7.2 g/dL | 13-17 g/dL |
| CRP | 210 mg/dL | <5 mg/dL |
| Urinalysis | Dysmorphic RBCs (3+), RBC casts | Negative |
| UPCR | 2.1 g/g | <0.3 g/g |
| A-a gradient | 52 mmHg | <10 mmHg |
| Chest CT | Bilateral diffuse ground-glass opacities and consolidation | Active DAH |

### Renal Biopsy

22 glomeruli obtained. Light microscopy: necrotising crescentic GN in 18/22 glomeruli (82%), predominantly cellular crescents with fibrinoid necrosis. 4 normal glomeruli. Immunofluorescence: linear IgG staining along GBM (3+), linear C3 staining (2+). Electron microscopy: no electron-dense deposits; GBM disruption. Chronicity: no fibrous crescents; minimal interstitial fibrosis (<5%).

### Diagnosis

Anti-GBM disease (Goodpasture syndrome) with severe RPGN and diffuse alveolar haemorrhage. High proportion of cellular crescents favours renal recovery potential.

### Management

1. Methylprednisolone 1 g IV daily x 3 (Day 0-2)
2. PLEX 60 mL/kg daily x 14 sessions (Day 0-13); anti-GBM negative on Day 10
3. Cyclophosphamide 3 mg/kg/day oral (200 mg daily) started Day 0
4. Prednisone 1 mg/kg/day (70 mg) with taper
5. TMP-SMX 800/160 mg daily for PJP prophylaxis
6. Supplemental oxygen (nasal cannula; weaned over 5 days)
7. Smoking cessation counselling initiated Day 1

### Outcome

Haemoptysis resolved by Day 3. Extubated from non-invasive ventilation by Day 5. Creatinine declined from 6.2 to 3.1 mg/dL by Day 14, and to 1.7 mg/dL by Week 8. Anti-GBM negative from Day 10. Cyclophosphamide continued for 8 weeks then switched to azathioprine 2 mg/kg/day. Prednisone tapered to 10 mg/day by month 4. At 6 months: Cr 1.2 mg/dL, eGFR 68, UPCR 0.3 g/g, anti-GBM negative. Azathioprine and low-dose prednisone continued to 12 months, then withdrawn. No relapse at 5-year follow-up.

### Teaching Points

| Point | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Medical emergency | Same-day PLEX + CyP + steroids; do not wait for full biopsy |
| Smoking association | Active smoker with DAH — smoking cessation is therapeutic |
| Cellular crescents | >80% cellular crescents predicts excellent recovery |
| PLEX duration | Guided by antibody titre, not fixed 14 sessions |
| Low relapse | Immunosuppression withdrawn at 12 months; no relapse |

## 3. CASE-ANTIGBM-ADV-001: Renal-Limited Anti-GBM in Elderly Female

**Classification**: Advanced Presentation  
**Phenotype**: Renal-limited anti-GBM GN  
**Source**: Based on elderly anti-GBM cohort

### Presentation

A 67-year-old female non-smoker with hypertension presents with progressive fatigue, anorexia, and lower extremity oedema over 4 weeks. She has no respiratory symptoms. Urinalysis in primary care showed 2+ protein and 3+ blood. Creatinine is 4.5 mg/dL (eGFR 12), increased from 1.1 mg/dL 6 weeks prior. She reports no haemoptysis, dyspnoea, or cough.

### Investigations

Anti-GBM antibodies positive (95 U/mL). ANCA negative. Chest imaging clear. Renal biopsy: 18 glomeruli, 10 with cellular crescents (56%), 4 fibrocellular, 2 fibrous, 2 normal. Linear IgG (3+) along GBM. Chronicity: moderate interstitial fibrosis (25%) and tubular atrophy.

### Diagnosis

Renal-limited anti-GBM disease in an elderly female (typical of the older bimodal peak).

### Management

1. Methylprednisolone 500 mg IV x 3 (reduced pulse due to age and infection risk)
2. PLEX 60 mL/kg daily x 12 sessions (anti-GBM negative on Day 8)
3. Cyclophosphamide 2 mg/kg/day oral (150 mg) — lower dose for age
4. Prednisone 0.5 mg/kg/day (35 mg) with slower taper due to age
5. TMP-SMX PJP prophylaxis

### Outcome

Creatinine plateaued at 3.2 mg/dL (eGFR 18) after induction. Anti-GBM negative from Day 8. Cyclophosphamide continued for 10 weeks (reduced course due to leucopenia at week 6; held for 1 week then restarted at 1 mg/kg/day). Switched to azathioprine at week 10. Prednisone tapered to 7.5 mg/day by month 6. At 12 months: Cr 3.0 mg/dL (eGFR 19), stable but no recovery to independent function. Listed for transplant assessment. No relapse at 3-year follow-up.

### Teaching Points

| Point | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Elderly phenotype | Renal-limited disease more common in older females |
| Reduced dosing | Age-adjusted CyP and steroids to reduce toxicity |
| Renal recovery limited | 56% cellular crescents but 25% interstitial fibrosis limited recovery |
| ESKD preparation | Early transplant evaluation once anti-GBM negative |
| Infection risk | Age >65 is an independent risk factor for treatment-related infection |

## 4. CASE-ANTIGBM-COM-001: Double-Positive ANCA and Anti-GBM Disease

**Classification**: Complications  
**Phenotype**: Double-positive (anti-GBM + MPO-ANCA)  
**Source**: Based on McAdoo et al. KI 2017 cohort

### Presentation

A 55-year-old male presents with oliguric AKI (Cr 7.8 mg/dL, eGFR 7), haemoptysis, and hypoxia (SpO2 82% on room air). He reports 3 months of fatigue, arthralgias, and 10 kg weight loss. ENT exam is unremarkable.

### Investigations

Anti-GBM positive (150 U/mL). MPO-ANCA positive (180 U/mL). PR3-ANCA negative. Renal biopsy: 14 glomeruli, all with crescents (100% involvement), 6 cellular, 5 fibrocellular, 3 fibrous. Linear IgG along GBM, pauci-immune staining in some glomeruli — consistent with overlap. Interstitial fibrosis 30%. Chest CT shows bilateral alveolar infiltrates.

### Diagnosis

Double-positive anti-GBM disease and MPO-ANCA vasculitis (poor prognosis variant).

### Management

1. Methylprednisolone 1 g IV x 3
2. PLEX 60 mL/kg daily x 14 sessions (anti-GBM negative by Day 12)
3. Cyclophosphamide 3 mg/kg/day oral (started same day)
4. Prednisone 1 mg/kg/day (75 mg) with taper
5. TMP-SMX PJP prophylaxis
6. Non-invasive ventilation for DAH

### Outcome

Anti-GBM cleared after 12 PLEX sessions. Dialysis-dependent at presentation; no renal recovery by week 8. MPO-ANCA persisted positive (60 U/mL) despite CyP. At 12 weeks, switched from CyP to rituximab 375 mg/m2 weekly x 4 for persistent ANCA positivity. MPO-ANCA negative by week 20. Remained dialysis-dependent. Listed for transplant after 6 months of serological remission. Received deceased-donor transplant at 18 months. Maintenance immunosuppression continued (tacrolimus, MMF, low-dose prednisone) for ANCA component. No recurrence of either disease at 3 years post-transplant.

### Teaching Points

| Point | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Double-positive prevalence | 5% of anti-GBM patients also have ANCA |
| Worst renal prognosis | <30% recover independent renal function |
| Dual treatment | PLEX + CyP for anti-GBM; ANCA requires longer maintenance |
| Rituximab for ANCA | Switch to rituximab for persistent ANCA positivity |
| Transplant feasible | Both diseases in remission; recurrence rate low |

## 5. CASE-ANTIGBM-ATY-001: Smoking and Hydrocarbon-Induced Anti-GBM

**Classification**: Atypical  
**Phenotype**: Triggered by environmental exposure  
**Source**: Based on case series of hydrocarbon-induced anti-GBM

### Presentation

A 33-year-old male painter presents with 2 weeks of haemoptysis and oliguria. He is a heavy smoker (25 pack-years) and reports daily occupational exposure to paint thinners and petroleum distillates without respiratory protection. Cr 4.2 mg/dL (eGFR 17).

### Investigations

Anti-GBM positive (120 U/mL). ANCA negative. Renal biopsy: 16 glomeruli, 12 cellular crescents, linear IgG. Chest CT: patchy ground-glass opacities consistent with mild DAH.

### Diagnosis

Anti-GBM disease (Goodpasture syndrome) triggered by smoking and occupational hydrocarbon exposure.

### Management

1. Methylprednisolone 1 g IV x 3
2. PLEX daily x 14 sessions
3. Cyclophosphamide 3 mg/kg/day oral
4. Prednisone 1 mg/kg/day with taper
5. Mandatory removal from occupational hydrocarbon exposure
6. Intensive smoking cessation programme

### Outcome

Anti-GBM negative by Day 13. Creatinine improved to 1.8 mg/dL (eGFR 42) by week 12. Cyclophosphamide continued for 10 weeks. Full renal recovery by 6 months (Cr 1.1 mg/dL, eGFR 72). Immunosuppression withdrawn at 12 months. Remained non-smoking and changed occupation. No relapse at 4-year follow-up.

### Teaching Points

| Point | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Environmental triggers | Hydrocarbons and smoking are established triggers |
| Occupational history | Inquire about paint, solvents, dry cleaning |
| Trigger removal | Critical for treatment success and relapse prevention |
| Good recovery | Young age and cellular crescents favour excellent outcome |

## 6. CASE-ANTIGBM-COM-002: Cyclophosphamide-Induced Haemorrhagic Cystitis

**Classification**: Complications  
**Phenotype**: Treatment complication  
**Source**: Based on CyP toxicity literature

### Presentation

A 28-year-old female with anti-GBM disease (Cr 3.5 mg/dL at diagnosis, no DAH) developed visible haematuria, suprapubic pain, and dysuria 4 weeks into oral cyclophosphamide therapy (cumulative dose 4.2 g). Urine culture negative. Urinalysis shows sterile haematuria with blood clots.

### Investigations

Cystoscopy shows diffuse mucosal inflammation and telangiectasias consistent with cyclophosphamide-induced haemorrhagic cystitis. No infection. No tumour.

### Management

1. Discontinue cyclophosphamide immediately
2. Aggressive IV hydration (3 L/day)
3. Continuous bladder irrigation (3-way catheter)
4. Mesna 200 mg IV q4h
5. Transfuse PRBCs for symptomatic anaemia (Hb drop to 8.5 g/dL)
6. Switch to rituximab 375 mg/m2 weekly x 4 for ongoing immunosuppression
7. Continue PLEX and steroids per protocol

### Outcome

Haematuria resolved after 5 days of bladder irrigation. Cyclophosphamide permanently discontinued. Rituximab completed 4 doses without complication. Anti-GBM negative. Renal recovery to Cr 1.4 mg/dL (eGFR 52). Switched to azathioprine maintenance. Cystoscopy at 6 months showed resolved inflammation. Long-term bladder cancer surveillance initiated (annual urine cytology).

### Teaching Points

| Point | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| CyP toxicity | Haemorrhagic cystitis in 5-15% (oral more than IV) |
| Cumulative dose | Risk increases with total exposure; keep course short |
| Mesna protocol | Mesna with hydration reduces risk for IV CyP |
| Rituximab rescue | Effective alternative when CyP must be discontinued |
| Bladder surveillance | Annual urine cytology post-CyP for malignancy screening |

## 7. CASE-ANTIGBM-PED-001: Paediatric Anti-GBM Disease

**Classification**: Special Population  
**Phenotype**: Paediatric anti-GBM  
**Source**: Based on paediatric anti-GBM case series

### Presentation

A 12-year-old female presents with 1 week of gross haematuria, periorbital oedema, and oliguria. She has mild cough but no haemoptysis. Cr 3.8 mg/dL (eGFR 18). Anti-GBM positive (180 U/mL). ANCA negative.

### Investigations

Renal biopsy: 20 glomeruli, 14 cellular crescents, linear IgG (3+). No fibrous crescents. Chest imaging clear.

### Diagnosis

Anti-GBM disease (renal-limited) in a paediatric patient.

### Management

1. Methylprednisolone 500 mg IV x 3 (weight-adjusted, 15 mg/kg/dose)
2. PLEX 40 mL/kg daily x 14 sessions (weight-adjusted)
3. Cyclophosphamide — decision made to use rituximab instead (fertility preservation)
4. Rituximab 375 mg/m2 weekly x 4
5. Prednisone 1 mg/kg/day (45 mg) with taper
6. TMP-SMX PJP prophylaxis
7. Growth and development monitoring plan

### Outcome

Anti-GBM negative by Day 14. Creatinine improved to 1.2 mg/dL (eGFR 62) by week 8, and 0.8 mg/dL (eGFR >90) by month 6. Azathioprine maintenance for 12 months. Prednisone withdrawn at 8 months. Normal growth and pubertal development at 2-year follow-up. No relapse at 4 years. Transition to adult nephrology planned at age 18.

### Teaching Points

| Point | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Paediatric rarity | <5% of all anti-GBM cases occur in children |
| Rituximab preferred | Avoids gonadotoxic cyclophosphamide |
| Weight-adjusted dosing | PLEX and steroids require paediatric dosing |
| Growth monitoring | Chronic steroids affect linear growth |
| Transition care | Structured transition to adult services |

## 8. CASE-ANTIGBM-REL-001: Post-Transplant Recurrence of Anti-GBM

**Classification**: Relapse  
**Phenotype**: Post-transplant recurrence  
**Source**: Based on transplant recurrence literature

### Presentation

A 40-year-old male with anti-GBM disease leading to ESKD received a deceased-donor kidney transplant 3 years ago. Pre-transplant anti-GBM was negative for 18 months. Current immunosuppression: tacrolimus, MMF, prednisone 5 mg/day. Presents with rising Cr (1.2 to 2.4 mg/dL over 4 weeks), new haematuria, and dyspnoea. Anti-GBM positive (75 U/mL). ANCA negative.

### Investigations

Renal biopsy: 12 glomeruli, 4 cellular crescents, linear IgG along GBM (2+). No rejection. No donor-specific antibodies. Chest CT: mild ground-glass opacities.

### Diagnosis

Post-transplant recurrence of anti-GBM disease (rare; <5% risk when antibody negative at transplant).

### Management

1. Methylprednisolone 500 mg IV x 3
2. PLEX daily x 10 sessions (anti-GBM negative by Day 8)
3. Rituximab 375 mg/m2 weekly x 4 (preferred over CyP in transplant setting)
4. Prednisone increased to 0.5 mg/kg/day with taper back to maintenance
5. Continue tacrolimus and MMF
6. TMP-SMX PJP prophylaxis

### Outcome

Anti-GBM negative by Day 8. Creatinine stabilised at 1.6 mg/dL (eGFR 48) by month 3. Complete resolution of pulmonary symptoms. No rejection episodes. At 5 years post-transplant, graft function stable (Cr 1.5 mg/dL, eGFR 51). Anti-GBM remains negative. No further recurrence.

### Teaching Points

| Point | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Recurrence risk | <5% if anti-GBM negative at transplant |
| Timing | Most recurrences occur within first 5 years |
| Treatment response | PLEX + rituximab effective in transplant setting |
| Graft survival | Excellent with prompt treatment of recurrence |
| ANCA double-positive | Higher recurrence risk driven by ANCA component |

## 9. CASE-ANTIGBM-ATY-002: Isolated Pulmonary Anti-GBM (Without Renal Involvement)

**Classification**: Atypical  
**Phenotype**: Isolated pulmonary haemorrhage  
**Source**: Based on case reports of renal-sparing anti-GBM

### Presentation

A 46-year-old female non-smoker presents with 2 weeks of haemoptysis, dyspnoea, and anaemia (Hb 8.9 g/dL). She has no urinary symptoms, normal urine output, and normal creatinine (0.9 mg/dL, eGFR 76). Urinalysis: no haematuria, no proteinuria.

### Investigations

Anti-GBM positive (65 U/mL). ANCA negative. Chest CT: bilateral ground-glass opacities consistent with DAH. Bronchoalveolar lavage shows progressively bloodier aliquots. Renal biopsy (performed despite normal urinalysis): 10 glomeruli, all normal on light microscopy. Immunofluorescence: linear IgG staining along GBM (1+) in a segmental pattern — subclinical antibody binding without glomerular injury. No crescents.

### Diagnosis

Isolated pulmonary anti-GBM disease (rare variant; <5% of cases).

### Management

1. Methylprednisolone 1 g IV x 3
2. PLEX daily x 10 sessions (anti-GBM negative by Day 7)
3. Cyclophosphamide 2 mg/kg/day oral x 8 weeks
4. Prednisone 1 mg/kg/day (60 mg) with taper
5. TMP-SMX prophylaxis

### Outcome

Haemoptysis resolved by Day 2. Anti-GBM negative by Day 7. Creatinine remained normal throughout. At 6 months: normal renal function, anti-GBM negative. Immunosuppression withdrawn at 12 months. No renal involvement developed. No relapse at 3-year follow-up.

### Teaching Points

| Point | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Isolated pulmonary | Extremely rare; <5% of anti-GBM disease |
| Renal biopsy value | Detects subclinical linear IgG deposition |
| Treat fully | Same therapy as pulmonary-renal disease |
| Renal monitoring | Long-term surveillance for late renal involvement |
| Excellent prognosis | Normal renal function at presentation predicts full recovery |
