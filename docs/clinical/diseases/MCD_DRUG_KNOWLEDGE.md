## 1. Document Purpose

This document provides the complete drug knowledge specification for Minimal Change Disease pharmacotherapy. It covers first-line corticosteroids, steroid-sparing agents (CNI, MMF, cyclophosphamide, rituximab), supportive medications, adverse effect profiles, drug monitoring requirements, and vaccination guidance.

## 3. Corticosteroids

### 3.1 Dosing Regimens

| Population | Induction Dose | Duration | Taper |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Children** | Prednisone 60 mg/m2/day (max 60 mg) | 4 weeks | Alternate-day taper over 3-6 months |
| **Adults** | Prednisone 1 mg/kg/day (max 80 mg) | 4-16 weeks | Alternate-day taper over 3-6 months |
| **Elderly** | Prednisone 0.75-1 mg/kg/day (max 60 mg) | 8-16 weeks | Slower taper due to higher toxicity risk |
| **Pulsed** | Methylprednisolone 0.5-1 g IV x 3 days | 3 doses | Transition to oral prednisone |

### 3.2 Adverse Effects

| System | Adverse Effect | Incidence | Monitoring |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Metabolic** | Hyperglycemia / steroid-induced diabetes | 10-20% adults | Fasting glucose at baseline and monthly during therapy |
| **Bone** | Osteoporosis, avascular necrosis | 5-15% | DEXA scan at baseline and annually; calcium + vitamin D |
| **Ocular** | Cataracts (posterior subcapsular) | 10-20% | Ophthalmology exam every 6-12 months |
| **Growth (children)** | Growth retardation | 20-40% | Growth velocity chart every 3 months |
| **Cardiovascular** | Hypertension | 15-20% | BP monitoring weekly |
| **Psychiatric** | Psychosis, mood disturbance | 3-5% | Clinical assessment; low threshold for referral |
| **Immunologic** | Increased infection risk | All | Infection surveillance; PCP prophylaxis if prolonged high-dose |
| **Dermatologic** | Acne, skin thinning, striae, hirsutism | 20-40% | Reassurance; symptomatic management |
| **GI** | Peptic ulcer, pancreatitis | 1-3% | PPI prophylaxis if additional risk factors |

### 3.3 Key Management Points

- Morning dosing minimises adrenal suppression
- Alternate-day taper reduces toxicity while maintaining efficacy
- Adrenal insufficiency risk on abrupt cessation: taper over weeks
- Growth retardation is dose- and duration-dependent; minimise cumulative exposure
- Baseline DEXA and glucose should be obtained before starting

## 4. Calcineurin Inhibitors

### 4.1 Dosing Regimens

| Drug | Dose | Target Trough Level | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Cyclosporine** | 3-5 mg/kg/day divided q12h | 100-150 ng/mL | 12-24 months |
| **Tacrolimus** | 0.05-0.1 mg/kg/day divided q12h | 5-10 ng/mL | 12-24 months |

### 4.2 Adverse Effects

| Adverse Effect | Frequency | Cyclosporine | Tacrolimus |
|---|---|---|---|
| Nephrotoxicity | 15-30% | ++ | ++ |
| Hypertension | 20-40% | ++ | + |
| Diabetes (new-onset) | 5-15% | + | ++ |
| Tremor | 20-40% | + | ++ |
| Hirsutism | 30-60% | ++ | - |
| Gingival hyperplasia | 20-40% | ++ | - |
| Hyperkalemia | 10-20% | ++ | ++ |
| Hypomagnesemia | 20-40% | ++ | ++ |
| Hyperuricemia | 10-20% | ++ | + |

### 4.3 Monitoring Protocol

| Parameter | Baseline | Frequency | Target |
|---|---|---|---|
| Serum creatinine | Yes | Every 2 weeks until stable, then monthly | <25% rise from baseline |
| Trough level | — | Every 2 weeks initially, then quarterly | Per drug target |
| BP | Yes | Every visit | <130/80 mmHg |
| Potassium | Yes | Monthly | 3.5-5.0 mEq/L |
| Magnesium | Yes | Monthly | >1.8 mg/dL |
| Urine protein | Yes | Weekly during induction | Complete remission |

### 4.4 Drug Interactions

- **Strong CYP3A4 inhibitors** (ketoconazole, diltiazem, verapamil, macrolides): increase CNI levels
- **Strong CYP3A4 inducers** (rifampin, phenytoin, carbamazepine): decrease CNI levels
- **NSAIDs**: increase nephrotoxicity (contraindicated in CNI patients)
- **RAAS inhibitors**: increase hyperkalemia risk

## 5. Mycophenolate Mofetil

### 5.1 Dosing

| Population | Dose | Frequency |
|---|---|---|
| **Adults** | 1.5-2 g/day | Divided q12h |
| **Children** | 600-1200 mg/m2/day | Divided q12h |
| **Mycophenolate sodium** equivalent | 720-1440 mg/day | Divided q12h |

### 5.2 Adverse Effects

| Adverse Effect | Incidence | Management |
|---|---|---|
| Diarrhoea | 20-40% | Dose reduction or split to q8h dosing |
| Nausea/vomiting | 15-30% | Divide doses, take with food |
| Leukopenia | 10-30% | Dose reduction; growth factor support |
| Anaemia | 10-20% | Iron supplementation; dose reduction |
| Infections | Increased risk | PCP prophylaxis; screening for BK virus |
| Teratogenicity | High (Pregnancy Category D) | Contraception mandatory; pregnancy test before initiation |

### 5.3 Key Advantage

Compared to CNI, MMF has no nephrotoxicity, making it the preferred first-choice steroid-sparing agent when renal function is a concern. The IMPORT trial demonstrated comparable efficacy to cyclophosphamide for steroid-dependent disease with a superior safety profile.

## 6. Cyclophosphamide

### 6.1 Dosing

| Population | Dose | Duration | Cumulative Limit |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Adults** | 2-2.5 mg/kg/day | 8-12 weeks | 500 mg/m2 cumulative maximum |
| **Children** | 2 mg/kg/day | 8-12 weeks | 168 mg/kg total |

### 6.2 Adverse Effects

| Adverse Effect | Incidence | Prevention/Monitoring |
|---|---|---|
| Haemorrhagic cystitis | 5-15% | Mesna, vigorous hydration, frequent voiding |
| Gonadal failure (male) | 30-50% (cumulative dose dependent) | Sperm banking before therapy |
| Gonadal failure (female) | 10-30% | Ovarian function monitoring |
| Leucopenia | 40-60% | CBC weekly; hold if WBC <3.0 x10^9 |
| Malignancy risk | 2-5% (long-term) | Avoid cumulative dose >500 mg/m2 |
| Nausea/vomiting | 60-80% | Antiemetic prophylaxis |
| Alopecia | 40-60% | Reversible after therapy completion |

### 6.3 Contraindications

- Active infection
- Pregnancy (Category D)
- Breastfeeding
- Prior cyclophosphamide exposure near cumulative limit
- Haemorrhagic cystitis history

## 7. Rituximab

### 7.1 Dosing Regimens

| Regimen | Dose | Frequency | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Standard** | 375 mg/m2 IV | Weekly x 2-4 doses | RITURNS |
| **Fixed dose** | 1 g IV | Days 1 and 15 | RITUXIMAB-NS |
| **Preemptive (SD)** | 375 mg/m2 IV | Single dose | Emerging practice |

### 7.2 Adverse Effects

| Adverse Effect | Incidence | Timing | Management |
|---|---|---|---|
| Infusion reactions | 20-40% | During infusion | Premedication; slow infusion rate |
| Serum sickness | 5-10% | 7-14 days post-dose | Corticosteroids; antihistamines |
| Hypogammaglobulinemia | 10-20% | 3-12 months post-dose | Check IgG levels; IVIG if severe infections |
| Neutropenia (late-onset) | 5-15% | 1-6 months post-dose | G-CSF support if needed |
| Hepatitis B reactivation | 1-5% | Variable | Screen HBsAg, HBcAb; antivirals if positive |
| PML (rare) | <0.1% | 6-12 months | MRI imaging; reduce immunosuppression |
| Infections | Increased | Variable | PCP prophylaxis; monitor for opportunistic infections |

### 7.3 Monitoring Post-Rituximab

| Parameter | Timing | Action |
|---|---|---|
| CD19/CD20 counts | 1 month; then every 3 months | Re-treatment when B-cell recovery |
| IgG levels | 3, 6, 12 months | IVIG if IgG <400 and recurrent infection |
| HBV serology | Before each course | Antiviral for HBsAg+ patients |
| ANC | Monthly x 6 months | Hold if ANC <1.0 |
| Urine protein | Monthly | Assess remission status |

### 7.4 Rituximab in Special Populations

- **Children:** Effective for FR/SD; 85% maintain remission at 12 months
- **Adults:** Comparable efficacy; higher infusion reaction risk
- **Elderly:** Increased infection risk; monitor closely
- **Pregnancy:** Category C; avoid unless benefit clearly outweighs risk

## 10. Vaccination Guidance

### 10.1 Recommended Vaccinations

| Vaccine | Timing | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Pneumococcal** (PCV13 + PPSV23) | Before immunosuppression | Annual revaccination for high-risk |
| **Influenza** (inactivated) | Annually | Avoid live attenuated |
| **Varicella** | If seronegative, before therapy | 2-dose series; defer if active immunotherapy |
| **Hepatitis B** | All patients | Check serology; vaccinate if non-immune |
| **Tdap** | As per age schedule | Safe during low-dose immunosuppression |
| **SARS-CoV-2** | Per local guidelines | Current recommendations |

### 10.2 Contraindicated Vaccines (During Immunosuppression)

- Measles-Mumps-Rubella (MMR)
- Varicella (if on >20 mg prednisone/day)
- Live attenuated influenza (Flumist)
- Yellow fever
- BCG
- Oral typhoid

### 10.3 Vaccination Principles in MCD

- Vaccinate early, before immunosuppression when possible
- Inactivated vaccines safe at any dose of immunosuppression
- Live vaccines contraindicated during high-dose steroids (>20 mg/d), within 3 months of rituximab, or during CNI/MMF/cyclophosphamide therapy
- Check vaccine titres and re-vaccinate after B-cell recovery post-rituximab
- Family members should receive all age-appropriate vaccines (including live) to reduce household transmission

## 11. Drug Selection Algorithm by Clinical Scenario

```
Steroid-Sensitive, Infrequent Relapse
  -> Steroid monotherapy + supportive care

Frequently Relapsing
  -> MMF (first-line) OR CNI (if MMF intolerant/ineffective)
  -> Rituximab if MMF/CNI failure

Steroid-Dependent
  -> Rituximab (preferred, high evidence)
  -> MMF (alternative)
  -> Cyclophosphamide (third-line, reserve)

Steroid-Resistant
  -> CNI (cyclosporine 3-5 mg/kg or tacrolimus 0.1 mg/kg)
  -> Re-biopsy to exclude FSGS
  -> Rituximab if CNI fails

Refractory (Failed CNI + Rituximab)
  -> Cyclophosphamide rescue
  -> Clinical trial consideration
```
