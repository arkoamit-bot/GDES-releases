## 2. Case Library

### CASE-FGN-MULT-001: Multiple Myeloma-Associated FGN

**Type:** atypical
**Source:** Myeloma-associated FGN

**History:**
A 68-year-old man with known IgG lambda multiple myeloma (Durie-Salmon stage II, on lenalidomide/dexamethasone) develops new nephrotic-range proteinuria (UPCR 4.5) and eGFR decline from 60 to 35 over 4 months.

**Examination:**
BP 145/88. 2+ edema. Bone tenderness. Fatigue.

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 4.5 g/g
- eGFR: 35 mL/min
- Cr: 1.8 mg/dL
- Albumin: 2.5 g/dL
- SPEP: IgG lambda M-spike 3.5
- sFLC: Kappa 8, lambda 450, ratio 0.02
- C3: Normal
- C4: Normal
- Beta-2-microglobulin: 5.2 mg/L
- Hb: 9.5 g/dL

**Biopsy Data:**
- LM: Mesangial hypercellularity, nodular sclerosis, no amyloid
- IF: IgG 3+ (lambda restricted — monoclonal), C3 2+, DNAJB9 2+, Congo Red negative
- EM: Fibrils 12-18 nm in mesangium and GBM — FGN pattern, not light chain deposition disease or amyloid

**Diagnosis:**
Fibrillary GN associated with multiple myeloma (IgG lambda) — MGRS-related

**Treatment:**
Daratumumab-based regimen added to lenalidomide/dexamethasone. Ramipril 10 mg. Supportive care. Hematology-oncology lead.

**Outcome:**
Month 3: UPCR 2.0, eGFR 42, M-spike 1.5. Month 6: UPCR 1.2, eGFR 45, M-spike 0.8. Renal and hematologic response to daratumumab.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- light chain deposition disease: score 0.2 — DNAJB9 positive fibrils confirm FGN; LCDD has non-fibrillary granular deposits

### CASE-FGN-STABLE-001: Slowly Progressive Idiopathic FGN

**Type:** atypical
**Source:** Slowly progressive FGN

**History:**
A 60-year-old woman found to have subnephrotic proteinuria (UPCR 1.2) and microscopic hematuria on routine labs. No edema. No known MGUS. No autoimmune disease. eGFR 68. Incidental finding.

**Examination:**
BP 128/76. No edema. No lymphadenopathy.

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 1.2 g/g
- eGFR: 68 mL/min
- Cr: 0.9 mg/dL
- Albumin: 3.8 g/dL
- SPEP/IFE: Negative
- sFLC: Normal
- C3/C4: Normal
- ANA: Negative
- Hep B/C: Negative

**Biopsy Data:**
- LM: Mild mesangial hypercellularity, no crescents, 5% IFTA
- IF: IgG 1+, DNAJB9 1+, Congo Red negative
- EM: Scattered fibrils 10-14 nm in mesangium

**Diagnosis:**
Idiopathic Fibrillary GN — early, slowly progressive

**Treatment:**
Ramipril 5 mg for renoprotection. No immunosuppression. Q6 monthly monitoring: eGFR, UPCR, BP. Annual SPEP/IFE (screen for late-onset MGRS).

**Outcome:**
3-year follow-up: UPCR stable 1.0-1.4, eGFR 62-68, no MGRS developed. Slowly progressive disease managed with RAASi alone.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- TBMD: score 0.1 — DNAJB9 positive fibrils on EM confirm FGN, not TBMD

### CASE-FGN-CRES-001: Crescentic FGN Treated with Immunosuppression

**Type:** complications
**Source:** Crescentic FGN

**History:**
A 52-year-old woman presents acutely with oliguria, hematuria, and rapidly rising Cr (1.0 to 3.5 over 2 weeks). She has known FGN (diagnosed 1 year ago, idiopathic, on RAASi only).

**Examination:**
BP 158/94. Oliguric (<400 mL/day). Edema. Pulmonary crackles (fluid overload).

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 5.5 g/g
- eGFR: 15 mL/min
- Cr: 3.5 mg/dL
- Albumin: 2.0 g/dL
- SPEP/IFE: Negative
- C3: Normal
- C4: Normal
- ANCA: Negative
- Anti-GBM: Negative
- ANA: Negative

**Biopsy Data:**
- LM: 40% cellular crescents, mesangial hypercellularity, 25% IFTA
- IF: IgG 3+, C3 2+, DNAJB9 3+
- EM: Fibrils 12-18 nm — FGN with severe crescentic activity

**Diagnosis:**
Crescentic FGN — rapidly progressive course

**Treatment:**
Methylprednisolone 1 g IV x3. Prednisone 60 mg daily. Cyclophosphamide 750 mg/m2 IV x2. PLEX not indicated (ANCA/anti-GBM negative). RAASi held during AKI.

**Outcome:**
Month 1: dialysis required (oliguric). Month 3: off dialysis, eGFR 22, UPCR 2.8. Month 6: eGFR 28, UPCR 1.5. Partial recovery. Remains CKD stage 4. Long-term immunosuppression with MMF.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- ANCA vasculitis: score 0.2 — ANCA negative, known FGN, DNAJB9 positive — crescentic FGN not ANCA GN

### CASE-FGN-TXR-001: Post-Transplant Recurrence of FGN

**Type:** complications
**Source:** Post-transplant FGN recurrence

**History:**
A 55-year-old woman with FGN (diagnosed age 50, ESKD at 53) received a deceased donor kidney transplant 2 years ago. She had MGRS (IgG lambda) that was treated with daratumumab before transplant. Now presents with new proteinuria (UPCR 3.2) and rising Cr.

**Examination:**
BP 138/84. 1+ edema. Graft non-tender.

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 3.2 g/g (was 0.2 post-transplant)
- eGFR: 38 mL/min (declined from 60)
- Cr: 1.8 mg/dL
- SPEP/IFE: IgG lambda M-spike 0.3 (low-level, was 0.8 pre-treatment)
- sFLC: Kappa 18, lambda 35, ratio 0.51 (abnormal)
- DNAJB9 antibodies: Pending

**Biopsy Data:**
- LM: Mesangial hypercellularity, no rejection
- IF: IgG 2+, C3 1+, DNAJB9 2+ — recurrent FGN
- EM: Fibrils 10-18 nm in mesangium — recurrent FGN; no rejection features

**Diagnosis:**
Post-Transplant Recurrent Fibrillary GN — despite pre-transplant clone-directed therapy

**Treatment:**
Restart daratumumab (16 mg/kg IV weekly x8 then q4w). Ramipril 10 mg. Tacrolimus continued. Prednisone 10 mg. Re-biopsy in 6 months.

**Outcome:**
Month 3: UPCR 1.8, eGFR 42, M-spike undetectable. Month 6: UPCR 0.8, eGFR 45. Partial response to daratumumab. Graft function stabilized.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- acute rejection: score 0.2 — DNAJB9-positive fibrils confirm recurrent FGN, not rejection

### CASE-FGN-SURV-001: Long-Term FGN Survivor with Favorable Outcome

**Source:** Long-term favorable FGN

**History:**
A 50-year-old woman diagnosed with FGN at age 45 (subnephrotic proteinuria, eGFR 75, DNAJB9 positive, Congo Red negative, no MGRS, no autoimmune disease). Treated with RAASi only. Now 5-year follow-up.

**Examination:**
BP 122/74. No edema.

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 0.6 g/g
- eGFR: 62 mL/min
- Cr: 1.1 mg/dL
- SPEP/IFE: Negative
- C3: Normal
- DNAJB9: Not repeated (no indication for re-biopsy)

**Biopsy Data:**
- performed: False
- note: Prior biopsy confirmed FGN. Stable — no repeat biopsy needed.

**Diagnosis:**
Fibrillary GN — favorable long-term course (5-year stable)

**Treatment:**
Ramipril 5 mg daily. Annual monitoring. No immunosuppression.

**Outcome:**
5 years from diagnosis: UPCR 0.6, eGFR 62. Slowly progressive but well-compensated. No MGRS developed (annual screening). Representative of FGN with slow progression — better prognosis.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- N/A

### CASE-FGN-CRO-001: FGN Associated with Crohn Disease

**Type:** typical
**Source:** Crohn-associated FGN

**History:**
A 45-year-old woman with Crohn disease (ileocolonic, diagnosed age 30, on infliximab) presents with new-onset proteinuria (UPCR 2.8) and microscopic hematuria. Crohn disease is currently active (diarrhea, abdominal pain, weight loss).

**Examination:**
BP 132/80. 1+ edema. Abdominal tenderness right lower quadrant. No rash or arthritis.

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 2.8 g/g
- eGFR: 72 mL/min
- Cr: 0.9 mg/dL
- Albumin: 3.0 g/dL
- SPEP/IFE: Negative — no monoclonal gammopathy
- sFLC: Normal ratio
- C3/C4: Normal
- ANA: Negative
- Fecal calprotectin: 450 mcg/g (active Crohn)
- Hep B/C: Negative

**Biopsy Data:**
- LM: Focal mesangial hypercellularity, mild IFTA (10%)
- IF: IgG 2+, C3 2+, DNAJB9 2+, Congo Red negative
- EM: Random fibrils 10-15 nm in mesangium

**Diagnosis:**
Fibrillary GN associated with active Crohn disease — autoimmune-associated FGN

**Treatment:**
Infliximab dose optimization (5 to 10 mg/kg). Ramipril 5 mg. Budesonide for Crohn flare. Renoprotection with RAASi.

**Outcome:**
Month 3: UPCR 1.8, Crohn symptoms improved. Month 6: UPCR 1.0, eGFR 75, calprotectin normal. Renal and GI improvement with Crohn disease control.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- lupus: score 0.1 — ANA negative, no SLE features, known Crohn disease — FGN secondary to autoimmune disease

### CASE-FGN-IDI-001: Idiopathic FGN with Rapid Progression

**Type:** typical
**Source:** Idiopathic progressive FGN

**History:**
A 70-year-old man presents with nephrotic syndrome (edema, UPCR 6.5, albumin 1.8), hypertension, and eGFR 28. No previous renal history. No MGUS on SPEP/IFE. No autoimmune symptoms. No malignancy on screening.

**Examination:**
BP 165/95. 3+ edema to thighs. Pulmonary edema on CXR. No lymphadenopathy.

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 6.5 g/g
- eGFR: 28 mL/min
- Cr: 2.4 mg/dL
- Albumin: 1.8 g/dL
- SPEP/IFE: Negative
- sFLC: Normal
- C3: Normal
- C4: Normal
- ANA: Negative
- Hep B/C: Negative
- Malignancy screen: Negative (colonoscopy, CT chest/abdomen)

**Biopsy Data:**
- LM: Mesangial hypercellularity, 30% crescents, 40% IFTA
- IF: IgG 3+, C3 2+, DNAJB9 3+, Congo Red negative
- EM: Fibrils 12-20 nm, random orientation, mesangial and subendothelial

**Diagnosis:**
Idiopathic Fibrillary GN — rapidly progressive with crescents, no identifiable underlying cause

**Treatment:**
Methylprednisolone 500 mg IV x3. Prednisone 60 mg daily. MMF 1 g BID. Ramipril 10 mg. Furosemide 80 mg. Eculizumab not indicated.

**Outcome:**
Month 1: eGFR 25, UPCR 4.0. Month 3: eGFR 18, UPCR 3.5 — treatment refractory. ESKD at 8 months. Started HD. Transplant evaluation — counsel on 30-40% recurrence risk.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- C3G: score 0.2 — Normal complement levels, IgG dominant on IF, and DNAJB9 positive confirm FGN not C3G

### CASE-FGN-MGR-001: MGRS-Associated FGN with Rituximab Response

**Type:** typical
**Source:** MGRS-FGN

**History:**
A 62-year-old man presents with 6-month history of lower extremity edema, foamy urine, and weight gain. He has known MGUS (IgG kappa) diagnosed 2 years ago. No other medical history. No GI symptoms. No malignancy.

**Examination:**
BP 148/86. 2+ pitting edema legs, periorbital edema. Fundoscopy: normal. No lymphadenopathy.

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 4.8 g/g
- eGFR: 48 mL/min
- Cr: 1.5 mg/dL
- Albumin: 2.2 g/dL
- SPEP: IgG kappa M-spike 1.2 g/dL
- IFE: IgG kappa monoclonal
- sFLC: Kappa 85 mg/L, lambda 12 mg/L, ratio 7.1
- C3: 82 (normal)
- C4: 28 (normal)
- ANA: Negative
- Hep B/C: Negative

**Biopsy Data:**
- LM: Mesangial hypercellularity, membranoproliferative pattern, mild IFTA (15%)
- IF: IgG 3+ (polyclonal kappa/lambda), C3 2+, C1q 1+, Congo Red negative, DNAJB9 3+
- EM: Randomly oriented fibrils 12-18 nm in mesangium and subendothelial space

**Diagnosis:**
Fibrillary GN, MGRS-associated (IgG kappa MGUS)

**Treatment:**
Rituximab 375 mg/m2 IV weekly x4. Ramipril 10 mg. Amlodipine 5 mg. Furosemide 40 mg. Hematology follow-up for MGUS.

**Outcome:**
Month 3: UPCR 2.2, eGFR 50. Month 6: UPCR 1.0, eGFR 52, M-spike 0.6. Month 12: UPCR 0.8, eGFR 54, M-spike undetectable. Partial renal and hematologic response.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- amyloidosis: score 0.1 — Congo Red negative and DNAJB9 positive exclude amyloidosis definitively
