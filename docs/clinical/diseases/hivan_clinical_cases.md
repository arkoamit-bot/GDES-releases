### CASE-HIV-EAR-001: Early HIVAN Detected on Screening

**Type:** atypical
**Source:** Early HIVAN screening

**History:**
A 45-year-old African American woman with known HIV (on ART, CD4 500, VL <40) is found to have new proteinuria (UPCR 1.8) on routine screening. She has no edema. eGFR 72. Recently switched from TDF to TAF due to tubular dysfunction.

**Examination:**
BP 128/78, no edema.

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 1.8 g/g
- eGFR: 72 mL/min
- Cr: 0.9 mg/dL
- Albumin: 3.5 g/dL
- HIV VL: <40 (suppressed)
- CD4: 500 cells/uL
- APOL1: G1/G1 (two risk alleles)
- Tubular function: Normal (off TDF now)

**Biopsy Data:**
- LM: Focal collapsing FSGS (10% glomeruli), mild microcysts, 5% IFTA
- IF: Negative
- EM: TRI present, early podocyte changes

**Diagnosis:**
Early HIVAN — detected on screening in a well-controlled HIV patient with APOL1 risk

**Treatment:**
Continue ART (dolutegravir + TAF + FTC). Add ramipril 5 mg. Monitor Q3 months.

**Outcome:**
12-month: UPCR 0.6, eGFR 68. Stable disease on ART + RAASi. Early detection preserved renal function.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- fsgs: score 0.2 — TRI on EM, APOL1 risk alleles, and HIV positivity point to HIVAN not idiopathic FSGS

### CASE-HIV-CHI-001: HIVAN in Perinatally Acquired HIV

**Type:** atypical
**Source:** Perinatal HIV HIVAN

**History:**
A 22-year-old African American woman with perinatally acquired HIV (on ART since birth, good adherence) presents with new proteinuria (UPCR 2.5) and eGFR 65. She is virally suppressed (VL <40), CD4 600. No prior renal issues.

**Examination:**
BP 130/80, no edema, normal development.

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 2.5 g/g
- eGFR: 65 mL/min
- Cr: 1.2 mg/dL
- Albumin: 3.2 g/dL
- HIV VL: <40
- CD4: 600
- APOL1: G1/G2
- Hep B/C: Negative
- Medications: DTG + ABC + 3TC (ART since infancy)

**Biopsy Data:**
- LM: Focal collapsing FSGS (10%), mild microcysts
- IF: Negative
- EM: TRI present

**Diagnosis:**
HIVAN in patient with perinatally acquired HIV — late-onset despite long-term viral suppression

**Treatment:**
Add ramipril 5 mg. Continue ART. Monitor Q3 months. APOL1 risk allele counseling. Family planning.

**Outcome:**
6-month: UPCR 1.0, eGFR 62. Stable on RAASi + ART. Illustrates that APOL1 risk can drive HIVAN even with controlled HIV.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- fsgs: score 0.2 — HIV positive + TRI on EM + APOL1 risk = HIVAN not idiopathic FSGS

### CASE-HIV-TDF-001: TDF Nephrotoxicity Mimicking HIVAN

**Type:** atypical
**Source:** TDF toxicity vs HIVAN

**History:**
A 40-year-old African American man with HIV on TDF-based ART (TDF + FTC + EFV) for 5 years. Presents with subnephrotic proteinuria (UPCR 1.5), eGFR 55, hypophosphatemia, and glycosuria. VL <40, CD4 500. No edema.

**Examination:**
BP 138/82, no edema.

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 1.5 g/g
- eGFR: 55 mL/min
- Cr: 1.4 mg/dL
- Phosphate: 1.8 mg/dL (low)
- Glycosuria: 3+ (normoglycemic)
- HIV VL: <40
- CD4: 500
- APOL1: G1/G0 (one risk allele)
- Urine protein: Tubular pattern (beta-2-microglobulin elevated)

**Biopsy Data:**
- LM: Mild tubular injury, no collapsing FSGS, no microcysts
- IF: Negative
- EM: No TRI, no podocyte changes

**Diagnosis:**
Tenofovir (TDF) Nephrotoxicity — tubular dysfunction, not HIVAN

**Treatment:**
Switch TDF to TAF (tenofovir alafenamide) — TAF has no nephrotoxicity. Continue ART with TAF + FTC + DTG. No RAASi needed (tubular proteinuria). Monitor phosphate, urine glucose.

**Outcome:**
3-month post-switch: eGFR 62, phosphate 2.8 (normal), glycosuria resolved, UPCR 0.4. Complete recovery of tubular function. Illustrates importance of distinguishing TDF toxicity from HIVAN.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- hivan: score 0.3 — No collapsing FSGS on biopsy, no TRI on EM, APOL1 low risk, tubular pattern = TDF toxicity not HIVAN

### CASE-HIV-IRIS-001: IRIS After ART Initiation

**Type:** complications
**Source:** HIVAN with IRIS

**History:**
A 36-year-old African American man with HIVAN (biopsy-proven collapsing FSGS, eGFR 28, UPCR 5.5) starts ART (dolutegravir + TAF + FTC). At week 3, develops fever, worsening edema, rising Cr (28 to 18), and new lymphadenopathy.

**Examination:**
BP 148/88, fever 38.5C, cervical lymphadenopathy, 2+ edema, enlarged liver.

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 6.5 (worsening)
- eGFR: 18 mL/min
- Cr: 3.2 mg/dL
- HIV VL: <40 (suppressed from 500K)
- CD4: 120 to 150 (rising)
- TB quantiferon: Negative
- CMV PCR: Negative
- EBV PCR: Negative
- Lymph node bx: Reactive hyperplasia, no malignancy

**Biopsy Data:**
- LM: Collapsing FSGS (stable compared to prior)
- IF: Negative
- EM: TRI present (stable)

**Diagnosis:**
HIVAN with Immune Reconstitution Inflammatory Syndrome (IRIS) after starting ART

**Treatment:**
Prednisone 0.5 mg/kg/day (30 mg) — taper over 4-6 weeks. Continue ART (do not stop). NSAIDs avoided (worsen AKI). Close monitoring.

**Outcome:**
Week 6: fever resolved, lymph nodes regressed, UPCR 3.5, eGFR 24. Month 3: off prednisone, UPCR 2.0, eGFR 30. IRIS resolved. ART continued.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- TB: score 0.3 — Negative TB quantiferon and lymph node histology = IRIS not TB

### CASE-HIV-RAP-001: Rapidly Progressive HIVAN without ART

**Type:** complications
**Source:** Advanced HIVAN ESKD

**History:**
A 30-year-old African American man with newly diagnosed HIV (CD4 50, VL 1.2M) presents with oliguric AKI, nephrotic-range proteinuria (UPCR 8.0), and eGFR 12. He has wasting syndrome, PCP pneumonia.

**Examination:**
BP 158/98, anasarca, respiratory distress (PCP), oral thrush, Kaposi lesions on trunk.

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 8.0 g/g
- eGFR: 12 mL/min
- Cr: 5.2 mg/dL
- Albumin: 1.2 g/dL
- HIV VL: 1,200,000 copies/mL
- CD4: 50 cells/uL
- APOL1: G2/G2
- Lactate: Normal
- Hep B: Negative

**Biopsy Data:**
- LM: Collapsing FSGS (80% glomeruli), diffuse microcysts, 50% IFTA
- IF: Negative
- EM: TRI, severe podocyte injury

**Diagnosis:**
Advanced HIVAN with ESKD at presentation — very high VL, low CD4, APOL1 risk

**Treatment:**
Start ART (dolutegravir + TAF + FTC). PCP treatment (TMP-SMX high dose). HAART initiation. Dialysis initiated (HD via tunneled catheter). Ramipril held (AKI). Prednisone not used (low CD4, active PCP, high IRIS risk).

**Outcome:**
Month 1: on HD, VL 8,000, CD4 80. Month 3: VL <40, CD4 150, still dialysis dependent. Month 6: VL <40, CD4 250, remains on HD — no renal recovery (too much scarring). Transplant evaluation initiated when CD4 >200 and VL suppressed.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- irgn: score 0.1 — Collapsing FSGS + TRI + HIV = HIVAN, not infection-related GN

### CASE-HIV-STAB-001: Stable HIVAN with Long-Term ART Adherence

**Type:** remission
**Source:** Stable long-term HIVAN

**History:**
A 50-year-old African American man with HIVAN diagnosed 10 years ago (eGFR 55, UPCR 3.5 at diagnosis, APOL1 G1/G1). Treated with ART + RAASi. Has maintained viral suppression and stable renal function for 10 years.

**Examination:**
BP 126/74, no edema.

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 0.4 g/g
- eGFR: 48 mL/min
- Cr: 1.5 mg/dL
- HIV VL: <40 (suppressed x10 years)
- CD4: 650
- APOL1: G1/G1

**Biopsy Data:**
- performed: False
- note: Prior biopsy confirmed HIVAN. No repeat biopsy needed.

**Diagnosis:**
HIVAN — long-term stable with ART + RAASi adherence

**Treatment:**
Dolutegravir + TAF + FTC. Ramipril 10 mg. Annual monitoring — HIV VL, CD4, eGFR, UPCR, BP.

**Outcome:**
10-year follow-up: eGFR 48 (slow decline from 55), UPCR 0.4. ART adherence excellent. No ESKD. Represents best-case scenario for HIVAN.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- N/A

### CASE-HIV-TXR-001: Successful Kidney Transplant for HIVAN

**Type:** remission
**Source:** HIV transplant success

**History:**
A 42-year-old African American woman with HIVAN (diagnosed age 38, ESKD at 40) on HD for 2 years. HIV well-controlled: VL <40, CD4 450 for >12 months. She receives a deceased donor kidney transplant.

**Examination:**
BP 132/78, well-functioning graft.

**Laboratory Data:**
- eGFR: 65 mL/min
- Cr: 1.1 mg/dL
- UPCR: 0.1 g/g
- HIV VL: <40
- CD4: 450
- Tacrolimus: 8 ng/mL

**Biopsy Data:**
- LM: No rejection, no collapsing FSGS
- IF: Negative
- EM: No TRI (donor kidney)

**Diagnosis:**
Successful kidney transplant — HIVAN with excellent graft function

**Treatment:**
Standard immunosuppression: tacrolimus + MMF + prednisone. Continue ART: dolutegravir + TAF + FTC. HIV VL Q1 month.

**Outcome:**
3-year post-transplant: eGFR 62, UPCR 0.1, VL <40, CD4 500. No HIVAN recurrence. Excellent outcome. Representative of HIV+ transplant success in ART era.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- N/A

### CASE-HIV-CLS-001: Classic HIVAN in African American Male

**Type:** typical
**Source:** Classic HIVAN

**History:**
A 38-year-old African American man presents with lower extremity edema, foamy urine, and weight loss over 3 months. He has risk factors for HIV (MSM, not on PrEP). No prior medical care. Reports fatigue, night sweats.

**Examination:**
BP 145/90, 2+ edema legs, oral thrush, wasting (BMI 19). No rash.

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 6.2 g/g
- eGFR: 32 mL/min
- Cr: 2.2 mg/dL
- Albumin: 1.9 g/dL
- HIV ELISA: Positive
- CD4: 120 cells/uL
- HIV VL: 850,000 copies/mL
- APOL1: G1/G2 (two risk alleles)
- Hep B/C: Negative
- Renal US: Enlarged kidneys 11.5 cm

**Biopsy Data:**
- LM: Collapsing FSGS (60% of glomeruli), tubular microcysts, moderate IFTA 25%
- IF: Negative for immune complexes — IgM trace in sclerotic segments
- EM: Tubuloreticular inclusions in endothelial cells, podocyte foot process effacement

**Diagnosis:**
HIVAN — classic presentation: African American, APOL1 risk, low CD4, high VL, collapsing FSGS

**Treatment:**
ART: dolutegravir 50 mg + TAF 25 mg + FTC 200 mg daily. Ramipril 10 mg. Amlodipine 5 mg. Fluconazole for thrush. PCP prophylaxis (TMP-SMX). ART adherence counseling.

**Outcome:**
Month 1: VL 12,000, CD4 180. Month 3: VL <40, CD4 280, UPCR 2.8, eGFR 38. Month 6: VL <40, CD4 350, UPCR 1.2, eGFR 42. Excellent response to ART + RAASi.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- drugInducedGn: score 0.1 — HIV positive, collapsing FSGS, TRI on EM, APOL1 risk = HIVAN not drug-induced
