### CASE-IGA-004: Advanced IgA Nephropathy with Progressive CKD in a Middle-Aged Man
**Type:** advanced
**History:**
A 45-year-old man with known IgAN diagnosed 12 years ago presents with worsening proteinuria and declining eGFR. He was initially managed with RAASi alone and had stable disease for 8 years. Over the past 4 years, proteinuria has gradually increased from 0.8 to 2.1 g/day despite maximally tolerated ARB. He had 2 episodes of macroscopic hematuria last year. Past medical history: hypertension (diagnosed 8 years ago), Gout (3 years ago). Medications: losartan 100 mg daily, amlodipine 5 mg, allopurinol 300 mg.
**Examination:**
BP 138/86 mmHg, pulse 80. BMI 32. Mild bilateral pedal edema. No rash, no joint swelling. Fundoscopy: mild hypertensive changes. CVS: S1S2 normal, no murmurs.
**Laboratory Data:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Serum creatinine | 1.9 mg/dL |
| eGFR | 38 (CKD-EPI) |
| UPCR | 2.1 g/g |
| Serum albumin | 3.6 g/dL |
| Uric acid | 7.8 mg/dL |
| Hb | 12.1 g/dL |
| C3 | normal |
| RBCs | 10-15 dysmorphic/hpf |
**Biopsy Data:**
| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| original_bx | M1E0S1T0C0 (12 years ago) |
| recent_bx_glomeruli | 18 |
| global_sclerosis | 6 |
| M | M1 |
| E | E0 |
| S | S1 |
| T | T2 |
| C | C1 (15%) |
| tubular_atrophy | 40% |
| interstitial_inflammation | present |
**Diagnosis:**
Progressive IgA Nephropathy with active lesions on re-biopsy. Oxford MEST-C M1E0S1T2C1.
**Treatment:**
TRF-budesonide 16 mg/day added. Dapagliflozin 10 mg started. BP medications adjusted. Allopurinol continued for gout.
**Outcome:**
At 6 months: proteinuria decreased from 2.1 to 1.4 g/g (33% reduction). eGFR stable at 37. BP 128/78. Continuing therapy. Reassess at 9 months.

### CASE-IGA-009: Recurrent IgA Nephropathy in Kidney Transplant
**Type:** advanced
**History:**
A 38-year-old man received a deceased donor kidney transplant 3 years ago for ESKD from IgAN. Maintenance immunosuppression: tacrolimus, MMF, prednisolone 5 mg daily. ### CASE-IGA-RISK-002: High-Risk IgA with Crescentic Disease
**Type:** rapid
**History:**
A 35-year-old male presents with macroscopic hematuria, rapid weight gain (8kg in 2 weeks), and frothy urine. He had an URTI 2 weeks ago. No prior renal history.
**Examination:**
BP 155/95 mmHg. Bilateral pitting edema to thighs. No rash.
**Laboratory Data:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| creatinine | 2.8 |
| eGFR | 28 |
| UPCR | 3.5 |
| serum_albumin | 2.9 |
| C3 | 110 |
| C4 | 25 |
| urine_rbc | >100/hpf |
| urine_casts | RBC casts + granular casts |
**Biopsy Data:**
| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| light_microscopy | Mesangial + endocapillary hypercellularity, 30% crescents |
| immunofluorescence | Dominant mesangial IgA (3+) |
| electron_microscopy | Mesangial and subendothelial deposits |
| oxford_score | M1 E1 S1 T0 C2 |
**Diagnosis:**
IgA nephropathy with active crescentic disease (M1E1S1T0C2)

**Outcome:**

### CASE-IGA-007: IgA Nephropathy in Pregnancy with Pre-Eclampsia Risk
**Type:** special
**History:**
A 31-year-old primigravida at 16 weeks gestation with known IgA Nephropathy (diagnosed 5 years ago). Pre-pregnancy: proteinuria 0.8 g/day, eGFR 78, BP 124/78 on losartan 50 mg. ACEi was discontinued at 6 weeks gestation. Now presents with new-onset hypertension and worsening proteinuria.
