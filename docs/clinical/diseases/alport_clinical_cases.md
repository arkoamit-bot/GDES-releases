## 2. Case Library

### CASE-ALP-ADA-001: Autosomal Dominant Alport (ADAS)

**Type:** atypical
**Source:** ADAS late-onset

**History:**
A 55-year-old man with longstanding microscopic hematuria and mild proteinuria (UPCR 0.6) presents with eGFR declining from 75 to 55 over 5 years. Mild high-frequency hearing loss attributed to age. No lenticonus. His father and paternal aunt had CKD in their 60s.

**Examination:**
BP 136/84. Fundoscopy: mild retinopathy. Audiometry: age-appropriate high-frequency loss.

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 0.6 g/g
- eGFR: 55 mL/min (declining)
- Cr: 1.4 mg/dL
- Genetic: COL4A3 c.3452G>A (p.Gly1151Asp) — heterozygous, autosomal dominant

**Biopsy Data:**
- LM: Focal segmental glomerulosclerosis (FSGS-like), 20% IFTA
- IF: No immune deposits
- EM: Mild GBM lamellation, not as severe as XLAS

**Diagnosis:**
Autosomal Dominant Alport Syndrome (ADAS) — late-onset, slowly progressive

**Treatment:**
Ramipril 10 mg. Empagliflozin 10 mg. Atorvastatin 20 mg. Annual monitoring. Genetic counseling: autosomal dominant — 50% transmission.

**Outcome:**
eGFR decline slowed from 4 to 2 mL/min/year after RAASi + SGLT2i. Proteinuria stable at UPCR 0.3. ESKD not expected until 70s if ever.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- FSGS: score 0.3 — Biopsy showed FSGS-like lesions but EM shows GBM lamellation and genetic testing confirmed COL4A3 = ADAS not primary FSGS

### CASE-ALP-SIB-001: Discordant Siblings — X-linked Alport

**Type:** atypical
**Source:** Discordant XLAS siblings

**History:**
A family presents for genetic counseling. Two brothers: Brother A (32) has ESKD and hearing loss; Brother B (28) has only microscopic hematuria (normal eGFR, no proteinuria, normal hearing). Both have the same COL4A5 mutation (missense).

**Examination:**
Brother A: BP 148/90 (on dialysis). Brother B: BP 118/74. Both: no lenticonus.

**Laboratory Data:**
- Brother_A: eGFR 8 mL/min (HD), UPCR 5.0 (pre-dialysis), hearing 60 dB loss
- Brother_B: eGFR 98, UPCR 0.1, normal hearing
- Genetic: Both: COL4A5 c.2345G>T (p.Gly782Val) — same mutation

**Biopsy Data:**
- Brother_A: EM: severe GBM lamellation, 80% sclerosis
- Brother_B: EM: mild GBM lamellation, no sclerosis

**Diagnosis:**
Discordant XLAS phenotype — same mutation, different severity (modifier genes/environment)

**Treatment:**
Brother A: on HD, transplant evaluation. Brother B: RAASi + SGLT2i for renoprotection, annual monitoring.

**Outcome:**
Brother A: transplant at 33 from living donor (wife). Brother B: stable at 30, UPCR 0.1, eGFR 92. Illustrates variable expressivity in XLAS even within same family.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- N/A

### CASE-ALP-XLF-001: XLAS Female Carrier with Mild Phenotype

**Type:** atypical
**Source:** XLAS female carrier

**History:**
A 45-year-old woman (mother of XLAS male) presents with persistent hematuria detected during family screening. She has mild proteinuria (UPCR 0.4) and normal eGFR (88). No hearing loss. No visual symptoms. She is asymptomatic.

**Examination:**
BP 124/78. Fundoscopy: mild retinal changes. Audiometry: normal for age.

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 0.4 g/g
- eGFR: 88 mL/min
- Cr: 0.7 mg/dL
- Genetic: COL4A5 c.1234G>A (same mutation as son) — heterozygous
- Hearing: Normal
- Urinalysis: 2+ blood, dysmorphic RBCs

**Biopsy Data:**
- performed: False
- note: Biopsy not needed — known COL4A5 mutation in XLAS carrier

**Diagnosis:**
X-Linked Alport Syndrome Carrier (heterozygous COL4A5) — mild phenotype

**Treatment:**
Ramipril 5 mg started for proteinuria. Annual monitoring: eGFR, UPCR, audiometry, ophthalmology. Reassured: 15-30% lifetime ESKD risk only. Genetic counseling for family planning.

**Outcome:**
3-year follow-up: UPCR 0.2 on ramipril, eGFR stable 86-90. No hearing loss. No lenticonus. Excellent prognosis.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- TBMD: score 0.2 — Could mimic TBMD but COL4A5 mutation confirms XLAS carrier status, not TBMD

### CASE-ALP-AGB-001: Post-Transplant Anti-GBM Disease in Alport

**Type:** complications
**Source:** Post-transplant anti-GBM

**History:**
A 28-year-old man with XLAS (COL4A5 large deletion, ESKD at 25) received a deceased donor kidney transplant 2 years ago. Presents with acute onset hematuria, oliguria, and rising Cr from 1.2 to 3.8 over 2 weeks.

**Examination:**
BP 162/98. Graft tender. Oliguric (<400 mL/day). Pulmonary edema on CXR.

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 4.2 g/g
- eGFR: 18 mL/min
- Cr: 3.8 mg/dL
- Anti_GBM: Positive (1:320)
- ANA: Negative
- ANCA: Negative
- Hb: 9.5 g/dL

**Biopsy Data:**
- LM: Crescentic GN (40% crescents), linear IgG along GBM
- IF: Linear IgG along GBM (3+) — anti-GBM disease
- EM: No immune complex deposits; GBM disruption

**Diagnosis:**
De Novo Anti-GBM Disease in Post-Transplant Alport Syndrome

**Treatment:**
Methylprednisolone 1 g IV x3. Plasma exchange 1.5 vol x10 sessions. Cyclophosphamide 750 mg/m2 IV x1. Rituximab 375 mg/m2 weekly x4. Haemodialysis resumed.

**Outcome:**
Month 1: dialysis-dependent, anti-GBM 1:40. Month 3: anti-GBM negative, graft function not recovered. Month 6: remained on HD. Second transplant considered with strict anti-GBM monitoring. High-risk recurrence.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- acute rejection: score 0.3 — Linear IgG and positive anti-GBM confirm anti-GBM disease, not rejection

### CASE-ALP-TRA-001: Alport Syndrome Post-Transplant — Excellent Outcome

**Type:** remission
**Source:** Post-transplant Alport

**History:**
A 35-year-old man with XLAS (COL4A5 truncating mutation, ESKD at 28, on HD for 4 years) received a living donor kidney transplant from his mother (COL4A5 carrier). Now 2 years post-transplant with excellent function.

**Examination:**
BP 126/78. Graft non-tender. No edema. Creatinine 1.0 mg/dL.

**Laboratory Data:**
- eGFR: 72 mL/min
- Cr: 1.0 mg/dL
- UPCR: 0.05 g/g
- Anti_GBM: Negative
- Hb: 13.5 g/dL
- Tacrolimus: 7.2 ng/mL

**Biopsy Data:**
- LM: No rejection, no GBM abnormalities
- IF: Normal collagen alpha-5(IV) in donor kidney GBM
- EM: Normal GBM (donor kidney)

**Diagnosis:**
Successful kidney transplant for Alport syndrome — no disease recurrence

**Treatment:**
Standard immunosuppression (tacrolimus, MMF, prednisone). Anti-GBM surveillance (annual). Audiometry and ophthalmology continued. Hearing aids still needed. No Alport recurrence.

**Outcome:**
5-year post-transplant: eGFR 65, no proteinuria, no anti-GBM antibodies. Excellent graft survival. Hearing loss stable with aids. Lenticonus stable.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- N/A

### CASE-ALP-ARA-001: Autosomal Recessive Alport (ARAS)

**Type:** typical
**Source:** ARAS consanguineous

**History:**
An 18-year-old woman of consanguineous parents presents with hematuria since childhood, nephrotic-range proteinuria (UPCR 5.2), eGFR 42, and rapidly progressive hearing loss. No family history of kidney disease (parents are first cousins — both heterozygous COL4A3 carriers, asymptomatic).

**Examination:**
BP 148/92. 2+ edema. Fundoscopy: retinopathy. Audiometry: 50 dB loss. Anterior lenticonus (early).

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 5.2 g/g
- eGFR: 42 mL/min
- Cr: 1.8 mg/dL
- Albumin: 2.6 g/dL
- Genetic: COL4A3 homozygous c.256G>A (p.Gly86Arg)
- Hearing: 50 dB loss

**Biopsy Data:**
- LM: Mesangial expansion, segmental sclerosis, interstitial foam cells
- IF: No immune deposits
- EM: GBM lamellation, alternating lucent and dense layers

**Diagnosis:**
Autosomal Recessive Alport Syndrome (ARAS) — biallelic COL4A3 mutation

**Treatment:**
Ramipril 10 mg. Empagliflozin 10 mg. Furosemide 40 mg for edema. Hearing aids. Genetic counseling for autosomal recessive inheritance (25% recurrence risk).

**Outcome:**
Proteinuria improved to UPCR 2.8 at 6 months. eGFR declined to 38 at 1 year. ESKD developed at age 22. Living-related donor transplant (heterozygous parent — low risk donor). No recurrence post-transplant.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- XLAS: score 0.1 — Female, no family history of kidney disease, consanguineous parents — ARAS confirmed by COL4A3 homozygosity

### CASE-ALP-PED-001: Child with Early Detection and RAASi

**Type:** typical
**Source:** Childhood Alport early detection

**History:**
A 6-year-old boy (son of a known XLAS male) was found to have microscopic hematuria on screening urinalysis. No symptoms. Normal hearing. Normal development. Genetic testing confirms COL4A5 mutation (same as father).

**Examination:**
BP 100/60 (normal for age). No edema. Fundoscopy normal. Audiometry normal.

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 0.1 g/g
- eGFR: 110 mL/min
- Cr: 0.3 mg/dL
- Genetic: COL4A5 c.1234G>A — hemizygous
- Hearing: Normal

**Biopsy Data:**
- performed: False
- note: Biopsy not indicated for diagnosis — genetic confirmation sufficient

**Diagnosis:**
X-Linked Alport Syndrome — early stage (hematuria only, normal eGFR, no proteinuria)

**Treatment:**
Start RAASi early? Currently no proteinuria — monitor. Ramipril will be started at first sign of proteinuria (microalbuminuria). Annual follow-up.

**Outcome:**
Followed annually. At age 9, UPCR increased to 0.3. Ramipril 2.5 mg started. Stable at age 12: UPCR 0.2, eGFR 108. Hearing: normal. Early detection and planned RAASi expected to delay ESKD by 10+ years.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- TBMD: score 0.1 — COL4A5 mutation = Alport, not TBMD (which is COL4A3/COL4A4 heterozygous)

### CASE-ALP-XLA-001: XLAS Male with Classic Triad

**Type:** typical
**Source:** Classic XLAS

**History:**
A 22-year-old man with known persistent hematuria since age 3 now presents with progressive proteinuria (UPCR 2.5), declining eGFR (52), and bilateral high-frequency hearing loss noted on college screening. Mother has microscopic hematuria (carrier). Maternal uncle died of kidney disease in his 30s.

**Examination:**
BP 142/88. Fundoscopy: dot-and-fleck retinopathy. Slit lamp: anterior lenticonus (bilateral). Audiometry: 45 dB loss at 4-8 kHz. 1+ edema.

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 2.5 g/g
- eGFR: 52 mL/min
- Cr: 1.6 mg/dL
- Albumin: 3.5 g/dL
- Hb: 12.5 g/dL
- Hearing: 45 dB loss at 4-8 kHz
- Genetic: COL4A5 c.1234G>A (p.Gly412Arg) — glycine missense

**Biopsy Data:**
- LM: Mesangial expansion, 30% glomerulosclerosis, interstitial foam cells
- IF: No immune deposits; absent alpha-5(IV) in GBM
- EM: GBM thickening with splitting and lamellation (basket-weave)

**Diagnosis:**
X-Linked Alport Syndrome (XLAS) — classic triad: nephropathy, hearing loss, lenticonus

**Treatment:**
Ramipril 10 mg. Empagliflozin 10 mg. Amlodipine 5 mg for BP. Hearing aids fitted. Genetic counseling initiated. Family cascade screening.

**Outcome:**
Month 6: UPCR 1.2, eGFR 50, BP 128/78. Year 2: UPCR 0.8, eGFR 46. Hearing aids improved quality of life. Transplant planning at eGFR <20.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- TBMD: score 0.1 — No hearing loss or lenticonus in TBMD; GBM splitting not thinning — confirms Alport
