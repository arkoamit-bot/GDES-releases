### CASE-DRG-TNF-001: Anti-TNF-Induced Lupus-Like GN

**Type:** atypical
**Source:** Anti-TNF lupus

**History:**
A 35-year-old woman with Crohn disease on infliximab for 2 years presents with arthralgia, malar rash, oral ulcers, and new proteinuria (UPCR 2.2) and microhematuria. No previous autoimmune disease.

**Examination:**
BP 132/80, malar rash, oral ulcers, swollen MCP joints. No edema.

**Laboratory Data:**

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| UPCR | 2.2 g/g |
| eGFR | 68 mL/min |
| Cr | 1.0 mg/dL |
| ANA | Positive (1:640) |
| Anti-dsDNA | Positive |
| C3 | 48 (low) |
| C4 | 10 (low) |
| PLA2R | Negative |
| Hep B/C | Negative |

**Biopsy Data:**

| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | Mesangial hypercellularity, mild endocapillary proliferation |
| IF | Full-house: IgG 2+, IgA 2+, IgM 1+, C3 2+, C1q 1+ |
| EM | Subendothelial and mesangial electron-dense deposits, tubuloreticular inclusions |

**Diagnosis:**
Anti-TNF (Infliximab)-Induced Lupus-Like Nephritis — indistinguishable from idiopathic Class III lupus

**Treatment:**
Stop infliximab. Hydroxychloroquine 400 mg daily. Prednisone 30 mg daily taper. MMF 1 g BID for renal involvement. Switch Crohn therapy to ustekinumab.

**Outcome:**
Month 3: Proteinuria UPCR 0.8, eGFR 72, ANA 1:160, dsDNA negative. Month 6: UPCR 0.3, eGFR 75. Near-complete resolution. No recurrence on ustekinumab.

**Differential Diagnosis:**

| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| lupus | 0.3 | Identical to idiopathic lupus — temporal relationship with anti-TNF and resolution upon withdrawal distinguishes as drug-induced |

### CASE-DRG-MIX-001: Overlapping Drug-Induced Patterns

**Type:** atypical
**Source:** Overlapping drug GN

**History:**
A 68-year-old man on chronic NSAID (ibuprofen) for arthritis who also recently started pembrolizumab for bladder cancer presents with AKI and mild proteinuria. The question is which drug is responsible.

**Examination:**
BP 144/88, 1+ edema, no rash.

**Laboratory Data:**

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 32 mL/min |
| Cr | 2.0 mg/dL |
| UPCR | 2.5 g/g |
| Eosinophils | 10% |
| ANA | Negative |
| ANCA | Negative |

**Biopsy Data:**

| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | Interstitial nephritis (ATIN) with eosinophils + mild mesangial proliferation |
| IF | Negative |
| EM | Mild podocyte changes, no deposits |

**Diagnosis:**
ICI-Induced ATIN (pembrolizumab) with incidental NSAID use — biopsy distinguishes pattern

**Treatment:**
Stop ibuprofen (suspect NSAID may contribute). Prednisone 1 mg/kg for ATIN. Pembrolizumab held temporarily.

**Outcome:**
Week 2: Cr 1.4, eosinophils normal. Week 4: Cr 1.1. ATIN resolved. ICI could be resumed. NSAID permanently avoided.

**Differential Diagnosis:**

| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| nsaid_mcd | 0.3 | Biopsy showed ATIN with eosinophils = ICI more likely than NSAID-MCD (MCD has no interstitial infiltrate) |

### CASE-DRG-ICI2-001: Checkpoint Inhibitor Collapsing GN

**Type:** complications
**Source:** ICI collapsing GN

**History:**
A 55-year-old woman with non-small cell lung cancer on nivolumab (anti-PD-1) + ipilimumab (anti-CTLA-4) develops acute nephrotic syndrome (UPCR 8.5, edema) and AKI (Cr 0.8 to 3.2) over 3 weeks after cycle 2. No hematuria.

**Examination:**
BP 155/92, 3+ edema anasarca, dyspnea.

**Laboratory Data:**

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| UPCR | 8.5 g/g |
| eGFR | 18 mL/min |
| Cr | 3.2 mg/dL |
| Albumin | 1.5 g/dL |
| HIV | Negative |
| ANA | Negative |
| ANCA | Negative |
| Anti-GBM | Negative |

**Biopsy Data:**

| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | Collapsing FSGS (40% glomeruli), ATIN overlapping |
| IF | Negative |
| EM | Podocyte foot process effacement, capillary collapse |

**Diagnosis:**
Checkpoint Inhibitor-Induced Collapsing GN (nivolumab + ipilimumab)

**Treatment:**
Discontinue ICI permanently (not safe to restart). Methylprednisolone 1 g IV x3. Prednisone 1 mg/kg/day. MMF 1 g BID. RAASi: ramipril 10 mg (once Cr stable). Dialysis initiated (AKI).

**Outcome:**
Month 1: dialysis-dependent. Month 3: off dialysis, eGFR 22, UPCR 4.0. Month 6: eGFR 20, UPCR 2.5. CKD stage 4 persistent. ICI not resumed — lung cancer under alternative therapy.

**Differential Diagnosis:**

| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| hivan | 0.1 | HIV negative, ICI therapy temporal association = ICI collapsing GN, not HIVAN |

### CASE-DRG-ICI-001: Checkpoint Inhibitor ATIN

**Type:** typical
**Source:** ICI-ATIN

**History:**
A 60-year-old man with metastatic melanoma on pembrolizumab (anti-PD-1) every 3 weeks presents after cycle 4 with AKI (Cr 1.0 to 2.8 over 2 weeks), sterile pyuria, and fatigue. No fever. No rash. No nephrotic syndrome.

**Examination:**
BP 142/84, no edema, skin: vitiligo (ICI effect).

**Laboratory Data:**

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 25 mL/min |
| Cr | 2.8 mg/dL |
| UPCR | 0.5 g/g |
| Urinalysis | Sterile pyuria (WBC 20-30), WBC casts, no eosinophiluria |
| Eosinophils | 8% |
| C3 | Normal |
| ANA | Negative |
| Pembrolizumab | Yes — 4 cycles |

**Biopsy Data:**

| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | Dense interstitial infiltrate (lymphocytes, plasma cells, eosinophils), tubulitis, no granulomas |
| IF | Negative |
| EM | Normal glomeruli, tubular injury |

**Diagnosis:**
Immune Checkpoint Inhibitor-Induced Acute Tubulointerstitial Nephritis (pembrolizumab)

**Treatment:**
Hold pembrolizumab. Prednisone 1 mg/kg (60 mg) daily. Taper over 6 weeks. Nephrology + oncology co-management.

**Outcome:**
Week 2: Cr 1.6, eosinophils normal. Week 4: Cr 1.0 (baseline). Steroids tapered. Pembrolizumab resumed after ATIN resolution (oncology decision). No recurrence. Illustrates successful ICI-ATIN management.

**Differential Diagnosis:**

| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| acute tubular necrosis | 0.3 | Sterile pyuria, WBC casts, eosinophilia and biopsy with tubulitis = ATIN not ATN |

### CASE-DRG-HER-001: Heroin-Associated FSGS (Heroin Nephropathy)

**Type:** typical
**Source:** Heroin nephropathy

**History:**
A 38-year-old man with 15-year history of IV heroin use presents with nephrotic syndrome (edema, UPCR 6.0) and eGFR 38. He continues to use heroin. No HIV. African American.

**Examination:**
BP 148/90, 2+ edema legs, needle track marks arms.

**Laboratory Data:**

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| UPCR | 6.0 g/g |
| eGFR | 38 mL/min |
| Cr | 1.8 mg/dL |
| Albumin | 1.9 g/dL |
| HIV | Negative |
| Hep C | Positive (HCV RNA 500K) |
| APOL1 | G1/G2 (two risk alleles) |
| C3 | Normal |

**Biopsy Data:**

| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | FSGS (classic NOS type), 35% IFTA |
| IF | IgM and C3 in sclerotic segments |
| EM | Podocyte foot process effacement, no TRI |

**Diagnosis:**
Heroin-Associated FSGS (Heroin Nephropathy) — APOL1 risk allele positive

**Treatment:**
Heroin cessation program (methadone/buprenorphine). RAASi: ramipril 10 mg. HCV treatment planned (DAA therapy). BP management. Counsel: high risk of progression without cessation.

**Outcome:**
Month 6: continued heroin use, UPCR 4.5, eGFR 28. Month 12: eGFR 18, ESKD imminent. Started HD. Illustrates rapid progression despite RAASi. Worse with APOL1 risk and continued use.

**Differential Diagnosis:**

| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| hivan | 0.1 | HIV negative, no TRI on EM, classic FSGS not collapsing = heroin nephropathy not HIVAN |

### CASE-DRG-LIT-001: Lithium-Induced Minimal Change Disease

**Type:** typical
**Source:** Lithium-MCD

**History:**
A 42-year-old woman with bipolar disorder on lithium carbonate 900 mg daily for 15 years presents with gradual onset of peripheral edema and foamy urine over 2 months. Lithium levels have been therapeutic (0.8 mEq/L). No other new medications.

**Examination:**
BP 128/76, 1+ edema legs.

**Laboratory Data:**

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| UPCR | 4.2 g/g |
| eGFR | 62 mL/min |
| Cr | 1.2 mg/dL |
| Albumin | 2.4 g/dL |
| Lithium | 0.8 mEq/L (therapeutic) |
| TSH | Normal (no lithium-induced hypothyroidism) |

**Biopsy Data:**

| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | Normal glomeruli |
| IF | Negative |
| EM | Podocyte foot process effacement — MCD pattern |

**Diagnosis:**
Lithium-Induced Minimal Change Disease

**Treatment:**
Switch lithium to valproate 500 mg BID (psychiatry-supervised taper over 4 weeks). Ramipril 5 mg. Continue RAASi.

**Outcome:**
Month 3: UPCR 1.0, eGFR 68. Month 6: UPCR 0.3, eGFR 70. Complete resolution after lithium switch. Valproate tolerated. Illustrates reversibility of lithium-MCD.

**Differential Diagnosis:**

| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| fsgs | 0.2 | Normal LM with effacement only = MCD, not FSGS. Lithium causes both — MCD reversible, FSGS not |

### CASE-DRG-NSA-001: NSAID-Induced Minimal Change Disease

**Type:** typical
**Source:** NSAID-MCD

**History:**
A 55-year-old woman with chronic knee pain on daily ibuprofen 600 mg TID for 18 months presents with acute onset of periorbital and leg edema, foamy urine, and weight gain of 4 kg over 2 weeks. No past kidney disease.

**Examination:**
BP 132/78, 2+ pitting edema legs, periorbital edema.

**Laboratory Data:**

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| UPCR | 6.5 g/g |
| eGFR | 72 mL/min |
| Cr | 0.9 mg/dL |
| Albumin | 1.8 g/dL |
| C3 | Normal |
| C4 | Normal |
| ANA | Negative |
| PLA2R | Negative |
| Hep B/C | Negative |

**Biopsy Data:**

| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | Normal glomeruli, mild interstitial edema |
| IF | Negative |
| EM | Diffuse podocyte foot process effacement, no deposits |

**Diagnosis:**
NSAID-Induced Minimal Change Disease — ibuprofen

**Treatment:**
Discontinue ibuprofen. Ramipril 5 mg. Furosemide 20 mg for edema. No steroids initially. Monitor.

**Outcome:**
Week 4: UPCR 2.0, eGFR 75. Week 8: UPCR 0.5, eGFR 78. Week 12: UPCR 0.1, eGFR 80. Complete resolution. Counsel: avoid all NSAIDs (including OTC).

**Differential Diagnosis:**

| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| mcd | 0.3 | Idiopathic MCD is identical histologically — drug history distinguishes (PLA2R negative, NSAID use) |

### CASE-DRG-PAM-001: Pamidronate-Induced Collapsing FSGS

**Type:** typical
**Source:** Pamidronate collapsing FSGS

**History:**
A 65-year-old man with multiple myeloma (on pamidronate 90 mg IV monthly x24 months) presents with nephrotic syndrome (UPCR 7.0, edema) and rapidly declining eGFR (62 to 28 over 3 months). No HIV risk factors.

**Examination:**
BP 148/86, 2+ edema, cachexia.

**Laboratory Data:**

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| UPCR | 7.0 g/g |
| eGFR | 28 mL/min |
| Cr | 2.4 mg/dL |
| Albumin | 1.6 g/dL |
| HIV | Negative |
| APOL1 | G1/G1 |
| SPEP | IgG kappa M-spike 2.5 |
| C3 | Normal |

**Biopsy Data:**

| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | Collapsing FSGS (50% glomeruli), tubular microcysts, 30% IFTA |
| IF | Negative |
| EM | Podocyte foot process effacement, no TRI (differentiates from HIVAN) |

**Diagnosis:**
Pamidronate-Induced Collapsing FSGS

**Treatment:**
Stop pamidronate. Switch to zoledronate (no renal toxicity). Ramipril 10 mg. Prednisone 1 mg/kg/day (limited evidence). Amlodipine 5 mg.

**Outcome:**
Month 3: UPCR 4.5, eGFR 22 — partial stabilisation. Month 6: UPCR 3.2, eGFR 20. Month 12: ESKD — started HD. Biopsy showed 80% sclerosis. Irreversible despite cessation.

**Differential Diagnosis:**

| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| hivan | 0.1 | No TRI on EM, HIV negative, pamidronate history = drug-induced collapsing FSGS, not HIVAN |
