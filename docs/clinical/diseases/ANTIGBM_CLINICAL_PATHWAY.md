**Domain**: Anti-Glomerular Basement Membrane Antibody Disease  
**Classification**: Clinical Pathway — 6-Stage Urgent Model

---

## 1. Overview

This document defines the complete 6-stage clinical pathway for anti-GBM disease, from initial urgent diagnosis through long-term follow-up, ESKD management, and renal transplantation. The pathway is designed around the imperative for immediate action — anti-GBM disease is a medical emergency where delays in diagnosis and treatment directly determine renal survival. Each stage specifies actions, timelines, entry and exit criteria, decision points, and responsible parties. The pathway integrates serological monitoring, biopsy interpretation, PLEX protocols, cyclophosphamide versus rituximab decision branching, and the exceptional low relapse rate that distinguishes anti-GBM disease from ANCA vasculitis.

## 3. Stage 1: Urgent Diagnosis

**Duration**: 2 days  
**Entry Criterion**: Clinical suspicion of anti-GBM disease (RPGN with or without DAH, pulmonary-renal syndrome)  
**Exit Criterion**: Confirmed anti-GBM diagnosis; transition to Stage 2 initiated same day

### Actions

| # | Action | Timeline | Responsible |
|---|--------|----------|-------------|
| 1.1 | Clinical assessment: AKI history, haemoptysis, smoking, hydrocarbon exposure, drug history | Day 0 (same day) | Nephrologist |
| 1.2 | Stat anti-GBM serology (ELISA/chemiluminescent immunoassay) | Day 0 (4-hour turnaround) | Clinical Immunology |
| 1.3 | Stat ANCA serology (PR3, MPO) to rule out double-positive | Day 0 | Clinical Immunology |
| 1.4 | Urgent renal biopsy (minimum 10 glomeruli; process within 12 hours of presentation) | Day 0-1 | Interventional Radiology/Nephrologist |
| 1.5 | Light microscopy with immediate reading for crescents | Day 0-1 | Renal Pathologist |
| 1.6 | Immunofluorescence (IgG, IgA, IgM, C3, C1q, kappa, lambda) — stat processing | Day 0-1 | Renal Pathologist |
| 1.7 | Chest imaging (CT chest or CXR) for pulmonary haemorrhage assessment | Day 0 | Radiology |
| 1.8 | Baseline labs: Cr, eGFR, CBC, haemoglobin, CRP, ESR, iron studies, DLCO | Day 0 | Clinical Team |

### Decision Points

- **If anti-GBM serology positive + linear IgG on IF**: Confirm diagnosis; initiate same-day treatment
- **If anti-GBM serology positive with negative IF (rare)**: Repeat biopsy; consider false-positive serology
- **If anti-GBM serology negative but linear IgG on IF**: Check for elution studies; retest serology in reference lab
- **If double-positive (anti-GBM + ANCA)**: Treat as anti-GBM AND plan ANCA-level maintenance therapy
- **If no linear IgG but pauci-immune IF**: Consider ANCA vasculitis; enter ANCA pathway
- **Critical rule**: Do NOT delay treatment awaiting full biopsy if serology strongly positive

## 4. Stage 2: Induction — PLEX and Immunosuppression

**Duration**: 14 days  
**Entry Criterion**: Confirmed anti-GBM disease diagnosis  
**Exit Criterion**: Completion of PLEX course; anti-GBM antibody negative or significantly reduced; clinical stabilisation

### Actions

| # | Action | Timeline | Responsible |
|---|--------|----------|-------------|
| 2.1 | Central venous access (dual-lumen apheresis catheter) for PLEX | Day 0-1 | Interventional Radiology |
| 2.2 | PLEX 60 mL/kg daily x 14 days (or until anti-GBM negative); use 5% albumin, FFP if post-PLEX bleeding risk | Day 0-14 | Apheresis Unit |
| 2.3 | Methylprednisolone 500-1000 mg IV daily x 3 days | Day 0-2 | Nephrologist |
| 2.4 | Cyclophosphamide 2-3 mg/kg/day oral (max 200 mg) OR IV 15 mg/kg q3weeks | Day 0+ (start same day) | Nephrologist |
| 2.5 | Prednisone 1 mg/kg/day (max 80 mg) oral after pulse completion | Day 3+ | Nephrologist |
| 2.6 | TMP-SMX 800/160 mg daily or 3x/week for PJP prophylaxis | Day 0+ | Nephrologist |
| 2.7 | Proton pump inhibitor for stress ulcer prophylaxis during high-dose steroids | Day 0+ | Nephrologist |
| 2.8 | Monitor anti-GBM antibody titres every 1-2 days (guide PLEX duration) | Day 0-14 | Clinical Immunology |
| 2.9 | Daily clinical assessment: urine output, haemoptysis, SpO2, fluid balance | Day 0-14 | Nephrology Team |
| 2.10 | Daily labs: Cr, eGFR, CBC, haemoglobin, calcium (PLEX-related hypocalcaemia) | Day 0-14 | Clinical Team |

### Decision Points: PLEX Protocol

- **Standard course**: 14 daily sessions; consider stopping earlier if anti-GBM negative x 2 consecutive days
- **Persistent positivity beyond 14 days**: Continue PLEX until negative (q2-3 days after 14)
- **Severe DAH with mechanical ventilation**: Consider 1.5 plasma volume exchanges; FFP for last exchange
- **PLEX complications**: Hypocalcaemia (calcium gluconate), hypotension (volume support), line infection (catheter care)

### Decision Points: Cyclophosphamide vs Rituximab

| Factor | Favour Cyclophosphamide | Favour Rituximab |
|--------|------------------------|------------------|
| Standard evidence | First-line (cohort studies) | Limited case series |
| Age >70 years | Increased toxicity | Preferred |
| Infection risk | Increases risk | Lower infection risk |
| Future fertility | Gonadotoxic | Preferred |
| Cytopenia at presentation | Avoid | Preferred |
| Malignancy history | Avoid | Preferred |
| Double-positive ANCA | Acceptable | Increasingly used |
| Resource availability | Widely available | Cost may limit access |

## 5. Stage 3: Continued Induction

**Duration**: 90 days  
**Entry Criterion**: Completed PLEX course; anti-GBM antibody reducing or negative  
**Exit Criterion**: Complete course of cyclophosphamide (2-3 months); transition to azathioprine

### Actions

| # | Action | Timeline | Responsible |
|---|--------|----------|-------------|
| 3.1 | Continue cyclophosphamide (oral 2 mg/kg/day or IV 15 mg/kg q3wk x 3-4 total doses) | Day 15-90 | Nephrologist |
| 3.2 | Continue prednisone taper (reduce by 5 mg/week initially, slower after 20 mg/day) | Day 15-90 | Nephrologist |
| 3.3 | Continue TMP-SMX PJP prophylaxis | Day 15-90 | Nephrologist |
| 3.4 | Weekly clinical and laboratory assessment (Cr, eGFR, urinalysis, UPCR, anti-GBM titre, CBC) | Weekly x 12 weeks | Nephrology Team |
| 3.5 | Assess renal recovery trajectory; if dialysis-dependent at presentation, evaluate at 4 weeks | Week 4 | Nephrologist |
| 3.6 | Anti-GBM antibody monitoring: monthly after negative confirmed | Monthly | Clinical Immunology |
| 3.7 | Smoking cessation counselling (critical for pulmonary outcome and relapse prevention) | Day 15+ | Multidisciplinary |
| 3.8 | Vaccination update: influenza, pneumococcal (avoid live vaccines until off immunosuppression) | Day 30-90 | Primary Care/Nephrology |

### Decision Points

- **If anti-GBM antibody titres rise during induction**: Consider repeat PLEX; exclude non-adherence
- **If dialysis-dependent at 4-6 weeks with no renal recovery**: Discuss ESKD pathway; realistic counselling
- **If severe cyclophosphamide toxicity (leucopenia WBC <3000, infection, cystitis)**: Dose reduce or switch to rituximab
- **If azathioprine intolerance**: Consider mycophenolate mofetil 1-2 g/day as alternative
- **Double-positive patients**: Consider continuing induction longer; plan for ANCA-type maintenance

## 6. Stage 4: Remission and Recovery

**Duration**: 180 days  
**Entry Criterion**: Completed cyclophosphamide course; switched to azathioprine; stable or improving renal function  
**Exit Criterion**: Anti-GBM antibody negative x 6 months; stable renal function; steroid taper to <10 mg/day

### Actions

| # | Action | Timeline | Responsible |
|---|--------|----------|-------------|
| 4.1 | Switch from cyclophosphamide to azathioprine 1-2 mg/kg/day (check TPMT before initiation) | Day 90 | Nephrologist |
| 4.2 | Continue prednisone taper (target 5-10 mg/day by month 6; aim to discontinue by 12 months) | Day 90-180 | Nephrologist |
| 4.3 | Continue TMP-SMX prophylaxis (minimum 6 months total) | Day 90-180 | Nephrologist |
| 4.4 | Monthly clinical and laboratory assessment (Cr, eGFR, urinalysis, UPCR, anti-GBM titre) | Monthly x 6 | Nephrology Team |
| 4.5 | Blood pressure management; RAAS blockade if proteinuria present | Monthly | Nephrologist |
| 4.6 | Bone density monitoring if continued steroids (DEXA scan) | Month 6 | Nephrologist |
| 4.7 | Steroid toxicity surveillance: fasting glucose, BP, weight, ophthalmology | Monthly-3 monthly | Nephrologist |
| 4.8 | Psychosocial support and patient education on disease, low relapse risk, signs of recurrence | Month 3-6 | Nephrology Team/Mental Health |

### Decision Points

- **If anti-GBM remains negative and renal function stable**: Continue azathioprine taper plan
- **If anti-GBM becomes positive after initial negative**: Investigate non-adherence; consider brief PLEX course
- **If progressive CKD despite remission**: Plan ESKD preparation (AV fistula, transplant referral)
- **If double-positive patient**: Do NOT withdraw immunosuppression; continue ANCA-type maintenance (rituximab or azathioprine)
- **If complete renal recovery**: Plan immunosuppression withdrawal at 12-18 months

## 7. Stage 5: Long-Term Follow-Up and ESKD

**Duration**: 365 days (ongoing per cycle)  
**Entry Criterion**: Completed 12-18 months of therapy; anti-GBM negative; in clinical remission  
**Exit Criterion**: Anti-GBM negative; off immunosuppression; transition to annual monitoring or ESKD pathway

### Actions

| # | Action | Timeline | Responsible |
|---|--------|----------|-------------|
| 5.1 | Withdraw azathioprine at 12-18 months (anti-GBM disease: <3% relapse) | Month 12-18 | Nephrologist |
| 5.2 | Withdraw prednisone (taper to zero over 4-8 weeks once azathioprine stopped) | Month 12-18 | Nephrologist |
| 5.3 | Continue TMP-SMX for 3 months after immunosuppression withdrawal | Month 12-18 | Nephrologist |
| 5.4 | Clinical and lab assessment q3 months during year 2; then q6 months | q3-6mo | Nephrologist |
| 5.5 | Anti-GBM antibody monitoring every 6 months after therapy withdrawal | q6mo | Clinical Immunology |
| 5.6 | Cardiovascular risk management (ACEi/ARB, statin, BP control, smoking cessation) | Ongoing | Nephrologist/Primary Care |
| 5.7 | If ESKD: dialysis access planning; transplant evaluation when anti-GBM negative x 6-12 months | Month 6-18 | Nephrologist/Transplant Team |
| 5.8 | Cancer screening (age-appropriate) — cyclophosphamide-related bladder cancer surveillance | Annually | Primary Care |

### Decision Points

- **Renal recovery sustained**: Withdraw immunosuppression at 12-18 months
- **ESKD established**: Proceed to dialysis and transplant evaluation
- **Double-positive patients**: Continue long-term immunosuppression (do not withdraw)
- **Relapse (rare)**: Return to Stage 5b

### Stage 5b: Relapse Management

**Duration**: 90 days  
**Entry Criterion**: Recurrent anti-GBM positivity with clinical activity (Cr rise, DAH, or both)  
**Note**: Relapse rate <3%; if relapse occurs, re-institute Stage 2 triple therapy and investigate trigger (smoking, hydrocarbon, infection).

---

## 8. Stage 6: Renal Transplantation

**Duration**: 365 days (per cycle)  
**Entry Criterion**: Irreversible ESKD; anti-GBM antibody negative for minimum 6-12 months  
**Exit Criterion**: Successful transplantation with stable graft function

### Actions

| # | Action | Timeline | Responsible |
|---|--------|----------|-------------|
| 6.1 | Ensure anti-GBM antibody negative for 6-12 months before listing | Pre-transplant | Nephrologist/Transplant Team |
| 6.2 | Complete transplant evaluation: cardiac, infectious, malignancy, urological assessment | Day 30-180 | Transplant Team |
| 6.3 | Standard immunosuppression induction and maintenance (no special regimen required) | Peri-operative | Transplant Surgeon/Nephrologist |
| 6.4 | Anti-GBM antibody monitoring post-transplant: at 1, 3, 6, 12 months, then annually | Post-transplant | Transplant Nephrology |
| 6.5 | Monitor for recurrence: anti-GBM titres, urinalysis, protocol biopsies as per centre | Ongoing | Transplant Nephrology |
| 6.6 | Cardiovascular risk management following transplant (CVD is leading cause of late graft loss) | Ongoing | Transplant Team |
| 6.7 | Smoking cessation reinforcement (critical to prevent pulmonary disease and graft vasculopathy) | Ongoing | Multidisciplinary |
| 6.8 | Patient education on signs of recurrence (haemoptysis, haematuria, oliguria) | Ongoing | Transplant Team |

### Transplant Outcomes

| Parameter | Outcome |
|-----------|---------|
| Patient survival at 5 years post-transplant | >85% |
| Graft survival at 5 years | Comparable to other causes of ESKD |
| Recurrence in graft | <5% (if anti-GBM negative at transplant) |
| Subclinical linear IgG deposition | Can occur without active disease |
| Timing of transplantation | After 6-12 months of negative anti-GBM serology |
