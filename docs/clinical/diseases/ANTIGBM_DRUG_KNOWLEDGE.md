# Anti-GBM Disease (Goodpasture Syndrome) Drug Knowledge

**Document ID**: ANTIGBM_DR  
**Version**: 1.0  
**Date**: 2026-07-10  
**Domain**: Anti-Glomerular Basement Membrane Antibody Disease  
**Classification**: Drug Knowledge — Therapeutic Agents

---

## 1. Overview

Anti-GBM disease therapy is centred on the triple combination of plasma exchange (PLEX), cyclophosphamide, and high-dose corticosteroids. Unlike ANCA-associated vasculitis where rituximab has become first-line, cyclophosphamide remains the immunosuppressant of choice in anti-GBM disease due to its rapid onset of action, extensive use in the published cohort evidence base, and the short duration required (2-3 months). Rituximab serves as an alternative in patients with contraindications to cyclophosphamide. PLEX is the critical and unique component of anti-GBM therapy — it directly removes the pathogenic autoantibodies. This document covers PLEX, cyclophosphamide, corticosteroids, rituximab, azathioprine maintenance, and TMP-SMX prophylaxis.

## 2. Therapeutic Agent Framework

### 2.1 Drug Categories

| Category | Agents | Role in Anti-GBM Disease |
|----------|--------|--------------------------|
| Apheresis (antibody removal) | Plasma exchange (PLEX) | First-line, same-day; core of acute therapy |
| Alkylating agent | Cyclophosphamide | First-line immunosuppression (2-3 months) |
| Biologic (B-cell depleting) | Rituximab | Alternative to CyP (intolerance, elderly, infection) |
| Corticosteroids | Methylprednisolone, Prednisone | Baseline anti-inflammatory therapy |
| Antimetabolite | Azathioprine | Maintenance after CyP course (6-12 months) |
| Antibiotic prophylaxis | TMP-SMX | PJP prophylaxis mandatory during induction |
| Alternative maintenance | Mycophenolate mofetil | Alternative if azathioprine intolerant |

## 3. Plasma Exchange (PLEX)

### 3.1 Procedure Profile

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Mechanism** | Removal of circulating pathogenic anti-GBM IgG antibodies; removal of inflammatory mediators |
| **Evidence level** | Category I indication per ASFA guidelines (strong recommendation, good evidence) |
| **Procedure** | Centrifugal or membrane separation; 1-1.5 plasma volume per session |
| **Replacement fluid** | 5% albumin (first-line); FFP if post-procedure bleeding or invasive procedure planned |
| **Dose** | 60 mL/kg exchanges |
| **Schedule** | Daily x 14 days (standard); can extend if anti-GBM remains positive |
| **Endpoint** | Anti-GBM antibody negative by ELISA on two consecutive measurements |
| **Vascular access** | Temporary dual-lumen dialysis/apheresis catheter (femoral, IJ, or subclavian) |
| **Anticoagulation** | Citrate (regional); heparin (systemic) as alternative |
| **Key evidence** | Lockwood et al. Lancet 1975; Levy et al. JASN 2001; KDIGO 2021 Ch 9 |

### 3.2 Monitoring During PLEX

| Parameter | Frequency | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| Anti-GBM antibody titres | Every 1-2 days | Determine PLEX duration; confirm negativity |
| Serum calcium (ionised) | Pre- and post-PLEX | Citrate chelation causes hypocalcaemia |
| Haemoglobin | Pre- and post-PLEX each session | Monitor haemolysis, bleeding |
| Fibrinogen | Before each session | Albumin depletion causes low fibrinogen |
| Platelet count | Every 2-3 days | Mechanical consumption |
| Creatinine/eGFR | Daily | Renal function trajectory |
| Urine output | Daily | Renal recovery indicator |

### 3.3 Complications

| Complication | Frequency | Management |
|-------------|-----------|------------|
| Hypocalcaemia (citrate effect) | 5-15% | IV calcium gluconate 10-20 mL; reduce flow rate |
| Hypotension | 3-5% | Volume expansion; rate reduction |
| Allergic reaction (to FFP) | 1-3% | Use albumin; premedicate |
| Catheter-related bloodstream infection | 2-5% | Strict sterile technique; early removal |
| Hypogammaglobulinaemia | Transient (expected) | IVIG if severe infection develops |
| Bleeding (low fibrinogen) | 5-10% | FFP replacement if fibrinogen <100 mg/dL or planned procedure |
| Line thrombosis | 2-4% | Heparin lock; thrombolytic if occluded |

## 4. Cyclophosphamide

### 4.1 Drug Profile

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Drug class** | Alkylating agent (nitrogen mustard) |
| **Mechanism** | Cross-links DNA; inhibits rapidly dividing T and B lymphocytes; suppresses autoantibody production |
| **Indication in anti-GBM** | First-line immunosuppression for all patients (alongside PLEX and steroids) |
| **Oral dose** | 2-3 mg/kg/day (max 200 mg) — adjust to target WBC 3000-5000/uL |
| **IV dose** | 15 mg/kg (max 1.2 g) q3-4 weeks x 3-4 doses |
| **Route preference** | Oral traditional; IV increasingly used (lower cumulative dose) |
| **Duration** | 2-3 months (short course, unlike ANCA) |
| **Cumulative limit** | Target <15 g lifetime; typically <10 g for anti-GBM course |
| **Key evidence** | Levy et al. JASN 2001; Johnson et al. Medicine 1985; KDIGO 2021 |

### 4.2 Administration and Monitoring

| Parameter | Frequency | Target |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| WBC count | Every 2-3 days initially, then weekly | Target WBC 3000-5000/uL |
| Absolute neutrophil count | With each CBC | >1500/uL |
| Platelet count | Weekly | >100,000/uL |
| Urinalysis for haematuria | Weekly | Detect haemorrhagic cystitis early |
| LFTs | Weekly | Hepatotoxicity monitoring |
| Hydration | 2-3 L/day oral/IV | Prevent cystitis |
| Mesna | With each dose (20% of CyP dose x 3 at 0, 4, 8 hours) | For IV CyP only |

### 4.3 Toxicity and Management

| Toxicity | Frequency | Prevention/Management |
|----------|-----------|----------------------|
| Leucopenia (WBC <3000) | 30-50% | Dose reduce or hold until WBC recovers; G-CSF if severe |
| Haemorrhagic cystitis | 5-15% (oral), <5% (IV) | Mesna + aggressive hydration for IV; limit cumulative dose |
| Nausea and vomiting | 40-60% | Antiemetics (ondansetron, арreptitant) |
| Infection (severe) | 20-30% | TMP-SMX prophylaxis; G-CSF; IVIG |
| Ovarian failure | 10-50% (age-dependent) | GnRH agonist co-treatment; limit to 2-3 months |
| Azoospermia | 30-60% | Sperm banking before therapy |
| Malignancy (bladder, leukaemia, skin) | 2-5% (cumulative) | Short course (2-3 months) minimises risk; bladder cancer screening |
| Pneumonitis | <1% | Discontinue; steroids |

## 5. Corticosteroids

### 5.1 Methylprednisolone (IV Pulse)

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Indication** | All patients at diagnosis; rapid anti-inflammatory effect |
| **Dose** | 500-1000 mg IV daily x 3 consecutive days |
| **Rationale** | Suppresses neutrophil recruitment and complement-mediated injury; reduces inflammation at sites of antibody binding |
| **Timing** | Start same day as diagnosis; can be given before PLEX |

### 5.2 Prednisone (Oral)

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Starting dose** | 1 mg/kg/day (max 60-80 mg) after pulse completion |
| **Taper** | Reduce by 5 mg/week until 20 mg/day; then 2.5 mg/week until 10 mg; then 1 mg/month |
| **Duration** | 6-12 months total; may discontinue when anti-GBM negative and stable |
| **Total course** | Shorter than ANCA vasculitis (stop by 12-18 months given <3% relapse) |

### 5.3 Steroid-Related Monitoring

| Parameter | Frequency |
|-----------|-----------|
| Fasting glucose | Weekly x 4, then monthly |
| Blood pressure | Each visit |
| Bone mineral density (DEXA) | Baseline, annually |
| Ophthalmologic exam (cataract, glaucoma) | Baseline, annually |
| Weight, BMI | Each visit |
| Infection surveillance | Each visit |

## 6. Rituximab (Alternative to Cyclophosphamide)

### 6.1 Drug Profile

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Drug class** | Chimeric monoclonal anti-CD20 antibody |
| **Mechanism** | B-cell depletion; reduces plasma cell precursors for autoantibody production |
| **Indication** | Alternative to cyclophosphamide (contraindication, intolerance, elderly, infection risk) |
| **Dose** | 375 mg/m2 IV weekly x 4 doses (RAVE protocol) OR 1000 mg IV day 1 and 15 |
| **Evidence** | Case series only (no RCT in anti-GBM); expert opinion level |
| **Pre-medication** | Acetaminophen 1000 mg, diphenhydramine 25-50 mg, methylprednisolone 100 mg IV |
| **Duration of effect** | B-cell depletion lasts 6-12 months |

### 6.2 Evidence Limitations

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **RCT data** | None in anti-GBM disease (disease too rare) |
| **Case series** | Small series show comparable outcomes to CyP |
| **Onset of action** | Slower than CyP (B-cell depletion takes 2-4 weeks) |
| **PLEX interaction** | PLEX removes rituximab; administer after PLEX session |
| **Preferred setting** | Elderly, high infection risk, cytopenia, prior malignancy |
| **Double-positive disease** | Increasingly used (ANCA component benefits from rituximab) |

### 6.3 Monitoring

| Parameter | Frequency | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| CD19+ B-cell count | Pre-dose and q3mo | Measure depletion |
| Immunoglobulins (IgG, IgA, IgM) | Baseline and q3mo | Hypogammaglobulinaemia risk |
| HBsAg, HBcAb, HCV | Baseline | HBV reactivation risk |
| ANCA if double-positive | At each visit | Relapse prediction |

### 6.4 Adverse Effects

| Effect | Frequency | Management |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| Infusion reaction | 5-10% | Slow infusion; premedication |
| Hypogammaglobulinaemia | 5-20% | IVIG if infections recurrent |
| Late neutropenia | 3-5% | Self-limiting; monitor CBC |
| HBV reactivation | 1-5% (if positive) | Antiviral prophylaxis |
| PML (progressive multifocal leukoencephalopathy) | Extremely rare | Discontinue if neurological symptoms |

## 7. Azathioprine (Maintenance)

### 7.1 Drug Profile

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Drug class** | Purine antimetabolite |
| **Mechanism** | Inhibits DNA synthesis in rapidly dividing T and B cells |
| **Indication** | Maintenance therapy after cyclophosphamide completion (6-12 months) |
| **Dose** | 1-2 mg/kg/day oral (start at 50 mg; titrate up over 2 weeks) |
| **Duration** | 6-12 months (until immunosuppression withdrawn) |
| **Key evidence** | Extrapolated from ANCA vasculitis; expert consensus |

### 7.2 TPMT Screening

| TPMT Activity | Population | Action |
|---------------|------------|--------|
| Normal | 90% | Full dose (2 mg/kg/day) |
| Intermediate | 10% | Start 50% dose; monitor closely |
| Deficient | 0.3% | Avoid azathioprine (use MMF or continue observation) |

### 7.3 Monitoring

| Parameter | Frequency | Threshold |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| CBC with differential | q2 weeks x 8 weeks, then monthly | Hold if WBC <3000 |
| LFTs | q2 weeks x 8 weeks, then monthly | Hold if ALT >3x ULN |
| Drug interaction | Strict avoidance of allopurinol/febuxostat | Fatal myelosuppression |

## 8. Trimethoprim-Sulfamethoxazole (TMP-SMX)

### 8.1 Prophylaxis Profile

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Indication** | Pneumocystis jirovecii pneumonia prophylaxis during induction and immunosuppression |
| **Duration** | Minimum 6 months; continue throughout immunosuppressive therapy |
| **Dose** | 800/160 mg (double-strength) daily OR 3 times weekly |
| **Alternatives** | Dapsone 100 mg daily (G6PD screen); atovaquone 1500 mg daily |
| **Monitoring** | CBC, LFTs, creatinine at baseline and monthly |
| **Key note** | Mandatory; PJP mortality in immunosuppressed patients is 30-50% |

## 9. Drug Interaction Summary

| Drug | Interacts With | Effect |
|------|---------------|--------|
| Cyclophosphamide | Allopurinol | Increased myelotoxicity |
| Cyclophosphamide | Warfarin | Decreased INR |
| Rituximab | Live vaccines | Contraindicated |
| Rituximab | PLEX | PLEX removes rituximab (dose after PLEX) |
| Azathioprine | Allopurinol, febuxostat | Fatal myelosuppression; absolute contraindication |
| Azathioprine | Warfarin | Decreased INR |
| TMP-SMX | Warfarin | Increased INR |
| TMP-SMX | Methotrexate | Increased MTX toxicity |
| Prednisone | NSAIDs | Increased GI bleeding risk |
| Prednisone | Hypoglycaemics | Increased glucose; adjust diabetes medications |

## 10. Vaccination Recommendations

| Vaccine | Timing | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Influenza (inactivated) | Annually | Safe during immunosuppression |
| Pneumococcal (PCV20/21) | Before therapy or when stable | If not given pre-treatment |
| COVID-19 (mRNA) | Per schedule | Safe during immunosuppression |
| HZV (Shingrix) | Before therapy or when stable | Non-live recombinant; safe |
| Live vaccines (MMR, VZV, yellow fever) | Contraindicated | Avoid during immunosuppression; delay until 6 months after therapy |
| Hepatitis B | Before therapy if possible | Double dose may be needed |

## 11. Treatment Summary by Phase

| Phase | Duration | Regimen |
|-------|----------|---------|
| Acute induction (PLEX) | 14 days | PLEX daily + methylprednisolone IV x3 + CyP + prednisone |
| Continued induction | 76 days | CyP + prednisone taper + TMP-SMX |
| Early maintenance | 6 months | Azathioprine + prednisone taper + TMP-SMX |
| Late maintenance | 6-12 months | Azathioprine (wean at 12-18 mo) |
| Post-therapy | Indefinite | Off all immunosuppression; annual monitoring (<3% relapse) |
