### CASE-CNI-CNV-001: CNI Toxicity + Concurrent Subclinical ABMR
**Type:** atypical
**Source:** Mixed CNI + ABMR
**History:**
A 52-year-old man 6 years post-transplant. Cr 1.8 (slowly rising from 1.4 over 2 years). UPCR 0.8. Biopsy shows CNI toxicity changes BUT also mild microvascular inflammation. DSA positive.
**Examination:**
BP 142/84, 1+ oedema, graft non-tender.
**Laboratory Data:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 38 mL/min |
| Cr | 1.8 mg/dL |
| UPCR | 0.8 |
| Tacrolimus trough | 5.5 |
| DSA | De novo anti-DQ MFI 1800 |
| C4d | Focal positive (C4d1) |
**Biopsy Data:**
| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | Arteriolar hyalinosis (35%), striped fibrosis (IFTA 25%), microcalcification AND g1, ptc1 |
| IF | C4d1 (focal PTC) |
| EM | GBM double contours (cg1) — early transplant glomerulopathy, plus podocyte injury |
**Diagnosis:**
Mixed: Chronic CNI Toxicity (Grade 2) + Chronic Active ABMR (Banff Category 2)
**Treatment:**
Dual management: (1) CNI toxicity: reduce tacrolimus to 4-6 (but ABMR needs adequate IS). (2) ABMR: IVIG 2 g/kg monthly x3. Rituximab 375 mg/m2 x1. Continue belatacept consideration.
**Outcome:**
Month 6: Cr 1.7, DSA MFI 600. UPCR 0.5. Illustrates common scenario: mixed pathology. Both CNI toxicity AND ABMR contribute to late graft dysfunction. Biopsy essential to identify both.
**Differential Diagnosis:**
| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| pure CNI toxicity | 0.3 | Presence of g>0, ptc>0, C4d+, DSA+ indicates concurrent ABMR despite CNI toxicity features. Treating only CNI toxicity would miss ABMR — biopsy essential. |

### CASE-CNI-TMA-001: CNI-Associated Thrombotic Microangiopathy
**Type:** atypical
**Source:** CNI TMA
**History:**
A 38-year-old woman 2 weeks post-transplant on tacrolimus. Presents with AKI (Cr 1.0 to 4.2), microangiopathic haemolytic anaemia (Hb 7.0, schistocytes, LDH 1200, platelet count 45K). Tacrolimus level 18.2.
**Examination:**
BP 168/100, petechiae, graft tender, oliguria.
**Laboratory Data:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 14 mL/min |
| Cr | 4.2 mg/dL |
| Hb | 7.0 (drop from 12) |
| Platelets | 45,000 |
| Schistocytes | Present on smear |
| LDH | 1200 U/L |
| Haptoglobin | Low |
| Tacrolimus trough | 18.2 ng/mL |
| DSA | Negative |
**Biopsy Data:**
| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | Glomerular capillary thrombi, arteriolar fibrin thrombi, endothelial swelling, mesangiolysis |
| IF | Fibrinogen+ in thrombi, C4d0 |
| EM | Endothelial injury, subendothelial widening, platelet aggregates |
**Diagnosis:**
CNI-Associated Thrombotic Microangiopathy (TMA) — tacrolimus-induced
**Treatment:**
STOP tacrolimus. Switch to belatacept. Plasma exchange 1.5 volume x5 sessions. Supportive: BP control, dialysis if needed.
**Outcome:**
After 5 PLEX sessions: platelets 120K, LDH 400, schistocytes resolved. Cr 2.0 (partial recovery). Belatacept continued. Illustrates rare but life-threatening CNI complication — requires immediate CNI cessation.
**Differential Diagnosis:**
| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| recurrent HUS | 0.2 | High tacrolimus level + immediate post-transplant timing + no prior HUS history = CNI-induced TMA. Distinguishing from recurrent aHUS requires genetic testing and complement studies. |

### CASE-CNI-ADV-001: Advanced CNI Toxicity with Irreversible Changes
**Type:** complications
**Source:** Advanced CNI toxicity
**History:**
A 60-year-old man 12 years post-transplant on long-term tacrolimus. Cr slowly rising 1.5 to 3.8 over 5 years. Proteinuria UPCR 1.2. eGFR 18. Evaluation shows advanced CNI toxicity.
**Examination:**
BP 158/92, 2+ oedema, fatigue, pallor.
**Laboratory Data:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 18 mL/min |
| Cr | 3.8 mg/dL |
| UPCR | 1.2 g/g |
| Tacrolimus trough | 5.0 (therapeutic) |
| DSA | Negative |
| Haemoglobin | 9.5 (anaemia of CKD) |
**Biopsy Data:**
| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | Severe arteriolar hyalinosis (>70% of arterioles), extensive striped fibrosis (IFTA 65%), global glomerulosclerosis 40%, tubular atrophy |
| IF | C4d0 |
| EM | Advanced foot process effacement, GBM thickening, no deposits |
**Diagnosis:**
Advanced Chronic CNI Toxicity (Grade 3) — irreversible
**Treatment:**
Supportive: CNI minimisation (tac target 3-5, but limited benefit). RAASi. BP control. CKD management: anaemia (darbepoetin), MBD, nutrition. Prepare for dialysis. Not a candidate for belatacept (eGFR <20).
**Outcome:**
Month 6: Cr 4.2, eGFR 14. Started HD. Re-transplant evaluation: belatacept-based IS for next graft. Illustrates consequence of late diagnosis and irreversible injury.
**Differential Diagnosis:**
| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| antibodyMediatedRejection | 0.1 | Long-standing CNI + hyalinosis/striped fibrosis + C4d0/DSA- + no microvascular inflammation = CNI toxicity, not ABMR. Both may co-exist — always exclude ABMR. |

### CASE-CNI-NOD-001: New-Onset Diabetes After CNI (NODAT)
**Type:** complications
**Source:** NODAT
**History:**
A 45-year-old woman 6 months post-transplant on tacrolimus. No prior diabetes. Now presents with polyuria, polydipsia, and random glucose 280. HbA1c 8.5%.
**Examination:**
BP 138/84, BMI 28, no oedema, graft non-tender.
**Laboratory Data:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 52 mL/min |
| Cr | 1.2 mg/dL |
| UPCR | 0.1 |
| Glucose | 280 mg/dL |
| HbA1c | 8.5% |
| Tacrolimus trough | 8.5 ng/mL |
| DSA | Negative |
**Biopsy Data:**
| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | No significant CNI toxicity changes (minimal hyalinosis, IFTA 5%) |
| IF | C4d0 |
**Diagnosis:**
New-Onset Diabetes After Transplant (NODAT) — tacrolimus-induced
**Treatment:**
Start insulin/oral hypoglycaemics. Consider switch from tacrolimus to cyclosporine (lower diabetogenic potential) if poor glycaemic control. Maintain other IS.
**Outcome:**
HbA1c improved to 7.0% with insulin + metformin. Tacrolimus maintained at lower trough (6.5). Illustrates metabolic complication of CNI therapy separate from nephrotoxicity.
**Differential Diagnosis:**
| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| type 2 diabetes | 0.2 | Temporal association with tacrolimus initiation and dose-dependent effect = NODAT. Tacrolimus impairs insulin secretion directly. Consider cyclosporine switch if refractory. |

### CASE-CNI-MTOR-001: mTOR Inhibitor Conversion with Proteinuria
**Type:** complications
**Source:** mTOR proteinuria
**History:**
A 48-year-old woman 3 years post-transplant with biopsy-proven CNI toxicity. Converted from tacrolimus to sirolimus 6 months ago. Now presents with new proteinuria (UPCR 1.5). GFR stable.
**Examination:**
BP 136/82, 1+ oedema, graft non-tender.
**Laboratory Data:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 42 mL/min |
| Cr | 1.5 mg/dL |
| UPCR | 1.5 g/g (increased from 0.3) |
| Sirolimus level | 6.5 ng/mL |
| DSA | Negative |
| Albumin | 3.4 g/dL |
**Biopsy Data:**
| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | Arteriolar hyalinosis (unchanged), IFTA 25%, no new changes. No rejection. Podocyte injury on EM |
| IF | C4d0 |
| EM | Diffuse foot process effacement (new compared to prior biopsy). No deposits. Consistent with mTOR-induced podocyte injury |
**Diagnosis:**
mTOR Inhibitor-Associated Proteinuria after CNI Toxicity Conversion
**Treatment:**
Add ramipril 10 mg. Target sirolimus 4-6 ng/mL (reduce dose). Monitor UPCR monthly. If proteinuria >2 or GFR declines: consider stopping sirolimus and switching to belatacept.
**Outcome:**
Month 3: UPCR 0.9 (improved with RAASi). Month 6: UPCR 0.6. GFR stable. Illustrates mTOR inhibitor can worsen proteinuria — monitor closely.
**Differential Diagnosis:**
| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| recurrentGN | 0.2 | No immune deposits on IF/EM, temporal association with mTOR conversion = mTOR-induced proteinuria, not recurrent GN. Reducing sirolimus + RAASi helps. |

### CASE-CNI-ACU-001: Acute CNI Toxicity from High Tacrolimus Level
**Type:** typical
**Source:** Acute CNI toxicity
**History:**
A 45-year-old man 3 months post-transplant. Tacrolimus dose increased after a mild rejection episode (trough now 14.2). Cr rising from 1.0 to 1.6 over 1 week. No graft tenderness. No fever.
**Examination:**
BP 152/90, graft non-tender, no oedema.
**Laboratory Data:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 40 mL/min |
| Cr | 1.6 mg/dL |
| Tacrolimus trough | 14.2 ng/mL (high) |
| DSA | Negative |
| UPCR | 0.2 |
| BK PCR | Negative |
**Biopsy Data:**
| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | No arteriolar hyalinosis. No tubulitis (t0). No interstitial inflammation. Normal glomeruli |
| IF | C4d0 |
| EM | Normal — no GBM changes, no deposits |
**Diagnosis:**
Acute CNI Toxicity — elevated tacrolimus level, no structural injury
**Treatment:**
Reduce tacrolimus dose: target 5-8 ng/mL. Recheck Cr in 1 week.
**Outcome:**
Week 1: Cr 1.2. Week 2: Cr 1.0. Tacrolimus 7.2. Complete resolution. Illustrates: acute CNI toxicity is haemodynamic and reversible with dose reduction.
**Differential Diagnosis:**
| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| tCellMediatedRejection | 0.1 | No tubulitis, no interstitial inflammation, no C4d, high tac level with temporal Cr rise = acute CNI toxicity. Reversible. TCMR would show tubulitis. |

### CASE-CNI-BEL-001: Belatacept Conversion for CNI Toxicity
**Type:** typical
**Source:** Belatacept conversion
**History:**
A 55-year-old man 4 years post-transplant with biopsy-proven chronic CNI toxicity (moderate arteriolar hyalinosis, IFTA 30%). Cr slowly rising from 1.2 to 2.0 over 3 years. EBV seropositive.
**Examination:**
BP 148/88, 1+ oedema, graft non-tender.
**Laboratory Data:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 34 mL/min |
| Cr | 2.0 mg/dL |
| UPCR | 0.6 g/g |
| Tacrolimus trough | 6.0 |
| DSA | Negative |
| EBV | IgG+ (seropositive) |
**Biopsy Data:**
| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | Arteriolar hyalinosis (40% of arterioles), striped fibrosis (IFTA 30%), microcalcification |
| IF | C4d0 |
| EM | GBM wrinkling, foot process effacement, no deposits |
**Diagnosis:**
Chronic CNI Toxicity (Grade 2) — candidate for belatacept conversion
**Treatment:**
Convert tacrolimus to belatacept: 5 mg/kg IV Days 1, 15, 29, then Q4 weeks. Continue MMF 1.5 g/day. Prednisone 5 mg. Ramipril 10 mg.
**Outcome:**
Month 6: eGFR 38 (improved from 34). Month 12: eGFR 40. UPCR 0.3. DSA negative. Illustrates belatacept conversion benefit: GFR stabilisation/improvement in chronic CNI toxicity.
**Differential Diagnosis:**
| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| antibodyMediatedRejection | 0.2 | C4d0, DSA negative, no microvascular inflammation, hyalinosis and striped fibrosis = CNI toxicity, not ABMR. Biopsy essential to distinguish. |

### CASE-CNI-CHR-001: Chronic CNI Toxicity on Protocol Biopsy
**Type:** typical
**Source:** Chronic CNI toxicity
**History:**
A 50-year-old woman 5 years post-transplant on tacrolimus/MMF/prednisone. Stable Cr 1.4 (eGFR 45). Protocol biopsy reveals characteristic changes. No Cr rise.
**Examination:**
BP 144/86, no oedema, graft non-tender.
**Laboratory Data:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 45 mL/min |
| Cr | 1.4 mg/dL |
| UPCR | 0.4 g/g |
| Tacrolimus trough | 6.5 (therapeutic) |
| DSA | Negative |
| BK PCR | Negative |
**Biopsy Data:**
| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | Mild arteriolar hyalinosis (25% of arterioles), mild striped fibrosis (IFTA 15%), occasional tubular microcalcification. No inflammation. No tubulitis |
| IF | C4d0 |
| EM | Focal foot process effacement, mild GBM wrinkling, no deposits |
**Diagnosis:**
Chronic CNI Toxicity (Grade 1) — subclinical on protocol biopsy
**Treatment:**
Minimise CNI: reduce tacrolimus target 4-6 ng/mL. Continue MMF 1.5 g/day. Start ramipril 5 mg (proteinuria + renoprotection).
**Outcome:**
Month 12: repeat protocol biopsy — stable hyalinosis, no progression. Cr 1.3. UPCR 0.2. Illustrates benefit of early detection and CNI minimisation before irreversible changes.
**Differential Diagnosis:**
| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| hypertensive nephrosclerosis | 0.2 | Arteriolar hyalinosis + striped fibrosis + microcalcification + C4d0 + DSA- = CNI toxicity. Hypertension alone causes diffuse hyalinosis, not nodular. CNI-associated is more nodular. |
