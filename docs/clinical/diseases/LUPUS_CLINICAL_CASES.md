### Case 1: Class IV-G Diffuse Proliferative LN (Typical)

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Case ID** | CASE-LN-TYP-001 |
| **Presentation Type** | `typical` |
| **Gold Standard** | Yes |
| **Source** | Seed file `seed_clinical_cases.py` |

**Summary:**

- 28-year-old African American female with known SLE (6-year history, anti-dsDNA positive, low C3/C4)
- Presented with worsening edema, foamy urine, fatigue over 3 weeks
- BP 148/92, UPCR 4.8g/g, serum creatinine 1.6 mg/dL (baseline 0.9), albumin 2.4 g/dL, eGFR 52
- Anti-dsDNA 420 IU/mL (elevated), C3 45 mg/dL (low), C4 8 mg/dL (low)
- Urinalysis: RBC casts, dysmorphic RBCs, WBCs, granular casts
- Renal biopsy: ISN/RPS Class IV-G (diffuse global proliferative), AI 14, CI 3
- IF: full-house pattern (IgG 3+, IgA 2+, IgM 2+, C3 3+, C1q 3+)
- EM: large subendothelial electron-dense deposits, wire loop lesions, tubuloreticular inclusions
- Treatment: pulse methylprednisolone 500mg IV x3 days, then MMF 2g/day + prednisone 0.5mg/kg/day taper + HCQ 400mg/day
- 6-month outcome: UPCR 0.4g/g, creatinine 1.0, anti-dsDNA 80 IU/mL, C3/C4 normalized (complete response)

**Key Teaching Points:**

- Class IV-G is the most common and most severe LN class
- Full-house IF pattern is the hallmark of LN
- Wire loop lesions on LM represent massive subendothelial deposits
- African American patients have 2-3x higher ESKD risk; early aggressive therapy essential
- MMF + steroids is first-line induction per KDIGO 2021; CyP reserved for severe/refractory

### Case 2: Class V Membranous LN (Nephrotic)

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Case ID** | CASE-LN-TYP-002 |
| **Presentation Type** | `typical` |
| **Gold Standard** | Yes |

**Summary:**

- 35-year-old Hispanic female with SLE (3-year history) presenting with nephrotic syndrome
- UPCR 8.2g/g, albumin 1.8 g/dL, eGFR 78, creatinine 0.9
- Anti-dsDNA 280 IU/mL, C3 62, C4 12 (low)
- Biopsy: ISN/RPS Class V (pure membranous), AI 2, CI 1
- IF: full-house with IgG dominant granular capillary wall staining
- EM: subepithelial electron-dense deposits, foot process effacement
- Anti-PLA2R negative (confirming LN-related membranous, not primary MN)
- Treatment: voclosporin 23.7mg BID + MMF 1g BID + low-dose prednisone (20mg tapering to 5mg) + HCQ 300mg/day
- 12-month outcome: UPCR 1.2g/g, albumin 3.2 g/dL (partial response)

**Key Teaching Points:**

- Class V presents as nephrotic syndrome; must distinguish from primary MN (PLA2R-negative in LN)
- Anti-PLA2R testing helps differentiate primary MN from LN-associated membranous
- Voclosporin + MMF + low-dose steroids achieved superior CRR in AURORA trials
- Full-house IF pattern distinguishes LN membranous from primary MN (IgG4-dominant)
- Class V may have slower response kinetics; extend assessment to 12 months

### Case 3: Crescentic RPGN (Rapid)

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Case ID** | CASE-LN-RAP-003 |
| **Presentation Type** | `rapid` |
| **Gold Standard** | Yes |

**Summary:**

- 22-year-old Asian female with new-onset SLE (diagnosed 6 months ago)
- Presented with rapidly declining renal function over 10 days (oliguria, edema, malaise)
- BP 162/100, creatinine 4.2 mg/dL (baseline 0.7), UPCR 6.5, eGFR 15
- Anti-dsDNA 680 IU/mL, C3 32, C4 4 (markedly low)
- Urinalysis: heavy RBC casts, active sediment
- Emergent biopsy: ISN/RPS Class IV-S with >60% cellular crescents, AI 20, CI 2
- IF: full-house with strong C1q, fibrinogen in crescents
- Treatment: pulse methylprednisolone 1g IV x3 days, then high-dose IV CyP (NIH protocol 0.75g/m2 monthly) + oral prednisone 1mg/kg/day + HCQ + PCP prophylaxis
- 6-month outcome: creatinine 1.8 (improved from 4.2), UPCR 2.1, partial response
- Transitioned to MMF maintenance + HCQ

**Key Teaching Points:**

- RPGN in LN = medical emergency; crescents on biopsy indicate severe active disease
- High AI (>12) indicates very high activity requiring aggressive induction
- NIH-dose CyP (not EuroLupus) preferred for severe presentations
- >50% crescents = poor prognosis even with treatment; early biopsy and treatment essential
- Pulse steroids before oral taper for rapid inflammation control
- PCP prophylaxis mandatory with high-dose immunosuppression

### Case 4: Relapsing LN After Maintenance Therapy (Relapse)

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Case ID** | CASE-LN-REL-004 |
| **Presentation Type** | `relapse` |
| **Gold Standard** | Yes |

**Summary:**

- 31-year-old African American female with Class IV LN, initially achieved complete response on MMF + HCQ
- Maintained on MMF 1.5g/day + HCQ for 18 months
- Presented with rising proteinuria (UPCR from 0.3 to 2.8) over 2 months
- Anti-dsDNA rising from 40 to 320 IU/mL; C3 dropping from 95 to 52
- Creatinine stable at 0.9; no active urine sediment
- Repeat biopsy: Class IV (focal activity), AI 8, CI 4 (increased from baseline CI 2)
- Decision: re-induction with CyP 500mg IV q2wk x6 (EuroLupus) + steroids, then switch to AZA maintenance
- 6-month outcome: UPCR 0.5g/g, anti-dsDNA 60, C3 normalized

**Key Teaching Points:**

- 30-40% of LN patients relapse within 5 years
- Rising anti-dsDNA with falling C3/C4 precedes clinical relapse by 3-6 months
- African American race is a major relapse risk factor
- Re-biopsy important to assess new activity vs chronic damage (CI increased from 2 to 4)
- Switch immunosuppressive class on relapse (MMF -> CyP -> AZA)
- Adherence assessment is critical; non-adherence is a major cause of apparent "relapse"

### Case 5: Refractory LN After Belimumab (Refractory)

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Case ID** | CASE-LN-RES-005 |
| **Presentation Type** | `resistant` |
| **Gold Standard** | Yes |

**Summary:**

- 38-year-old African American female with Class IV-G LN, failed MMF induction and CyP re-induction
- Added belimumab 10mg/kg IV q4 weeks to MMF + low-dose steroids
- After 12 months on belimumab + MMF: UPCR remained 3.5g/g, anti-dsDNA 280, C3 48
- eGFR declining from 55 to 42 over 18 months
- Repeat biopsy: Class IV with significant chronicity (AI 6, CI 8)
- Started rituximab 375mg/m2 weekly x4 (off-label)
- 6 months post-rituximab: UPCR 1.8, eGFR stable at 40, anti-dsDNA 90, C3 72
- Continued on rituximab maintenance (1g x2 q6 months)

**Key Teaching Points:**

- Refractory LN defined as failure of >=2 lines of immunosuppression
- Belimumab add-on is evidence-based for active LN (BLISS-LN trial)
- Repeat biopsy with CI assessment is essential: CI 8 indicates limited reversibility
- Rituximab (off-label) is rescue therapy for refractory LN
- Persistent anti-dsDNA elevation despite treatment indicates ongoing disease activity
- ESKD preparation should be discussed when eGFR declining despite therapy

### Case 6: Childhood-Onset LN (Special/Pediatric)

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Case ID** | CASE-LN-PED-006 |
| **Presentation Type** | `special` |
| **Gold Standard** | Yes |

**Summary:**

- 14-year-old African American female, new-onset SLE with Class IV-S LN
- Presented with facial edema, hypertension (145/95), hematuria, and rash
- UPCR 5.2g/g, creatinine 0.8 (age-appropriate), albumin 2.1, eGFR 110 (pediatric formula)
- Anti-dsDNA 520, C3 38, C4 6
- Biopsy: Class IV-S (segmental proliferative), AI 12, CI 1
- Treatment: pulse methylprednisolone 30mg/kg x3 days, then MMF weight-based (40mg/kg/day) + prednisone taper + HCQ 200mg/day
- Parental counseling: teratogenicity of MMF, importance of adherence, long-term monitoring
- 12-month outcome: UPCR 0.3g/g, complete response; maintained on MMF + HCQ

**Key Teaching Points:**

- 50-70% of childhood-onset SLE develops LN; higher proportion than adult-onset
- Weight-based dosing required for all immunosuppressants in pediatric patients
- Class IV-S (segmental) may have slightly better prognosis than IV-G (global)
- Adherence is a major challenge in adolescents; involve family in management
- HCQ is lifelong; ophthalmologic screening starts at age 8 or after 5 years of use
- Transition planning to adult nephrology essential

### Case 7: LN in Pregnancy (Pregnancy)

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Case ID** | CASE-LN-PRE-007 |
| **Presentation Type** | `pregnancy` |
| **Gold Standard** | Yes |

**Summary:**

- 29-year-old Caucasian female with Class III LN in remission (UPCR 0.2, anti-dsDNA 40) on MMF + HCQ
- Conceived while on MMF; immediately switched to AZA 100mg/day + continued HCQ 300mg/day
- Pregnancy complicated by mild proteinuria (UPCR 0.8) at 28 weeks; BP stable
- Anti-dsDNA rising to 180 at 32 weeks; C3 65 (mildly low)
- Managed with close monitoring q2 weeks; no additional immunosuppression needed
- Delivered healthy baby at 37 weeks via elective C-section (APLS positive, not anticoagulated)
- Postpartum: flare monitoring q2 weeks; restarted AZA + HCQ; transitioned to MMF 6 weeks postpartum

**Key Teaching Points:**

- MMF is teratogenic (D); must switch to AZA minimum 6 weeks before conception
- HCQ is continued throughout pregnancy (reduces flares, improves outcomes)
- Low-dose prednisone acceptable in pregnancy; avoid pulse steroids if possible
- LN flares are common in pregnancy (30-50%); close monitoring essential
- APL antibody positivity affects pregnancy management (fetal loss risk)
- Postpartum is high-risk for flares; intensify monitoring
- Pre-conception planning is essential for LN patients of childbearing age

### Case 8: ESKD Transition (ESKD)

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Case ID** | CASE-LN-ESK-008 |
| **Presentation Type** | `eskd` |
| **Gold Standard** | Yes |

**Summary:**

- 45-year-old African American male with 15-year history of Class IV LN
- Progressed to ESKD despite multiple lines of therapy (MMF, CyP, rituximab)
- Currently on hemodialysis (3x/week), eGFR 8
- APL antibody positive (on warfarin for previous DVT)
- Remaining on HCQ + low-dose AZA for SLE control
- Evaluating for renal transplant: HLA sensitized (panel reactive antibodies 85%)
- Plan: desensitization protocol, transplant with rituximab induction, post-transplant MMF + tacrolimus + prednisone
- Ongoing monitoring: SLE activity, APL management, cardiovascular risk

**Key Teaching Points:**

- 10-20% of LN patients progress to ESKD despite modern therapy
- African American patients have highest ESKD risk in LN
- SLE may "burn out" after ESKD but still requires monitoring and treatment
- APL positivity complicates transplant management (perioperative anticoagulation)
- Transplant in LN: outcomes comparable to non-LN ESKD; recurrence rate 10-30%
- HLA sensitization common in LN due to prior immunosuppression and transfusions
- Post-transplant protocol: induction (basiliximab or thymoglobulin), maintenance (tacrolimus + MMF + steroids)

### Case 9: Class III Focal Proliferative with APLS (Mixed)

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Case ID** | CASE-LN-MIX-009 |
| **Presentation Type** | `mixed` |
| **Gold Standard** | Yes |

**Summary:**

- 40-year-old female with SLE and APLS (triple positive: lupus anticoagulant, anticardiolipin, anti-beta2GPI)
- Presented with Class III focal proliferative LN (25% glomeruli affected), AI 8, CI 1
- UPCR 2.2g/g, creatinine 1.1, albumin 2.9, eGFR 68
- Anti-dsDNA 220, C3 58, C4 10
- History of one fetal loss at 16 weeks (attributed to APLS)
- Treatment: MMF 2g/day + prednisone taper + HCQ + warfarin (INR 2-3 for APLS)
- 6-month outcome: UPCR 0.6g/g, partial response; APLS managed with continued anticoagulation
- Discussed pregnancy planning: warfarin to heparin switch at conception, low-dose aspirin, close monitoring

**Key Teaching Points:**

- Class III (focal proliferative) affects <50% of glomeruli; better prognosis than Class IV
- APLS complicates LN management: thrombotic risk, pregnancy loss
- APL antibody positivity requires anticoagulation (warfarin INR 2-3) or low-dose aspirin
- Warfarin is teratogenic (X); must switch to LMWH at conception
- Combined LN + APLS management requires multidisciplinary approach (nephrology, hematology, obstetrics)
- Class III may respond well to MMF-based induction with lower CyP requirement
