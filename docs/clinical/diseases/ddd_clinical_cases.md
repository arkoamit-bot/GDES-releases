### CASE-DDD-DRU-001: DDD with Ocular Drusen

**Type:** atypical
**Source:** DDD with ocular drusen

**History:**
A 45-year-old woman with known DDD (diagnosed age 30, stable on RAASi) presents with gradual vision loss over 2 years. She has been monitored for drusen since diagnosis. Recent fundoscopy shows extensive drusen-like deposits in the macula. Visual acuity: 20/60 both eyes.

**Examination:**
BP 124/78. No edema. Fundoscopy: numerous soft drusen in the macula (similar to age-related macular degeneration). Renal: well-compensated CKD (eGFR 52).

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 0.8 g/g
- eGFR: 52 mL/min
- Cr: 1.3 mg/dL
- C3: 65 (borderline low)
- C3NeF: Positive (weak)
- CH50: Normal
- Ophthalmology: Extensive drusen, early macular degeneration — DDD-related

**Biopsy Data:**
- performed: False
- note: Prior biopsy: classic DDD (dense intramembranous deposits); current renal status stable

**Diagnosis:**
Dense Deposit Disease with ocular complications (complement deposits in Bruch membrane causing drusen)

**Treatment:**
Continue ramipril 5 mg. Refer to retinal specialist. Monitor visual acuity Q6 months. AREDS vitamin supplements. Sun protection. No eculizumab (stable renal disease).

**Outcome:**
Visual acuity stable over 1 year (20/60). Renal stable (eGFR 50, UPCR 0.7). Requires long-term ophthalmology surveillance for progression to macular degeneration.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- age-related macular degeneration: score 0.3 — DDD-related drusen are clinically and histologically identical to AMD — DDD accelerates complement-mediated drusen formation in Bruch membrane

### CASE-DDD-LIP-001: DDD with Partial Lipodystrophy

**Type:** atypical
**Source:** DDD with lipodystrophy

**History:**
A 16-year-old boy presents for cosmetic concern: progressive loss of facial fat over 3 years (sunken cheeks, prominent zygoma). On routine screening: 2+ proteinuria, microscopic hematuria, normal eGFR. No edema. No hearing loss.

**Examination:**
BP 126/78. Striking facial: loss of buccal, temporal, and periorbital fat pads (partial lipodystrophy). No edema. Fundoscopy: no drusen.

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 1.2 g/g
- eGFR: 95 mL/min
- Cr: 0.7 mg/dL
- Albumin: 3.8 g/dL
- C3: 32 (low)
- C4: 38 (normal)
- C3NeF: Positive
- CH50: 20 U/mL
- Anti_CFH: Negative
- Fasting glucose: Normal
- HbA1c: 5.2%

**Biopsy Data:**
- LM: Mild mesangial hypercellularity
- IF: C3 2+ along GBM
- EM: Focal dense intramembranous deposits — early DDD

**Diagnosis:**
Dense Deposit Disease presenting with partial lipodystrophy — incidental renal findings

**Treatment:**
Ramipril 5 mg for proteinuria. Educate: partial lipodystrophy is cosmetic (no treatment). Autologous fat transfer may be considered. Monitor renal function and complement Q3-6 months. Ophthalmology baseline.

**Outcome:**
Month 12: UPCR 0.6 on ramipril, eGFR stable 92. C3 persistently low. Lipodystrophy unchanged (cosmetic only). Early treatment may delay progression. Long-term prognosis: favorable if proteinuria controlled.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- TBMD: score 0.1 — Hematuria + proteinuria with low C3 and C3NeF + lipodystrophy = DDD, not TBMD

### CASE-DDD-TXR-001: Post-Transplant Recurrence of DDD

**Type:** complications
**Source:** Post-transplant DDD recurrence

**History:**
A 28-year-old woman with DDD who reached ESKD at age 25 (despite prednisone and eculizumab) received a deceased donor kidney transplant 2 years ago. C3NeF was still positive at transplant. She presents with new proteinuria (UPCR 2.8) and rising Cr over 3 months.

**Examination:**
BP 142/88. Graft non-tender. 1+ edema. Fundoscopy: drusen progression.

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 2.8 g/g (was 0.3 post-transplant)
- eGFR: 38 mL/min (declined from 65)
- Cr: 1.9 mg/dL (was 1.1)
- C3: 30 (low)
- C3NeF: Positive (persistent)
- CH50: Normal
- Anti_GBM: Negative
- Tacrolimus: 8.5 ng/mL

**Biopsy Data:**
- LM: Mesangial hypercellularity, early GBM thickening
- IF: C3 3+ along GBM
- EM: Dense intramembranous deposits in graft GBM — recurrent DDD; no rejection

**Diagnosis:**
Post-Transplant Recurrent Dense Deposit Disease — 40-50% recurrence risk

**Treatment:**
Restart eculizumab 900 mg IV weekly x4 then 1200 mg q2w (meningococcal vaccination confirmed). Ramipril 10 mg. Tacrolimus continued. MMF 1 g BID. Prednisone 10 mg daily.

**Outcome:**
Month 3 after eculizumab restart: UPCR 1.2, eGFR 45, C3 improved to 55. Month 12: UPCR 0.6, eGFR 50. Graft function stabilized. Drusen stable. Ongoing eculizumab and monitoring.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- acute rejection: score 0.2 — C3-dominant IF and EM dense deposits confirm DDD recurrence, not rejection

### CASE-DDD-ECU-001: Eculizumab-Responsive DDD

**Type:** remission
**Source:** Eculizumab-responsive DDD

**History:**
A 20-year-old woman with DDD diagnosed at age 14 (treated with prednisone + MMF, partial response) presents with rapid decline: eGFR from 60 to 28 over 6 months despite RAASi and low-dose prednisone. New crescents on repeat biopsy (30%). No anti-CFH antibodies.

**Examination:**
BP 130/82. 1+ edema. No lipodystrophy.

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 4.5 g/g
- eGFR: 28 mL/min
- Cr: 2.4 mg/dL
- Albumin: 2.8 g/dL
- C3: 25 (low)
- C4: 35 (normal)
- C3NeF: Positive (strong)
- CH50: <10
- Anti_CFH: Negative
- Genetic: CFH heterozygous mutation (p.Tyr402His — risk allele for AMD also)

**Biopsy Data:**
- LM: Mesangial hypercellularity, 30% cellular/fibrocellular crescents, 20% IFTA
- IF: C3 3+, C5b-9 2+ along GBM
- EM: Ribbon-like dense intramembranous deposits; new subendothelial deposits suggesting activation

**Diagnosis:**
Dense Deposit Disease with rapidly progressive crescentic course — eculizumab candidate

**Treatment:**
Eculizumab 900 mg IV weekly x4 then 1200 mg q2w (meningococcal ACWY + MenB given). RAASi continued. Prednisone 20 mg (tapering). MMF continued.

**Outcome:**
Month 1: CH50 <10%, Cr stable 2.3. Month 3: UPCR 2.0, eGFR 38. Month 6: UPCR 0.6, eGFR 48. Month 12: UPCR 0.3, eGFR 52, C3 68 (improved). Excellent response. Eculizumab continues.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- c3: score 0.1 — DDD confirmed by intramembranous deposits; response to eculizumab supports complement-driven pathogenesis

### CASE-DDD-SLO-001: Slowly Progressive DDD — Favorable Outcome

**Type:** remission
**Source:** Slowly progressive DDD

**History:**
A 25-year-old woman with DDD diagnosed at age 8 (nephrotic syndrome, low C3, C3NeF positive, dense deposits on EM) has been followed for 17 years. She was treated with prednisone for 2 years in childhood and has been on RAASi since age 10. She now has mild proteinuria and stable CKD.

**Examination:**
BP 118/76. No edema. Fundoscopy: mild drusen (stable).

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 0.4 g/g
- eGFR: 62 mL/min
- Cr: 1.1 mg/dL
- Albumin: 3.9 g/dL
- C3: 62 (low normal)
- C3NeF: Negative (resolved)
- CH50: Normal
- Genetic: No mutation identified (likely C3NeF-mediated)

**Biopsy Data:**
- performed: False
- note: Prior childhood biopsy confirmed DDD. Current stable — no repeat biopsy needed

**Diagnosis:**
Slowly Progressive Dense Deposit Disease — long-term favorable outcome

**Treatment:**
Ramipril 5 mg daily. Annual monitoring: eGFR, UPCR, BP, ophthalmology. No immunosuppression. Continue RAASi.

**Outcome:**
Age 30: UPCR 0.3, eGFR 58 (slow decline over 22 years). Drusen stable — no visual impairment. Delivered healthy child (with high-risk obstetric monitoring). C3NeF negative for 10 years. Excellent example of DDD with favorable long-term course.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- N/A

### CASE-DDD-ADU-001: Adult-Onset DDD with Rapid Progression

**Type:** typical
**Source:** Adult anti-CFH DDD

**History:**
A 32-year-old man presents with nephrotic syndrome (edema, UPCR 6.0), hypertension, and eGFR 35. He had mild proteinuria noted 2 years ago but was lost to follow-up. Now has rapid decline over 3 months. No partial lipodystrophy. No family history.

**Examination:**
BP 160/95. 3+ edema to thighs. Pulmonary edema on CXR. No lipodystrophy.

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 6.0 g/g
- eGFR: 35 mL/min
- Cr: 2.2 mg/dL
- Albumin: 1.8 g/dL
- C3: 22 (low)
- C4: 28 (normal)
- C3NeF: Positive
- CH50: 15 U/mL
- Anti_CFH: Positive (titer 1:640)
- CFH: Low (factor H deficiency pattern)

**Biopsy Data:**
- LM: Mesangial hypercellularity, endocapillary proliferation, 25% crescents, 30% IFTA
- IF: C3 3+ along GBM and mesangium; IgG trace
- EM: Dense ribbon-like intramembranous deposits; subendothelial deposits also present (mixed pattern — C3GN-DDD overlap)

**Diagnosis:**
Dense Deposit Disease — adult onset with anti-CFH autoantibodies, rapidly progressive with crescents

**Treatment:**
Methylprednisolone 1 g IV x3 days. Plasma exchange 1.5 vol x10 sessions. Cyclophosphamide 750 mg/m2 IV x1. Ramipril 10 mg. Amlodipine 5 mg. Eculizumab considered if no response. Meningococcal vaccination before eculizumab.

**Outcome:**
Month 1: Cr stable 2.0, anti-CFH titer 1:80. Month 3: UPCR 3.5, eGFR 38, anti-CFH 1:40. Month 6: UPCR 2.0, eGFR 42. Continued PLEX + immunosuppression response. Eculizumab not needed. Long-term: eGFR stabilized at 40-45.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- c3: score 0.2 — Mixed C3GN-DDD features but dominant dense intramembranous deposits confirm DDD

### CASE-DDD-CHI-001: Classic DDD in a Child

**Type:** typical
**Source:** Classic childhood DDD

**History:**
An 11-year-old girl presents with 3-week history of periorbital and lower extremity edema, foamy urine, and weight gain of 5 kg. She had an upper respiratory infection 2 weeks ago. Her mother notes she has lost facial fat over the past year (sunken cheeks). No family history of kidney disease.

**Examination:**
BP 138/88 (>95th percentile). 2+ pitting edema legs, periorbital edema, ascites. Facial: loss of buccal and temporal fat pads (partial lipodystrophy). Fundoscopy: drusen-like deposits.

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 5.8 g/g
- eGFR: 82 mL/min
- Cr: 0.8 mg/dL
- Albumin: 2.1 g/dL
- C3: 28 mg/dL (low, normal 75-175)
- C4: 32 mg/dL (normal)
- C3NeF: Positive (strong)
- CH50: <10 U/mL
- Anti_CFH: Negative
- Cholesterol: 320 mg/dL
- Genetic: No pathogenic complement gene mutation identified

**Biopsy Data:**
- LM: Diffuse mesangial hypercellularity, endocapillary proliferation, GBM thickening
- IF: C3 3+ along GBM and mesangium; IgG trace; IgM negative; IgA negative
- EM: Ribbon-like highly electron-dense intramembranous deposits along entire length of GBM — pathognomonic for DDD; mesangial dense deposits also present

**Diagnosis:**
Dense Deposit Disease — childhood onset with classic triad: nephrotic syndrome, low C3 with normal C4, C3NeF positive

**Treatment:**
Ramipril 5 mg daily. Prednisone 1 mg/kg/day (40 mg) x8 wks then taper over 6 months. Atorvastatin 10 mg. Furosemide 20 mg for edema. Eculizumab not indicated (no crescents). Ophthalmology referral. Rare GN registry enrolment.

**Outcome:**
Month 2: edema resolved, UPCR 2.1, C3 stable low. Month 6: UPCR 1.5, eGFR 85, prednisone tapered to 10 mg. Month 12: UPCR 0.8, C3 45 (improved). Partial response to prednisone + RAASi. Long-term follow-up planned.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- c3: score 0.2 — C3GN has subendothelial deposits on EM — DDD has intramembranous dense ribbon-like deposits (pathognomonic difference)
- mpgn: score 0.1 — IC-MPGN has IgG + C3 on IF and subendothelial deposits — DDD is C3-dominant with no significant immunoglobulins

### CASE-DDD-GEN-001: Familial DDD — Complement Gene Mutation

**Type:** typical
**Source:** Familial DDD CFH mutation

**History:**
A 9-year-old boy presents with nephrotic syndrome. His older sister (age 14) had DDD diagnosed at age 12 and is on RAASi (stable). Consanguineous parents (first cousins). Family history: paternal uncle had kidney disease of unknown cause, died in his 30s.

**Examination:**
BP 132/86. 2+ edema. No lipodystrophy. Fundoscopy: normal.

**Laboratory Data:**
- UPCR: 6.5 g/g
- eGFR: 78 mL/min
- Cr: 0.6 mg/dL
- Albumin: 1.9 g/dL
- C3: 18 (very low)
- C4: 32 (normal)
- C3NeF: Negative
- CH50: 5 U/mL
- CFH: Very low (<10% normal)
- Anti_CFH: Negative
- Genetic: CFH homozygous c.325G>A (p.Gly109Arg) — complete factor H deficiency

**Biopsy Data:**
- LM: Diffuse mesangial hypercellularity, endocapillary proliferation
- IF: C3 3+ along GBM and mesangium
- EM: Dense ribbon-like intramembranous deposits — DDD pattern

**Diagnosis:**
Familial Dense Deposit Disease due to homozygous CFH mutation (complete factor H deficiency)

**Treatment:**
Ramipril 2.5 mg. Fresh frozen plasma (FFP) infusion for CFH replacement (30 mL/kg q2 weeks). Prednisone 1 mg/kg/day x8 wks. Eculizumab may be considered if progressive. Sibling already on treatment. Genetic counseling.

**Outcome:**
Month 3: UPCR 3.0, eGFR 80, C3 35 (partial improvement with FFP). Month 6: UPCR 1.8, eGFR 82. FFP continues. Prednisone tapered. Sibling stable on RAASi only.

**Differential Diagnosis:**
- C3GN: score 0.2 — C3NeF-negative DDD with CFH mutation — EM confirms DDD pattern not C3GN
