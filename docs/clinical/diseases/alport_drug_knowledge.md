## 2. Drug Classification

| Drug Class | Role | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| ACE inhibitors (Ramipril, Lisinopril) | First-line renoprotection: reduce proteinuria, slow ESKD | KDIGO 1B |
| ARBs (Losartan, Valsartan) | Alternative if ACEi intolerant | KDIGO 1B |
| SGLT2i (Dapagliflozin, Empagliflozin) | Additional renoprotection after RAASi | ERA/EDTA 2C |
| CCBs (Amlodipine) | BP management add-on | KDIGO 1C |
| Thiazide diuretics | BP management add-on | KDIGO 1C |
| Loop diuretics (Furosemide) | Edema management | KDIGO 1C |
| Immunosuppression | NOT indicated for Alport syndrome (unless post-transplant anti-GBM) | KDIGO 1A |
| PLEX + CYC + Steroids | Post-transplant anti-GBM disease | KDIGO 2C |
| Rituximab | Post-transplant anti-GBM (refractory) | KDIGO 2D |

## 3. Key Drugs

### 3.1 ACE Inhibitors (RAASi)
- Ramipril 2.5-10 mg daily. Start low, titrate to max tolerated dose
- Monitor K+, Cr 2-4 wks after initiation/dose change
- Target proteinuria reduction 40-60%
- Start at first sign of proteinuria (even microalbuminuria)
- Contraindications: pregnancy (teratogenic), bilateral renal artery stenosis, angioedema history

### 3.2 SGLT2 Inhibitors
- Dapagliflozin 10 mg or Empagliflozin 10 mg daily
- eGFR >=20 to initiate; continue until dialysis/transplant
- Monitor for volume depletion, euglycemic DKA (rare)
- Additional cardiovascular benefit
- Start only after RAASi established

### 3.3 CCBs
- Amlodipine 2.5-10 mg daily
- Add-on therapy if BP not at target on RAASi + SGLT2i
- Monitor for peripheral edema

### 3.4 Post-Transplant Anti-GBM Regimen
- Methylprednisolone 1 g IV x3 days
- Plasma exchange 1.5 vol x10-14 sessions
- Cyclophosphamide 750 mg/m2 IV x1-3
- Rituximab 375 mg/m2 weekly x4 (alternative to CYC if contraindicated)

## 4. Drug Safety

| Drug | Key Side Effects | Monitoring |
|---|---|---|
| ACEi | Cough, hyperkalemia, AKI, angioedema | K+, Cr at 2-4 wks, then Q3mo |
| ARB | Hyperkalemia, AKI | K+, Cr at 2-4 wks, then Q3mo |
| SGLT2i | Volume depletion, UTI, DKA (rare) | Volume status, eGFR |
| Amlodipine | Peripheral edema, dizziness | Clinical assessment |
| Furosemide | Electrolyte depletion, AKI, ototoxicity | K+, Cr, hearing |
| PLEX | Bleeding, hypocalcemia, anaphylaxis | Coags, Ca, vitals |
| Cyclophosphamide | Myelosuppression, hemorrhagic cystitis, infertility | CBC, UA, LFT |
| Rituximab | Infusion reaction, PML, HBV reactivation | Screen HBV, CBC |

---

## 5. Transplant Considerations

- NO standard immunosuppression modification needed for Alport
- Anti-GBM surveillance: monitor anti-GBM antibodies, hematuria, Cr at 3, 6, 12 months then annually
- COL4A5+ females may be living donors (low proteinuria risk)
- No disease recurrence in allograft
