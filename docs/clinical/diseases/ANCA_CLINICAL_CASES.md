## 1. Overview

This document presents 8 clinical cases covering the spectrum of ANCA-associated vasculitis with renal involvement, including both serotypes (PR3, MPO), all clinical syndromes (GPA, MPA, EGPA), and key management scenarios (rapidly progressive, relapsing, refractory, complicated, and special populations). Each case follows a standardised format including presentation, investigations, diagnosis, management, and teaching points.

## 2. CASE-ANCA-RAP-001: PR3-Positive GPA with Diffuse Alveolar Haemorrhage

**Classification**: Rapid Presentation  
**Serotype**: PR3/c-ANCA positive  
**Source**: Based on RAVE trial population and PEXIVAS DAH subgroup  

### Presentation

A 45-year-old female presents to the emergency department with a 3-day history of haemoptysis, dyspnoea at rest, and oliguria. She reports a 2-month history of sinus congestion, arthralgias, and 8 kg weight loss over 3 months. On examination she is hypoxic (SpO2 88% on room air), tachypnoeic (RR 28/min), and hypertensive (BP 160/95 mmHg). Chest auscultation reveals bilateral crackles. Lower extremity oedema is absent.

### Investigations

| Test | Result | Reference Range |
|------|--------|-----------------|
| Serum creatinine | 4.8 mg/dL | 0.7–1.3 mg/dL |
| eGFR | 12 mL/min/1.73m2 | >90 mL/min/1.73m2 |
| PR3-ANCA (ELISA) | >200 U/mL | <20 U/mL |
| c-ANCA (IIF) | Positive (1:640) | Negative |
| MPO-ANCA | Negative | Negative |
| Haemoglobin | 7.8 g/dL | 12–16 g/dL |
| CRP | 185 mg/dL | <5 mg/dL |
| Urinalysis | Dysmorphic RBCs, RBC casts | Negative |
| UPCR | 1.8 g/g | <0.3 g/g |
| A-a gradient | 45 mmHg | <10 mmHg |
| Anti-GBM | Negative | Negative |

### Imaging and Biopsy

Chest CT shows bilateral ground-glass opacities and consolidation consistent with DAH. Renal biopsy (16 glomeruli) reveals necrotising crescentic GN in 12 of 16 glomeruli (75% crescents, predominantly cellular), fibrinoid necrosis, and pauci-immune IF. No globally sclerotic glomeruli. Berden classification: crescentic class. Brix score: 1 (low chronicity).

### Diagnosis

PR3-positive GPA with rapidly progressive GN and severe DAH (pulmonary-renal syndrome). Berden crescentic class.

### Management

1. Methylprednisolone 1 g IV daily x 3 (Day 0–2)
2. Rituximab 375 mg/m2 weekly x 4 (Day 0, 7, 14, 21)
3. PLEX 7 sessions over 14 days (for severe DAH with hypoxaemia)
4. Prednisone 1 mg/kg/day (60 mg) with taper over 16 weeks
5. Avacopan 30 mg BID (initiated Day 7 after stabilisation)
6. TMP-SMX 800/160 mg daily (PJP prophylaxis)
7. Supplement oxygen (weaned over 7 days)

### Outcome

By Day 7, haemoptysis resolved and SpO2 normalised. By Week 4, Cr declined to 2.1 mg/dL and eGFR improved to 35 mL/min/1.73m2. At 6 months, Cr 1.4 mg/dL, PR3-ANCA negative, BVAS = 0, off prednisone on avacopan. At 12 months, sustained remission maintained with avacopan and rituximab 500 mg q6mo.

### Teaching Points

| Point | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Pulmonary-renal syndrome | DAH with RPGN is a medical emergency |
| PLEX indication | Severe DAH with hypoxaemia despite steroids |
| Rituximab first-line | PR3-AAV benefits from rituximab over CyP |
| Avacopan integration | Can be added after initial stabilisation for steroid-sparing |
| Berden crescentic class | High proportion of cellular crescents favours recovery |

## 3. CASE-ANCA-ADV-001: MPO-Positive MPA with Advanced CKD

**Classification**: Advanced Presentation  
**Serotype**: MPO/p-ANCA positive  
**Source**: Based on Berden sclerotic class population  

### Presentation

A 68-year-old male presents with 6 months of progressive fatigue, anorexia, weight loss, and lower extremity oedema. He has been on antihypertensive therapy for 5 years with poor control. Urinalysis in primary care showed 2+ protein and microscopic haematuria. eGFR in clinic is 18 mL/min/1.73m2 (Cr 3.2 mg/dL, increased from 1.5 mg/dL 8 months prior). He has no pulmonary or ENT symptoms.

### Investigations

MPO-ANCA positive (150 U/mL), PR3-ANCA negative, p-ANCA positive by IIF. Renal biopsy: 22 glomeruli, 12 globally sclerotic (55%), 4 cellular crescents, 6 fibrocellular crescents. Significant interstitial fibrosis (40%) and tubular atrophy. Pauci-immune IF. Berden classification: sclerotic class. Brix score: 4 (high chronicity).

### Diagnosis

MPO-positive MPA with advanced CKD (sclerotic Berden class). High chronicity limits renal recovery potential.

### Management

1. Methylprednisolone 500 mg IV x 2 (reduced dose given advanced CKD and infection risk; PEXIVAS-informed)
2. Rituximab 375 mg/m2 weekly x 4
3. Prednisone 0.5 mg/kg/day (35 mg) with accelerated taper over 10 weeks
4. TMP-SMX 3x/week for PJP prophylaxis
5. Realistic counselling: eGFR 18, sclerotic class, interstitial fibrosis

### Outcome

Creatinine plateaued at 2.8 mg/dL (eGFR 22) after induction. At 6 months, no further decline but no recovery. MPO-ANCA became negative. He progressed to ESKD at 18 months and initiated haemodialysis. Transplant evaluation initiated with AAV in remission. Received deceased-donor transplant at 24 months with no recurrence at 3-year follow-up.

### Teaching Points

| Point | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Berden sclerotic class | >50% globally sclerotic glomeruli predicts irreversible damage |
| Brix score | High chronicity = low recovery potential |
| Reduced-dose steroids | PEXIVAS supports lower steroid dosing |
| ESKD transition | Prepare early; remission before transplant listing |
| Age and comorbidity | Older age increases infection risk; reduce immunosuppression |

## 4. CASE-ANCA-REL-001: Relapsing PR3-Positive GPA

**Classification**: Relapse  
**Serotype**: PR3/c-ANCA positive  
**Source**: Based on RITAZAREM trial population  

### Presentation

A 35-year-old female with known PR3-positive GPA (originally diagnosed 4 years ago, treated with rituximab induction, completed 2-year rituximab maintenance 6 months ago) presents with sinus pain, arthralgias, and 1+ haematuria on dipstick. PR3-ANCA titre has risen from negative to 80 U/mL over 3 months. Creatinine stable at 1.0 mg/dL. BVAS = 4 (arthralgia, sinusitis, haematuria).

### Diagnosis

Minor relapse of PR3-positive GPA (limited ENT, joint, renal activity without organ-threatening features).

### Management

1. Rituximab 375 mg/m2 weekly x 4 (re-induction based on RITAZAREM protocol)
2. Methylprednisolone 500 mg IV x 1 (limited pulse)
3. Prednisone 30 mg/day with rapid taper over 8 weeks
4. Extended rituximab maintenance: 500 mg q4mo (rather than q6mo) for 2 years

### Outcome

Complete remission by 8 weeks. PR3-ANCA negative by 12 weeks. No further relapses on q4mo rituximab. Extended maintenance planned for total 5 years given PR3 serotype and history of relapse.

### Teaching Points

| Point | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| PR3 relapse risk | Higher than MPO; ANCA titre monitoring helpful |
| RITAZAREM protocol | Re-induction with rituximab; intensify dosing interval |
| Rising ANCA | Not always treated pre-emptively (predicts not mandates) |
| Extended maintenance | Consider >2 years in high-risk PR3 patients |

## 5. CASE-ANCA-RES-001: Refractory MPO-Positive MPA with Avacopan Response

**Classification**: Resistant  
**Serotype**: MPO/p-ANCA positive  
**Source**: Based on ADVOCATE trial population  

### Presentation

A 58-year-old male with MPO-positive MPA, renal-limited disease, failed to achieve remission after 16 weeks of cyclophosphamide (4 doses, cumulative 4.8 g) and prednisone taper. Creatinine remains at 2.5 mg/dL (baseline 2.0), MPO-ANCA positive at 90 U/mL, persistent dysmorphic haematuria. BVAS = 6.

### Diagnosis

Refractory MPA (resistant to cyclophosphamide induction).

### Management

1. Switch to rituximab 375 mg/m2 weekly x 4
2. Discontinue cyclophosphamide (cumulative limit respected)
3. Initiate avacopan 30 mg BID for steroid-sparing
4. Prednisone taper continued (currently 20 mg/day)
5. Re-biopsy: 18 glomeruli, 6 cellular crescents, 3 fibrocellular, 9 normal

### Outcome

By Week 8 of rituximab-avacopan, Cr improved to 1.8 mg/dL. By Week 16, MPO-ANCA negative, BVAS = 0. Prednisone tapered off by Week 20. Avacopan continued for 52 weeks. Sustained remission at 18 months.

### Teaching Points

| Point | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Definition refractory | No response to standard therapy at 12–16 weeks |
| Switch strategy | CyP to rituximab is established salvage strategy |
| Avacopan in refractory | C5aR blockade provides complement-directed effect |
| Re-biopsy value | Exclude irreversible chronicity; guides decision to persist |

## 6. CASE-ANCA-EAR-001: Early MPO-Positive Renal-Limited and ENT AAV

**Classification**: Early Presentation  
**Serotype**: MPO/p-ANCA positive  
**Source**: Based on early AAV detection cohort  

### Presentation

A 42-year-old female presents with 6 weeks of nasal congestion, crusting, and episodic epistaxis seen by ENT. Urinalysis performed for screening shows microscopic haematuria (10–20 RBCs/hpf) and 1+ protein (UPCR 0.6 g/g). Creatinine 1.0 mg/dL, eGFR 78. MPO-ANCA positive (45 U/mL). Renal biopsy: 14 glomeruli, 2 cellular crescents, focal segmental necrotising lesions, pauci-immune IF.

### Diagnosis

Early MPO-positive MPA with limited ENT involvement and subclinical crescentic GN.

### Management

1. Rituximab 375 mg/m2 weekly x 4
2. Prednisone 0.5 mg/kg/day (30 mg) with taper over 12 weeks
3. TMP-SMX PJP prophylaxis
4. Avacopan considered but not initiated (low disease activity; steroid-sparing less critical)

### Outcome

Complete renal remission at 12 weeks. ENT symptoms resolved by 6 weeks. Creatinine stable at 0.9 mg/dL. ANCA negative at 6 months. Maintenance rituximab 500 mg q6mo.

### Teaching Points

| Point | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Subclinical AAV | Urinalysis screening in ENT patients can detect early GN |
| Focal Berden class | >50% normal glomeruli = excellent prognosis |
| Minimal steroids | Early disease can be managed with lower steroid burden |
| Treatment prevents ESKD | Early intervention prevents crescent progression |

## 7. CASE-ANCA-COM-001: Double-Positive ANCA and Anti-GBM Disease

**Classification**: Complications  
**Serotype**: MPO/p-ANCA positive  
**Source**: Based on double-positive cohort series  

### Presentation

A 55-year-old male presents with oliguric AKI (Cr 6.5 mg/dL, eGFR 8), haemoptysis, and hypoxia. MPO-ANCA positive (120 U/mL). Anti-GBM ELISA positive (85 U/mL; normal <20). Renal biopsy: necrotising crescentic GN (14/18 glomeruli with crescents), linear IgG staining on IF (anti-GBM), plus pauci-immune staining pattern consistent with overlap.

### Diagnosis

Double-positive ANCA-associated vasculitis and anti-GBM disease.

### Management

1. Methylprednisolone 1 g IV x 3
2. PLEX 7 sessions over 14 days (plus additional 3–7 sessions until anti-GBM negative)
3. Cyclophosphamide IV 15 mg/kg (q3weeks) (preferred over rituximab in anti-GBM overlap)
4. Prednisone 1 mg/kg/day with taper
5. TMP-SMX prophylaxis

### Outcome

Anti-GBM became negative after 10 PLEX sessions. Dialysis-dependent at 4 weeks; no renal recovery by 12 weeks. Listed for transplantation with AAV in remission. One-year post-transplant, no recurrence of either disease.

### Teaching Points

| Point | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Double-positive prevalence | 5–10% of AAV patients also have anti-GBM |
| Worst renal prognosis | <50% recover independent renal function |
| Aggressive PLEX | Continue until anti-GBM negative, not fixed 7 sessions |
| Cyclophosphamide over rituximab | Anti-GBM evidence base uses CyP |
| Transplant viable | Both diseases in remission; recurrence rare |

## 8. CASE-ANCA-PED-001: Paediatric PR3-Positive GPA

**Classification**: Special Population  
**Serotype**: PR3/c-ANCA positive  
**Source**: Based on paediatric AAV cohort studies  

### Presentation

A 14-year-old female presents with 2 weeks of haematuria, periorbital oedema, and epistaxis. Cr 1.8 mg/dL (eGFR 42), PR3-ANCA positive (180 U/mL). Renal biopsy: 20 glomeruli, 10 cellular crescents, fibrinoid necrosis, pauci-immune. ENT exam shows granulomatous nasal lesions.

### Diagnosis

Paediatric PR3-positive GPA with RPGN.

### Management

1. Methylprednisolone 500 mg IV x 3 (weight-adjusted pulse)
2. Rituximab 375 mg/m2 weekly x 4 (preferred over CyP for fertility preservation)
3. Prednisone 1 mg/kg/day (50 mg) with taper over 14 weeks
4. TMP-SMX prophylaxis
5. Avacopan not used (limited paediatric data at time of management)

### Outcome

Complete remission at 12 weeks. Creatinine 0.7 mg/dL (eGFR >90). PR3-ANCA negative at 6 months. Maintenance rituximab 500 mg q6mo. Growth and development monitored. Stable at 3-year follow-up.

### Teaching Points

| Point | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Paediatric AAV | Similar to adult but fertility preservation paramount |
| Rituximab preferred | Avoids cyclophosphamide gonadotoxicity |
| Growth monitoring | Chronic steroids and CKD affect growth |
| Transition to adult care | Structured transition programme necessary |

## 9. CASE-ANCA-TXR-001: Post-Transplant Recurrence of MPO-Positive MPA

**Classification**: Special Population  
**Serotype**: MPO/p-ANCA positive  
**Source**: Based on post-transplant AAV recurrence literature  

### Presentation

A 50-year-old male with MPO-positive MPA who received a deceased-donor kidney transplant 18 months ago. AAV was in serological and clinical remission prior to transplant. Current immunosuppression: tacrolimus, mycophenolate mofetil, and low-dose prednisone. Presents with rising Cr (1.2 to 1.9 mg/dL), new haematuria, and MPO-ANCA positive (65 U/mL). Biopsy shows 3 cellular crescents in 12 glomeruli with fibrinoid necrosis, pauci-immune IF. No rejection.

### Diagnosis

Post-transplant recurrence of MPO-positive AAV.

### Management

1. Methylprednisolone 500 mg IV x 2
2. Rituximab 375 mg/m2 weekly x 4
3. MMF dose increased
4. No change to tacrolimus
5. TMP-SXM PJP prophylaxis

### Outcome

Creatinine stabilised at 1.5 mg/dL after rituximab. MPO-ANCA negative at 3 months. No graft loss. Continued standard maintenance immunosuppression. At 5 years post-transplant, graft function stable (eGFR 45) with no further recurrence.

### Teaching Points

| Point | Detail |
|-------|--------|
| Recurrence rate | 10–20% post-transplant; higher in PR3 than MPO |
| Not contraindication | AAV patients have excellent transplant outcomes overall |
| Treatment same | Re-induction with rituximab; maintain calcineurin inhibitor |
| Monitor ANCA post-Tx | Rising titre may predict recurrence |
| Graft survival | Excellent with prompt treatment of recurrence |
