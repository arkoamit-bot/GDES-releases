### CASE-TCMR-MIX-001: Mixed TCMR + ABMR Rejection
**Type:** atypical
**Source:** Mixed TCMR/ABMR
**History:**
A 45-year-old woman 3 years post-transplant. Cr 1.0 to 2.6 over 2 weeks. Tacrolimus level low (3.8). DSA positive (de novo anti-DQ MFI 2800). C4d positive on biopsy. Both TCMR and ABMR features.
**Examination:**
BP 146/86, graft tender, urine output low-normal.
**Laboratory Data:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 26 mL/min |
| Cr | 2.6 mg/dL |
| DSA | De novo anti-DQ MFI 2800, C1q-negative |
| Tacrolimus trough | 3.8 ng/mL |
| BK PCR | Negative |
**Biopsy Data:**
| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | i2, t2, g1, ptc1, v0 — both tubulitis and microvascular inflammation |
| IF | C4d2 (focal positive) |
| EM | Endothelial swelling, no GBM duplication (acute phase) |
**Diagnosis:**
Mixed TCMR (Type I) + Active ABMR — de novo DSA, under-immunosuppression
**Treatment:**
MP 500 mg IV x3. IVIG 2 g/kg. PLEX x5. Rituximab 375 mg/m2 x1. Optimise tacrolimus 6-8 ng/mL.
**Outcome:**
Week 4: Cr 1.4. DSA MFI 600. Month 3: Cr 1.1. DSA negative. Illustrates: mixed rejection requires treating BOTH components — steroids/ATG alone not sufficient.
**Differential Diagnosis:**
| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| antibodyMediatedRejection | 0.3 | Both TCMR features (i>0, t>0) AND ABMR features (g>0, ptc>0, C4d+, DSA+) = mixed rejection. Each component would be undertreated if only one diagnosed. |

### CASE-TCMR-SR-001: Steroid-Resistant Type I TCMR
**Type:** atypical
**Source:** Steroid-resistant TCMR
**History:**
A 42-year-old woman 8 months post-transplant. Cr 1.1 to 2.4. Treated with MP 500 mg IV x3 — no improvement by Day 7 (Cr still 2.2). DSA negative. BK negative. Tacrolimus trough 5.8 (adequate).
**Examination:**
BP 138/84, graft mildly tender, no fever.
**Laboratory Data:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 28 mL/min |
| Cr | 2.4 mg/dL (post-steroids: 2.2) |
| DSA | Negative |
| BK PCR | Negative |
| Tacrolimus trough | 5.8 ng/mL |
**Biopsy Data:**
| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | i3, t3, v0 (Type IB) — dense interstitial infiltrate, severe tubulitis >10 cells/tubule, no arteritis |
| IF | C4d0 |
| EM | Normal |
**Diagnosis:**
Steroid-Resistant Type IB TCMR — no response to pulse MP
**Treatment:**
ATG 1.5 mg/kg IV x10 days. Re-biopsy planned if no response by Day 14. CMV/PJP prophylaxis.
**Outcome:**
Day 14: Cr 1.4. Month 1: Cr 1.1. Complete response to ATG. DSA negative at 3 months. Illustrates: steroid resistance occurs in 20-30% of Type I TCMR — ATG effective.
**Differential Diagnosis:**
| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| antibodyMediatedRejection | 0.1 | No C4d, no DSA, no microvascular inflammation = pure TCMR. Steroid resistance in TCMR is well recognised — does not imply ABMR |

### CASE-TCMR-BKV-001: BKVN Mimicking TCMR
**Type:** complications
**Source:** BKVN mimic
**History:**
A 60-year-old man 10 months post-transplant. Cr 1.0 to 2.0 over 4 weeks. Tacrolimus trough 6.0. DSA negative. BK PCR 150,000 copies/mL. Biopsy shows tubulitis and interstitial infiltrate with plasma cells.
**Examination:**
BP 130/78, graft non-tender, no fever.
**Laboratory Data:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 34 mL/min |
| Cr | 2.0 mg/dL |
| DSA | Negative |
| BK PCR | 150,000 copies/mL (high) |
| SV40 stain | Positive on biopsy |
| Tacrolimus trough | 6.0 ng/mL |
**Biopsy Data:**
| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | i2, t2, v0 — prominent plasma cells in infiltrate, viral cytopathic changes (intranuclear inclusions) |
| IF | C4d0 |
| SV40 | Positive (confirmed BKVN) |
| EM | No glomerular changes |
**Diagnosis:**
BK Virus Nephropathy — SV40 positive. NOT TCMR. Histologic mimic of TCMR.
**Treatment:**
Reduce immunosuppression: lower tacrolimus target 4-6 ng/mL, reduce MMF to 1 g/day. Monitor BK PCR weekly. Do NOT give steroids or ATG — would worsen BKVN.
**Outcome:**
Week 4: BK PCR 25,000. Week 8: BK PCR 3000 (clearing). Cr 1.6 (partial recovery). Illustrates critical distinction: BKVN mimics TCMR — SV40 and BK PCR distinguish. Giving ATG would be catastrophic.
**Differential Diagnosis:**
| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| tCellMediatedRejection | 0.3 | Identical histology (i>0, t>0, plasma cells) but SV40+ and BK PCR+ = BKVN. This is the most important differential — TCMR treatment worsens BKVN |

### CASE-TCMR-III-001: Type III TCMR with Graft Loss
**Type:** complications
**Source:** Type III TCMR
**History:**
A 55-year-old man 1 year post-transplant. Stopped all immunosuppression 3 weeks ago (non-adherence). Presents with anuria, Cr 4.8, graft tenderness, fever 39C. Graft rupture clinically suspected.
**Examination:**
BP 160/96, graft markedly tender and enlarged, anuria, fever 39C.
**Laboratory Data:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 12 mL/min |
| Cr | 4.8 mg/dL |
| Tacrolimus trough | 0 (not detectable) |
| DSA | Negative (but potential to develop) |
| BK PCR | Negative |
| WBC | 15,000 |
**Biopsy Data:**
| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | v3 (transmural arteritis + fibrinoid necrosis), i3, t3, haemorrhagic infarction in sections |
| IF | C4d0 |
| EM | Infarction, no glomerular deposits |
**Diagnosis:**
Type III TCMR (Severe) — complete non-adherence, graft infarction
**Treatment:**
ATG 1.5 mg/kg IV — started urgently. Only 2 doses tolerated. Graft non-viable — transplant nephrectomy required on Day 4 (ruptured graft). Return to dialysis.
**Outcome:**
Graft loss. Nephrectomy confirmed haemorrhagic infarction. Patient returned to dialysis. Re-transplant evaluation: need to demonstrate adherence before re-listing. Illustrates catastrophic outcome of untreated severe TCMR.
**Differential Diagnosis:**
| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| antibodyMediatedRejection | 0.1 | C4d0, DSA negative, transmural arteritis with fibrinoid necrosis = Type III TCMR. ABMR does NOT cause transmural arteritis. Extensive infarction is hallmark of v3. |

### CASE-TCMR-LAT-001: Late TCMR from Non-Adherence
**Type:** typical
**Source:** Late TCMR
**History:**
A 28-year-old man 5 years post-transplant stopped taking tacrolimus 3 months ago (ran out of insurance). Cr rising insidiously from 1.4 to 3.2 over 2 months. Now eGFR 22. No fever. No graft pain.
**Examination:**
BP 142/86, no oedema, graft non-tender.
**Laboratory Data:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 22 mL/min |
| Cr | 3.2 mg/dL |
| Tacrolimus trough | 0.5 ng/mL (negligible) |
| DSA | Negative (no DSA detected — pure TCMR) |
| BK PCR | Negative |
| UPCR | 0.3 g/g |
**Biopsy Data:**
| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | i2, t2, v0, IFTA 20% — active TCMR on a background of mild chronic damage |
| IF | C4d0 |
| EM | No transplant glomerulopathy (cg0) |
**Diagnosis:**
Late Type I TCMR — non-adherence (no insurance, stopped tacrolimus)
**Treatment:**
Restart tacrolimus (target 5-8 ng/mL), MMF 1 g BID. MP 500 mg IV x3. Prednisone taper. Social work: financial assistance, medication access programs.
**Outcome:**
Week 2: Cr 2.0. Month 1: Cr 1.6. Month 3: Cr 1.5 (not back to baseline — IFTA limits recovery). DSA negative. Illustrates late TCMR with incomplete recovery due to chronic damage.
**Differential Diagnosis:**
| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| antibodyMediatedRejection | 0.3 | No DSA, C4d0, no microvascular inflammation = pure TCMR. Late ABMR would have transplant glomerulopathy and DSA |

### CASE-TCMR-SUB-001: Subclinical TCMR on Protocol Biopsy
**Type:** typical
**Source:** Subclinical TCMR
**History:**
A 40-year-old woman 3 months post-living donor kidney transplant. Stable creatinine 0.9. Protocol biopsy (routine surveillance) reveals unexpected findings. Tacrolimus trough 5.0.
**Examination:**
BP 128/76, no oedema, graft non-tender, well-functioning graft clinically.
**Laboratory Data:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 68 mL/min |
| Cr | 0.9 mg/dL |
| UPCR | 0.1 g/g |
| DSA | Negative |
| Tacrolimus trough | 5.0 ng/mL |
| BK PCR | Negative |
**Biopsy Data:**
| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | i1, t1, v0 (borderline changes) — focal mild interstitial infiltrate and tubulitis. No arteritis |
| IF | C4d0 |
| EM | Normal glomeruli and capillaries |
**Diagnosis:**
Subclinical TCMR (borderline) — detected on 3-month protocol biopsy
**Treatment:**
Optimise tacrolimus target 6-8 ng/mL (increase from 5.0). Consider MP 250 mg IV x2 if clinical suspicion high. Close observation with monthly Cr and DSA.
**Outcome:**
Month 6: protocol biopsy — i0, t0 (resolved). Cr 0.9. DSA negative. Illustrates value of protocol biopsy: early detection and treatment of subclinical TCMR prevents progression.
**Differential Diagnosis:**
| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| cniToxicity | 0.1 | No arteriolar hyalinosis, no striped fibrosis, normal EM = not CNI toxicity. Subclinical TCMR has i>0/t>0 even with normal Cr |

### CASE-TCMR-I-001: Type I TCMR — Steroid-Responsive
**Type:** typical
**Source:** Type I TCMR
**History:**
A 35-year-old woman 6 months post-deceased donor kidney transplant (tacrolimus/MMF/prednisone) presents with creatinine rise from 1.0 to 1.8 over 1 week. No oliguria. No fever. Tacrolimus trough 4.2 ng/mL (sub-therapeutic last 2 months). Adherent per self-report.
**Examination:**
BP 134/82, graft non-tender, no oedema.
**Laboratory Data:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 38 mL/min |
| Cr | 1.8 mg/dL |
| Tacrolimus trough | 4.2 ng/mL |
| DSA | Negative (class I and II) |
| BK PCR | Negative |
| CMV PCR | Negative |
**Biopsy Data:**
| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | i2, t2, v0 (Type IA) — diffuse interstitial mononuclear infiltrate, moderate tubulitis. No arteritis |
| IF | C4d0 (negative) |
| EM | No glomerular or capillary changes |
**Diagnosis:**
Type IA TCMR — associated with sub-therapeutic tacrolimus
**Treatment:**
Methylprednisolone 500 mg IV daily x3. Oral prednisone 60 mg/day taper over 3 weeks. Tacrolimus dose increased (target 6-8 ng/mL). MMF continued 1 g BID.
**Outcome:**
Week 1: Cr 1.3. Week 2: Cr 1.1 (baseline). Tacrolimus 7.2. Month 3: stable Cr 1.0. DSA negative. Illustrates typical steroid-responsive Type I TCMR.
**Differential Diagnosis:**
| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| antibodyMediatedRejection | 0.2 | C4d0, DSA negative, arteritis absent, tubulitis present = TCMR not ABMR. ABMR has microvascular inflammation (g+ptc) and DSA |

### CASE-TCMR-II-001: Type II TCMR Requiring ATG
**Type:** typical
**Source:** Type II TCMR
**History:**
A 50-year-old man 2 years post-transplant (tacrolimus/MMF). Non-adherent to tacrolimus (trough <3 ng/mL for 4 months). Presents with Cr 1.2 to 2.8 over 10 days. Graft tenderness. Fever 38.0C.
**Examination:**
BP 148/88, graft tender, temperature 38C, urine output reduced.
**Laboratory Data:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 24 mL/min |
| Cr | 2.8 mg/dL |
| Tacrolimus trough | 2.5 ng/mL |
| DSA | Negative |
| BK PCR | Negative |
| WBC | Normal |
**Biopsy Data:**
| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | i2, t2, v1 (Type IIA) — interstitial infiltrate, tubulitis, intimal arteritis <25% luminal area |
| IF | C4d0 |
| EM | No glomerular changes, no GBM duplication |
**Diagnosis:**
Type IIA TCMR — non-adherence to tacrolimus
**Treatment:**
ATG (Thymoglobulin) 1.5 mg/kg IV daily x7 days (total 10.5 mg/kg). Pre-medicate. Optimise tacrolimus (target 6-8). Valganciclovir CMV prophylaxis. TMP-SMX PJP prophylaxis. Adherence counselling.
**Outcome:**
Day 5: Cr 1.8. Day 10: Cr 1.4. Week 4: Cr 1.1. DSA negative at 1 month. Adherence improved. Patient educated about consequences. Illustrates vascular TCMR — requires ATG, not just steroids.
**Differential Diagnosis:**
| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| antibodyMediatedRejection | 0.1 | Arteritis (v1) + tubulitis (t2) + C4d0 + DSA negative = TCMR Type II. In ABMR, v-lesion is accompanied by g+ptc and C4d/DSA |
