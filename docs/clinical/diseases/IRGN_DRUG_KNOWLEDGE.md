## 1. Document Purpose

This document provides the complete drug knowledge specification for Infection-Related Glomerulonephritis pharmacotherapy. Most patients require only supportive care; immunosuppression is reserved for crescentic/RPGN cases. All agents for active IRGN treatment are used off-label.

---

## 2. Drug Classification Overview

| Drug Class | Role | Setting | Evidence Level |
|---|---|---|---|
| Antihypertensives (CCB, beta-blocker) | BP control during acute nephritic phase | First-line acute HTN | KDIGO 1B |
| Diuretics (furosemide) | Volume overload management | Oedema, pulmonary congestion | KDIGO 1B |
| Antibiotics (penicillin) | Treat active GAS infection | Active infection | IDSA 1A |
| Corticosteroids | Immunosuppression | Crescentic >50% / RPGN | KDIGO 2D |
| Cyclophosphamide | Immunosuppression | Crescentic RPGN | KDIGO 2D |
| MMF | Steroid-sparing | Atypical/refractory | KDIGO 2D |
| ACEi/ARB | Renoprotection | Persistent proteinuria >3mo | KDIGO 1B |
| Plasma exchange | Antibody removal | Dialysis-dependent RPGN | KDIGO 2D |

## 3. Antihypertensives

### 3.1 Acute Phase

| Drug | Dose | Route | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Amlodipine | 2.5-10 mg daily | PO | First-line, well tolerated |
| Nifedipine (short-acting) | 10-20 mg PRN | PO/SL | Reserved for hypertensive urgency |
| Labetalol | 100-400 mg BID | PO | Pregnancy-safe alternative |
| Hydralazine | 10-50 mg TID | PO | Third-line, reflex tachycardia |

### 3.2 Long-Term
ACEi/ARB for persistent proteinuria >3 months.

---

## 4. Diuretics

### 4.1 Furosemide
- **Dose:** 20-80 mg IV/PO daily to BID
- **Indication:** Oedema, pulmonary congestion, hypertension with fluid overload
- **Monitor:** Electrolytes, Cr, urine output
- **Risk:** AKI from over-diuresis; hypokalaemia

---

## 5. Antibiotics

### 5.1 Group A Streptococcus
- **Penicillin V:** 250-500 mg PO BID-QID x10 days
- **Amoxicillin:** 50 mg/kg/day PO divided TID x10 days
- **Alternative:** Cephalexin, clindamycin, azithromycin

### 5.2 MRSA
- **Vancomycin:** 15-20 mg/kg IV q8-12h (trough 15-20 mcg/mL)
- **Daptomycin:** 6 mg/kg IV daily

### 5.3 Endocarditis-associated
- **Penicillin G:** 4 million U IV q4h + gentamicin (synergy)
- **Vancomycin:** For MRSA prosthetic valve endocarditis

---

## 6. Corticosteroids

### 6.1 Indication
Crescentic GN >50% or RPGN. NOT for typical childhood PSGN.

| Formulation | Dose | Route | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|
| Methylprednisolone | 10-30 mg/kg/day (max 1 g) | IV | 3 days (pulse) |
| Prednisone | 0.5-1 mg/kg/day (max 80 mg) | PO | Taper over 3-6 months |

---

## 7. Cyclophosphamide

### 7.1 Indication
RPGN with crescents >50% not responding to steroids alone.

| Regimen | Dose | Route | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| IV pulse | 500-750 mg/m2 | IV | Monthly x3-6 |

### 7.2 Adverse Effects
Bone marrow suppression, haemorrhagic cystitis, gonadal toxicity, malignancy.

---

## 8. MMF

### 8.1 Indication
Persistent proteinuria despite supportive care; steroid-sparing in refractory cases.

**Dose:** 1-2 g/day PO divided BID for 6-12 months.

---

## 9. ACEi/ARB

### 9.1 Indication
Persistent proteinuria >0.5 g/day beyond 3 months.

| Drug | Starting Dose | Target Dose |
|---|---|---|
| Lisinopril | 5-10 mg daily | 20-40 mg daily |
| Losartan | 25-50 mg daily | 100 mg daily |

---

## 10. Plasma Exchange

| Setting | Volume | Sessions |
|---|---|---|
| Dialysis-dependent RPGN | 1.5 plasma volume | 5-7 |
| Pulmonary haemorrhage | 1.5 plasma volume | 5-7+ |

---

## 11. Vaccination

| Vaccine | Timing |
|---|---|
| Pneumococcal (PCV20) | After recovery, if persistent CKD |
| Influenza | Annual |
| Meningococcal | If complement deficiency identified |
