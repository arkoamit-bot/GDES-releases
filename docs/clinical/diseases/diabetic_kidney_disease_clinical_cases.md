## 1. Advanced DKD with Macroalbuminuria and Declining eGFR

### 1.1 Presentation

A 65-year-old Hispanic woman with 15-year history of type 2 DM (insulin, HbA1c 9.2%), hypertension, and CAD presents with peripheral edema, foamy urine, and fatigue. ACR 850 mg/g. eGFR 38.

### 1.2 Examination

BP 156/92 mmHg. BMI 34. Bilateral 2+ pitting edema to knees. Fundoscopy: severe NPDR with macular edema. JVP elevated.

### 1.3 Diagnosis

Diabetic Kidney Disease — Stage G3bA3 (advanced DKD with macroalbuminuria)

### 1.4 Management

Losartan 100 mg (switched from lisinopril). Empagliflozin 10 mg. Finerenone 10 mg (K+ 4.8 OK). Furosemide 40 mg for edema. Insulin intensified (basal-bolus). Atorvastatin 80 mg maintained.

### 1.5 Outcome

Month 1: K+ 5.3 (finerenone held, restarted at 10 mg when K+ 5.0). Month 3: BP 132/78, ACR 520, eGFR 36, edema improved. Month 6: ACR 380, eGFR 34. Month 12: ACR 290, eGFR 32. DKD progression slowed.

### 1.6 Teaching Points

1. See text
2. Key diagnostic features: Diabetic Kidney Disease — Stage G3bA3 (advanced DKD with macroalbuminuria)
3. Treatment based on CKD stage and albuminuria level

## 2. DKD with Heart Failure and Hyperkalemia

### 2.1 Presentation

A 72-year-old man with type 2 DM 18 years, DKD (eGFR 28, ACR 560), HFrEF (EF 35%), and CAD on lisinopril 20 mg, empagliflozin 10 mg, spironolactone 25 mg, furosemide 80 mg, and metformin. Presents with K+ 6.2 and fatigue.

### 2.2 Examination

BP 100/60. HR 95. JVP 10 cm. Bilateral crackles. 2+ edema. Cold peripheries.

### 2.3 Diagnosis

DKD G4A3 with acute hyperkalemia, HFrEF, and cardiorenal syndrome

### 2.4 Management

IV calcium gluconate + insulin/glucose + bicarbonate for K+ 6.2. Spironolactone held. Lisinopril reduced to 10 mg. Furosemide IV 80 mg. Dialysis not needed acutely. Lifestyle: low-K+ diet.

### 2.5 Outcome

Day 1: K+ 5.0 after treatment. Week 1: K+ 4.8, eGFR 24. Month 1: K+ 4.6, eGFR 25. Spironolactone discontinued permanently. Lisinopril resumed at 10 mg. Empagliflozin continued. HF managed by cardiology.

### 2.6 Teaching Points

1. See text
2. Key diagnostic features: DKD G4A3 with acute hyperkalemia, HFrEF, and cardiorenal syndrome
3. Treatment based on CKD stage and albuminuria level

## 3. Rapidly Declining eGFR in DKD with Hyperkalemia

### 3.1 Presentation

A 70-year-old Caucasian man with type 2 DM 20 years, DKD (baseline eGFR 44, ACR 220), and HFpEF on lisinopril 40 mg, empagliflozin 10 mg, spironolactone 25 mg, and furosemide 40 mg. Presents with eGFR drop from 44 to 29 over 3 months.

### 3.2 Examination

BP 108/64. HR 100. Mild edema. JVP 8 cm. Lungs clear.

### 3.3 Diagnosis

DKD G4A3 with rapid eGFR decline due to hyperkalemia and polypharmacy

### 3.4 Management

Spironolactone discontinued. Lisinopril reduced to 20 mg. Furosemide increased to 80 mg BID. Kayexalate for K+ 5.8. Finerenone not started (K+ too high). Empagliflozin continued. Insulin intensified.

### 3.5 Outcome

Week 2: K+ 5.0, eGFR 32. Month 1: K+ 4.6, eGFR 33, BP 124/76. Month 3: eGFR 31, ACR 280. eGFR stabilised after polypharmacy adjustment.

### 3.6 Teaching Points

1. See text
2. Key diagnostic features: DKD G4A3 with rapid eGFR decline due to hyperkalemia and polypharmacy
3. Treatment based on CKD stage and albuminuria level

## 4. DKD Remission with Triple Therapy

### 4.1 Presentation

A 55-year-old Asian woman with type 2 DM 12 years, DKD (eGFR 60, ACR 320) started on ramipril 10 mg, dapagliflozin 10 mg, finerenone 20 mg, and semaglutide 1 mg/week 2 years ago. Now returns for annual review.

### 4.2 Examination

BP 118/72. BMI 26 (lost 12 kg). Fundoscopy: stable NPDR. No edema.

### 4.3 Diagnosis

DKD G2A1 — complete remission of albuminuria with triple therapy + GLP-1 RA

### 4.4 Management

Continues ramipril 10 mg, dapagliflozin 10 mg, finerenone 20 mg, semaglutide 1 mg/week. Atorvastatin 20 mg. Lifestyle maintained.

### 4.5 Outcome

Sustained ACR <30 for 24 months. eGFR stable 60-65. HbA1c 6.0-6.5%. No CV events. No hyperkalemia. Albuminuria regression is associated with excellent long-term prognosis.

### 4.6 Teaching Points

1. See text
2. Key diagnostic features: DKD G2A1 — complete remission of albuminuria with triple therapy + GLP-1 RA
3. Treatment based on CKD stage and albuminuria level

## 5. Long-Term DKD Follow-Up After Kidney Transplant

### 5.1 Presentation

A 60-year-old man with type 2 DM 30 years, DKD causing ESKD at age 55, received a deceased donor kidney transplant 5 years ago. Now on tacrolimus, mycophenolate, prednisone 5 mg. Has developed post-transplant diabetes again (HbA1c 7.6%).

### 5.2 Examination

BP 132/78. No edema. Graft non-tender. Fundoscopy: stable NPDR. No palpable purpura.

### 5.3 Diagnosis

Recurrent Diabetic Kidney Disease in Renal Allograft (Stage G3bA2)

### 5.4 Management

Empagliflozin 10 mg (monitor for UTI and volume). Ramipril 2.5 mg. Insulin optimization. Tacrolimus dose reduced (target 5-7). Atorvastatin 40 mg continued.

### 5.5 Outcome

Month 3: ACR 60, eGFR 48. Month 6: ACR 42, HbA1c 7.0%, eGFR 47. No rejection. Graft function stable. DKD recurrence mild and controlled.

### 5.6 Teaching Points

1. See text
2. Key diagnostic features: Recurrent Diabetic Kidney Disease in Renal Allograft (Stage G3bA2)
3. Treatment based on CKD stage and albuminuria level

## 6. DKD with Superimposed IgA Nephropathy

### 6.1 Presentation

A 45-year-old man with type 2 DM 10 years (HbA1c 7.5%, metformin) and known DKD (ACR 150, eGFR 55) presents with sudden macroscopic hematuria during upper respiratory infection. Previously ACR was stable on losartan 50 mg.

### 6.2 Examination

BP 142/88. No edema. Fundoscopy: mild NPDR. Throat: mild erythema.

### 6.3 Diagnosis

Diabetic Kidney Disease with Superimposed IgA Nephropathy

### 6.4 Management

Losartan increased to 100 mg. Dapagliflozin 10 mg started. Prednisone 0.8 mg/kg/day x8 weeks then taper. Supportive care for IgAN. Metformin continued.

### 6.5 Outcome

Month 3: hematuria resolved, ACR 320, eGFR 50. Month 6: ACR 180, eGFR 52. Month 12: ACR 85, eGFR 52. Prednisone tapered off. Now managed as DKD + IgAN in remission.

### 6.6 Teaching Points

1. See text
2. Key diagnostic features: Diabetic Kidney Disease with Superimposed IgA Nephropathy
3. Treatment based on CKD stage and albuminuria level

## 7. Early DKD with Microalbuminuria in Type 2 DM

### 7.1 Presentation

A 58-year-old African American man with 8-year history of type 2 DM (metformin 1000 mg BID, HbA1c 7.8%) and hypertension (lisinopril 10 mg) presents for routine follow-up. Screening ACR shows 65 mg/g. No edema. No visual symptoms.

### 7.2 Examination

BP 138/86 mmHg. BMI 32. Fundoscopy: background diabetic retinopathy (mild NPDR). No peripheral edema. Feet: normal sensation.

### 7.3 Diagnosis

Diabetic Kidney Disease — Stage G2A2 (CKD Stage 2 with microalbuminuria)

### 7.4 Management

Lisinopril increased to 20 mg daily. Dapagliflozin 10 mg added. Atorvastatin 40 mg started. Metformin continued. Lifestyle counseling: low-sodium DASH diet, 30 min walking daily.

### 7.5 Outcome

Month 3: BP 126/78, ACR 45, HbA1c 7.2%. Month 6: ACR 28 (regression to A1), eGFR 76, weight -4 kg. Month 12: sustained ACR <30, HbA1c 6.9%, eGFR stable at 75.

### 7.6 Teaching Points

1. See text
2. Key diagnostic features: Diabetic Kidney Disease — Stage G2A2 (CKD Stage 2 with microalbuminuria)
3. Treatment based on CKD stage and albuminuria level

## 8. Type 1 DM with DKD Diagnosed After 15 Years

### 8.1 Presentation

A 28-year-old woman with type 1 DM since age 13 (insulin pump, HbA1c 8.5%) presents for routine screening. She has had diabetes for 15 years. First ACR: 120 mg/g. eGFR 82. She has diabetic retinopathy (proliferative).

### 8.2 Examination

BP 128/80. Fundoscopy: proliferative diabetic retinopathy (post-laser). No edema. Feet: normal monofilament.

### 8.3 Diagnosis

Diabetic Kidney Disease — Stage G2A2 in type 1 DM

### 8.4 Management

Enalapril 5 mg (titrated to 20 mg). Empagliflozin 10 mg (off-label for type 1, risk of DKA — educate). Insulin pump optimization for tighter control. Atorvastatin 20 mg.

### 8.5 Outcome

Month 3: ACR 85, BP 122/76, eGFR 80. Month 6: ACR 55, eGFR 78. Month 12: ACR 32 (nearly normalised), eGFR 78. DKD progression slowed with RAASi + SGLT2i.

### 8.6 Teaching Points

1. See text
2. Key diagnostic features: Diabetic Kidney Disease — Stage G2A2 in type 1 DM
3. Treatment based on CKD stage and albuminuria level
