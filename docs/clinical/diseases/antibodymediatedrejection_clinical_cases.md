### CASE-AMR-HLA-001: ABMR from Non-HLA Antibodies (AT1R)

**Type:** atypical
**Source:** Non-HLA ABMR

**History:**
A 42-year-old woman with kidney transplant 6 months ago. Rising Cr (1.0 to 2.4). No HLA DSA detected. Suspicion: non-HLA antibody-mediated rejection. AT1R antibody testing sent.

**Examination:**
BP 168/100 (severe hypertension — clincher for AT1R), mild oedema, graft non-tender.

**Laboratory Data:**

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 26 mL/min |
| Cr | 2.4 mg/dL |
| UPCR | 1.2 g/g |
| HLA DSA | Negative (all class I and class II) |
| AT1R antibody | >40 U/mL (strongly positive) |
| ETAR antibody | Positive |
| Renin | High |
| Aldosterone | High |

**Biopsy Data:**

| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | g2, ptc2, v1 — microvascular inflammation with arteritis |
| IF | C4d2 |
| EM | Endothelial injury, subendothelial oedema, no deposits |

**Diagnosis:**
Non-HLA ABMR (Anti-AT1R Antibody-Mediated)

**Treatment:**
Losartan 50 mg (AT1R blockade — specific therapy). Methylprednisolone 500 mg IV x3. IVIG 2 g/kg. PLEX x3 (removes AT1R antibodies).

**Outcome:**
Week 2: BP 135/85, Cr 1.6. Month 1: Cr 1.2. AT1R antibody 18 U/mL. Month 3: Cr 1.1, AT1R 12 U/mL. Excellent response to losartan + PLEX/IVIG. AT1R-specific therapy key.

**Differential Diagnosis:**

| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| cniToxicity | 0.1 | Microvascular inflammation (g+ptc >0) and AT1R antibodies = AT1R-mediated ABMR. CNI toxicity has arteriolar hyalinosis, not microvascular inflammation |

### CASE-AMR-C4D-001: C4d-Negative ABMR Detected on Surveillance Biopsy

**Type:** atypical
**Source:** C4d-negative ABMR

**History:**
A 55-year-old woman with kidney transplant (deceased donor, no pre-formed DSA). Stable creatinine 1.0 at 6 months. Protocol biopsy reveals microvascular inflammation despite normal Cr. DSA testing sent.

**Examination:**
BP 130/78, no oedema, graft non-tender.

**Laboratory Data:**

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 62 mL/min |
| Cr | 1.0 mg/dL |
| UPCR | 0.15 g/g |
| DSA | De novo anti-DQ MFI 1800 (low-level) |
| Tacrolimus trough | 7.1 ng/mL |
| BK PCR | Negative |

**Biopsy Data:**

| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | g1, ptc1, no tubulitis, no IFTA |
| IF | C4d0 (negative) |
| EM | Mild endothelial changes, no GBM duplication, no PTCML |

**Diagnosis:**
C4d-Negative Active ABMR (subclinical) — Banff Category 1 but C4d-negative

**Treatment:**
Optimise tacrolimus to 7-8 ng/mL. IVIG 2 g/kg x1. No pulse steroids (low grade). Increased surveillance.

**Outcome:**
Month 3: DSA MFI 500 (cleared). Month 12: repeat protocol biopsy — g0, ptc0, C4d0. Stable Cr 1.0. Illustrates detection of subclinical C4d-negative ABMR by protocol biopsy; early treatment prevents progression.

**Differential Diagnosis:**

| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| tCellMediatedRejection | 0.1 | No tubulitis, C4d negative, but DSA+ and g+ptc >0 = C4d-negative ABMR pattern |

### CASE-AMR-MIX-001: Mixed ABMR + TCMR Rejection

**Type:** atypical
**Source:** Mixed ABMR/TCMR

**History:**
A 38-year-old man with transplant 2 years ago presents with acute Cr rise (1.4 to 3.6 over 1 week) after stopping tacrolimus for 10 days (ran out of medication). Oliguria. Graft tenderness. Fever (38.5C).

**Examination:**
BP 146/88, graft tender and swollen, temperature 38.5C, oliguria.

**Laboratory Data:**

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 18 mL/min |
| Cr | 3.6 mg/dL |
| DSA | De novo anti-DQ MFI 3800, C1q-negative |
| Tacrolimus trough | 1.8 ng/mL (very low) |
| BK PCR | Negative |
| CMV PCR | Negative |

**Biopsy Data:**

| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | g2, ptc2, v1 (intimal arteritis), t2, i2 — both microvascular inflammation and tubulitis/interstitial infiltrate |
| IF | C4d2 (focal) |
| EM | Endothelial swelling, mild GBM changes |

**Diagnosis:**
Mixed ABMR + TCMR (Banff Categories 1 + Type IIA)

**Treatment:**
Methylprednisolone 500 mg IV x3. IVIG 2 g/kg. PLEX x5. Rituximab 375 mg/m2 x1. Restore tacrolimus (target 8-10). MMF 1 g BID.

**Outcome:**
Week 2: Cr 2.2. Week 4: Cr 1.6. DSA MFI 1200. Month 3: biopsy — g1, ptc0, t0, C4d1. Resolution of both rejection components. Essential to treat both.

**Differential Diagnosis:**

| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| tCellMediatedRejection | 0.3 | TCMR alone would not have microvascular inflammation (g>0, ptc>0) or C4d/DSA — mixed rejection requires treating both components |

### CASE-AMR-HYP-001: Hyperacute ABMR (Pre-Transplant)

**Type:** complications
**Source:** Hyperacute ABMR

**History:**
A 60-year-old man receiving a living-donor kidney from his wife. Crossmatch at transplant is retrospectively positive (B-cell FCXM positive, DSA MFI >10,000). Minutes after reperfusion: the graft becomes mottled, blue, and flaccid. No urine output.

**Examination:**
Intra-operative: graft cyanosis, poor turgor, no urine. BP stable.

**Laboratory Data:**

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| Pre-op DSA | Not available (urgent transplant, crossmatch not resulted) |
| Retrospective DSA | Anti-HLA-A2 MFI 12,000, C1q-positive |
| Post-op Cr | Not clearing |
| UOP | 0 mL |

**Biopsy Data:**

| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | Diffuse glomerular and peritubular capillary microthrombi, neutrophil margination, cortical necrosis |
| IF | C4d3 (diffuse, bright). IgG 1+ |
| EM | Endothelial destruction, platelet aggregates, fibrin thrombi |

**Diagnosis:**
Hyperacute ABMR — pre-formed high-titre complement-fixing DSA (anti-HLA-A2)

**Treatment:**
Exploratory laparotomy confirms graft necrosis. Transplant nephrectomy performed. Patient returned to dialysis.

**Outcome:**
Graft loss within hours. Return to dialysis. Counselling: DSA persists — future transplant requires desensitisation and strict DSA avoidance. Illustrates catastrophic outcome of unrecognised high-titre DSA.

**Differential Diagnosis:**

| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| acute arterial thrombosis | 0.2 | Diffuse microthrombi + C4d3 + DSA = hyperacute ABMR, not surgical thrombosis. Surgical thrombosis would affect main vessels |

### CASE-AMR-RET-001: Refractory ABMR Treated with Bortezomib

**Type:** complications
**Source:** Refractory ABMR

**History:**
A 48-year-old woman (3rd transplant) with high MFI DSA (anti-DQ, MFI 9500, C1q-positive). Received PLEX/IVIG/Rituximab but creatinine rising and DSA persisting. Refractory ABMR.

**Examination:**
BP 144/86, mild oedema, graft non-tender.

**Laboratory Data:**

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 25 mL/min |
| Cr | 2.6 mg/dL |
| DSA | anti-DQ MFI 9500 (persistent despite PLEX/IVIG/Rituximab), C1q-positive |
| Tacrolimus trough | 7.5 ng/mL |
| BK PCR | Negative |

**Biopsy Data:**

| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | g3, ptc3, v1 — severe microvascular inflammation, IFTA 15% |
| IF | C4d3 |
| EM | Endothelial injury, GBM duplication starting, no PTCML |

**Diagnosis:**
Refractory Acute ABMR — failed PLEX/IVIG/Rituximab

**Treatment:**
Bortezomib 1.3 mg/m2 SC x4 (Days 1, 4, 8, 11). Continue PLEX after each dose (clears newly released DSA). Monitor neuropathy.

**Outcome:**
Week 6: DSA MFI 3200. Week 12: biopsy — g1, ptc1, C4d1. Cr 1.8. Partial response. Bortezomib tolerated. Illustrates proteasome inhibitor rescue therapy.

**Differential Diagnosis:**

| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| acute tubular necrosis | 0.1 | Biopsy shows g3/ptc3/C4d3 = ABMR, not ATN. ATN would have tubular injury but no microvascular inflammation |

### CASE-AMR-ACU-001: Acute ABMR in Pre-Sensitised Recipient

**Type:** typical
**Source:** Acute ABMR

**History:**
A 45-year-old woman with ESKD from lupus nephritis receives a deceased-donor kidney transplant. She has pre-formed DSA (anti-DQ, MFI 4200, C1q-negative). Day 7: creatinine rises from 1.0 to 2.8. Oliguria. No fever. BP 148/90.

**Examination:**
BP 148/90, graft tender on palpation, urine output <500 mL/day, mild pedal oedema.

**Laboratory Data:**

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 22 mL/min |
| Cr | 2.8 mg/dL |
| DSA | anti-DQ MFI 6200 (rising from 4200) |
| C1q | Negative |
| Tacrolimus trough | 6.2 ng/mL |
| BK PCR | Negative |
| CMV PCR | Negative |
| Urinalysis | No WBC casts, no eosinophils |

**Biopsy Data:**

| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | g2, ptc2, v0 — active microvascular inflammation. No tubulitis (t0). No interstitial infiltrate |
| IF | C4d3 (bright linear in all PTC) |
| EM | Endothelial swelling, early GBM changes. No PTCML |

**Diagnosis:**
Acute Active ABMR (Banff Category 1) — pre-formed anti-DQ DSA

**Treatment:**
Methylprednisolone 500 mg IV x3. IVIG 2 g/kg (40 g x2 days). PLEX x5 sessions every other day. Optimise tacrolimus (target 7-8). MMF 1 g BID.

**Outcome:**
Week 2: Cr 1.5. Week 4: Cr 1.1 (baseline). DSA MFI 2500. Month 3: repeat biopsy — g1, ptc0, C4d1. Resolution. Tac 6.5, MMF 1g BID. Good response to PLEX/IVIG/steroids. Illustrates importance of DSA monitoring and early treatment.

**Differential Diagnosis:**

| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| tCellMediatedRejection | 0.2 | No tubulitis (t0), C4d3 with DSA = ABMR not TCMR. TCMR would show t>1 and i>1 |

### CASE-AMR-CHR-001: Chronic ABMR from De Novo DSA (Non-Adherence)

**Type:** typical
**Source:** Chronic ABMR

**History:**
A 30-year-old man with kidney transplant 4 years ago (non-adherent to tacrolimus — level <4 for 12+ months). Gradual creatinine rise (1.0 to 2.2 over 2 years). New proteinuria (UPCR 1.8). Now eGFR 35.

**Examination:**
BP 152/92, mild oedema, no graft tenderness.

**Laboratory Data:**

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 35 mL/min |
| Cr | 2.2 mg/dL |
| UPCR | 1.8 g/g |
| DSA | De novo anti-DQ MFI 8500, C1q-positive |
| Tacrolimus trough | 3.2 ng/mL (sub-therapeutic) |
| BK PCR | Negative |

**Biopsy Data:**

| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | cg2 (GBM double contours in 50% glomeruli), ptc1, g1, cv2, IFTA 30% |
| IF | C4d1 (focal, weak) |
| EM | GBM double contours, PTCML 5-7 layers, endothelial swelling |

**Diagnosis:**
Chronic Active ABMR (Banff Category 2) — de novo anti-DQ, non-adherence

**Treatment:**
Restore tacrolimus target 5-8 ng/mL. IVIG 2 g/kg monthly x6. Ramipril 10 mg for proteinuria. Amlodipine 5 mg for BP. Adherence counselling. MEMS caps.

**Outcome:**
Month 6: Cr 2.0, UPCR 1.2. DSA MFI 6000. Month 12: Cr 2.1, UPCR 1.0. No reversal but slowed. Illustrates late presentation, limited reversibility, and role of non-adherence.

**Differential Diagnosis:**

| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| transplantGlomerulopathy | 0.2 | Transplant glomerulopathy = the pathologic lesion of chronic ABMR. DSA+ and microvascular inflammation confirm ABMR as the cause |

### CASE-AMR-CHR2-001: Chronic ABMR with Transplant Glomerulopathy

**Type:** typical
**Source:** Chronic ABMR with cg

**History:**
A 50-year-old man 8 years post-transplant with slowly rising Cr (1.0 to 2.8 over 4 years), proteinuria (UPCR 2.5), and new hypertension. DSA positive (anti-DR, MFI 2200). Adherent to medications.

**Examination:**
BP 156/94, 1+ oedema, graft non-tender.

**Laboratory Data:**

| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 24 mL/min |
| Cr | 2.8 mg/dL |
| UPCR | 2.5 g/g |
| DSA | Anti-DR MFI 2200 (de novo), C1q-negative |
| Tacrolimus trough | 5.8 ng/mL |
| BK PCR | Negative |
| Albumin | 3.2 g/dL |

**Biopsy Data:**

| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | cg3 (severe GBM duplication 80%), ptc1, g1, cv3, IFTA 40% |
| IF | C4d1 (focal weak) |
| EM | GBM double contours, PTCML 8-10 layers, endothelial loss |

**Diagnosis:**
Chronic ABMR with Severe Transplant Glomerulopathy — advanced chronic injury

**Treatment:**
Optimise tacrolimus to 6-8 ng/mL. Ramipril 10 mg. Amlodipine 5 mg. IVIG 2 g/kg monthly x3. No PLEX/rituximab (chronic phase).

**Outcome:**
Month 12: Cr 3.2 (slowly rising), UPCR 2.0. Graft survival 3 more years. Illustrates progressive chronic ABMR despite therapy — irreversible structural damage.

**Differential Diagnosis:**

| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| recurrentGN | 0.2 | DSA+ and microvascular inflammation with cg = chronic ABMR, not recurrent GN. IF negative for immune complexes |
