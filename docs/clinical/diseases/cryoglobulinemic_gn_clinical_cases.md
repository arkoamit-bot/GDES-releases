## 1. Non-HCV Cryoglobulinemic GN Secondary to Sjogren Syndrome

### 1.1 Presentation

A 45-year-old woman with known Sjogren syndrome (dry eyes, dry mouth, positive anti-SSA/Ro) presents with new purpura, Raynaud phenomenon, and proteinuria found on routine labs. No HCV risk factors.

### 1.2 Examination

BP 134/82 mmHg. Sparse purpura on lower legs. Dry oral mucosa. No synovitis.

### 1.3 Diagnosis

Non-HCV Type III cryoglobulinemic GN secondary to Sjogren syndrome

### 1.4 Management

HCQ 400 mg daily continued. Prednisone 30 mg daily taper. Mycophenolate mofetil 1 g BID. Artificial tears and oral care for sicca symptoms.

### 1.5 Outcome

Month 3: UPCR 0.8, purpura resolved, C4 improving. Month 6: UPCR 0.3, C4 normalised, prednisone tapered off. Month 12: sustained remission on MMF 1.5 g/day.

### 1.6 Teaching Points

1. See text
2. Key diagnostic features: Non-HCV Type III cryoglobulinemic GN secondary to Sjogren syndrome
3. Treatment based on severity and aetiology

## 2. CryoGN Progressing to B-Cell Lymphoma

### 2.1 Presentation

A 58-year-old woman with 12-year history of Type II mixed cryoglobulinemia and CryoGN previously treated with rituximab (x3 courses) now presents with weight loss, night sweats, and lymphadenopathy. CryoGN has been worsening.

### 2.2 Examination

BP 136/84 mmHg. Palpable lymph nodes (cervical, axillary, inguinal). Splenomegaly. Mild purpura. Neuropathy in feet.

### 2.3 Diagnosis

Type II cryoglobulinemia complicated by DLBCL transformation with CryoGN

### 2.4 Management

R-CHOP chemotherapy (rituximab, cyclophosphamide, doxorubicin, vincristine, prednisone) x6 cycles. DAAs for HCV.

### 2.5 Outcome

Month 3: PET partial response, C4 improving. Month 6: Complete metabolic response (PET negative), cryoglobulins negative, UPCR 0.6, Cr 1.5. Month 12: sustained remission of lymphoma, CryoGN in remission.

### 2.6 Teaching Points

1. See text
2. Key diagnostic features: Type II cryoglobulinemia complicated by DLBCL transformation with CryoGN
3. Treatment based on severity and aetiology

## 3. Cryoglobulinemic GN Presenting as RPGN with Dialysis

### 3.1 Presentation

A 60-year-old man with known HCV cirrhosis (Child-Pugh A) presents with 1-week oliguria, rapidly rising Cr from 1.5 to 5.0, palpable purpura, and acute onset foot drop (mononeuritis multiplex). He has not received DAA therapy.

### 3.2 Examination

BP 158/94 mmHg. Widespread palpable purpura (legs, trunk). Bilateral pitting oedema to thighs. Left foot drop. Crackles at lung bases.

### 3.3 Diagnosis

Cryoglobulinemic GN with RPGN, 45% crescents, severe systemic vasculitis, HCV cirrhosis

### 3.4 Management

Haemodialysis initiated. Methylprednisolone 500 mg IV x3. Rituximab 375 mg/m2 weekly x4. Plasma exchange 1.5 vol x5. Prednisone 60 mg daily taper. Sofosbuvir/velpatasvir started after clinical stabilisation at week 2.

### 3.5 Outcome

Week 2: dialysis discontinued, Cr 3.5. Month 1: Cr 2.8, C4 improving. Month 3: Cr 2.0, UPCR 1.5, HCV RNA undetectable. Month 6: Cr 1.6 (eGFR 42), UPCR 0.6, C4 normal, foot drop improving. Month 12: CKD stage 3b, SVR12, no further vasculitis.

### 3.6 Teaching Points

1. See text
2. Key diagnostic features: Cryoglobulinemic GN with RPGN, 45% crescents, severe systemic vasculitis, HCV cirrhosis
3. Treatment based on severity and aetiology

## 4. Relapsing Cryoglobulinemic GN after DAA Failure

### 4.1 Presentation

A 55-year-old man with HCV genotype 3 and cirrhosis developed CryoGN 2 years ago, treated with sofosbuvir/velpatasvir 12 weeks. SVR12 was achieved at 6 months. Now presents with recurrent purpura, arthralgia, and rising Cr. HCV RNA detectable (reinfection from ongoing IVDU).

### 4.2 Examination

BP 142/86 mmHg. New purpura on legs. Mild oedema. Stigmata of chronic liver disease.

### 4.3 Diagnosis

Relapsed CryoGN due to HCV reinfection after successful DAA treatment

### 4.4 Management

Retreatment: glecaprevir/pibrentasvir 16 weeks (pangenotypic, Mavyret). Rituximab 375 mg/m2 weekly x4. Prednisone taper. Addiction medicine referral.

### 4.5 Outcome

Month 1: HCV RNA 200. Month 3: HCV undetectable, C4 improving, UPCR 1.5. Month 6: SVR12, C4 normal, UPCR 0.5, Cr 1.8. Ongoing addiction treatment.

### 4.6 Teaching Points

1. See text
2. Key diagnostic features: Relapsed CryoGN due to HCV reinfection after successful DAA treatment
3. Treatment based on severity and aetiology

## 5. Long-Term Remission After DAA-Treated CryoGN

### 5.1 Presentation

A 48-year-old woman now 5 years after treatment for HCV-CryoGN. She received glecaprevir/pibrentasvir x8 weeks and rituximab x4 doses for moderate disease (Cr 2.0, UPCR 3.5). She achieved SVR12 and renal remission.

### 5.2 Examination

BP 124/78 mmHg (on lisinopril 10 mg). No purpura, oedema, or neuropathy. Normal sensation.

### 5.3 Diagnosis

Long-term remission of CryoGN after DAA + rituximab therapy

### 5.4 Management

Lisinopril 10 mg daily. No immunosuppression. Annual surveillance.

### 5.5 Outcome

Stable Cr 0.9-1.1, UPCR <0.2, C4 normal, cryoglobulins negative over 5 years. SVR durable. No lymphoma on annual surveillance. Discharged to primary care with annual renal check.

### 5.6 Teaching Points

1. See text
2. Key diagnostic features: Long-term remission of CryoGN after DAA + rituximab therapy
3. Treatment based on severity and aetiology

## 6. Paediatric CryoGN from HCV Vertical Transmission

### 6.1 Presentation

A 14-year-old boy with vertically-acquired HCV (treatment-naive) presents with 2-week history of leg purpura, knee pain, and foamy urine. Mother had HCV. No other medical history.

### 6.2 Examination

BP 132/80 mmHg. Palpable purpura on lower legs. Mild bilateral knee effusion. No oedema.

### 6.3 Diagnosis

Paediatric CryoGN secondary to vertically-acquired chronic HCV

### 6.4 Management

Sofosbuvir/ledipasvir 12 weeks (weight-based). No immunosuppression (mild disease). Close monitoring.

### 6.5 Outcome

Month 1: HCV RNA declining. Month 3: HCV undetectable, C4 improving, UPCR 1.0, purpura resolved. Month 6: SVR12 confirmed, C4 normal, UPCR 0.2, Cr normal. Month 12: fully recovered.

### 6.6 Teaching Points

1. See text
2. Key diagnostic features: Paediatric CryoGN secondary to vertically-acquired chronic HCV

## 7. Type I Cryoglobulinemic GN from MGUS

### 7.1 Presentation

A 68-year-old man with known IgG kappa MGUS presents with 2-month history of cold-induced purpura, digital cyanosis in cold weather, and progressive leg oedema. No HCV risk factors.

### 7.2 Examination

BP 146/88 mmHg. Purpura on cold-exposed areas. Raynaud phenomenon. Bilateral 1+ oedema. No neuropathy.

### 7.3 Diagnosis

Type I cryoglobulinemic GN secondary to IgG kappa MGUS (smouldering myeloma)

### 7.4 Management

Bortezomib/dexamethasone 4 cycles. Daratumumab added for suboptimal response at cycle 2. Cold avoidance, supportive care.

### 7.5 Outcome

Month 3: M-spike reduced 60%, cryoglobulins negative, UPCR 1.5. Month 6: UPCR 0.6, Cr 1.3, no purpura. Month 12: haematologic VGPR, renal remission.

### 7.6 Teaching Points

1. See text
2. Key diagnostic features: Type I cryoglobulinemic GN secondary to IgG kappa MGUS (smouldering myeloma)

## 8. HCV-Associated Mixed Cryoglobulinemic GN with Purpura

### 8.1 Presentation

A 52-year-old woman with chronic HCV (genotype 1b, untreated) presents with 4-week history of recurrent palpable purpura on lower extremities, arthralgia of hands and knees, and 2-week history of foamy urine and periorbital oedema. She reports fatigue and mild paraesthesia in feet.

### 8.2 Examination

BP 148/92 mmHg. Palpable purpura on both lower legs (non-blanching). 1+ bilateral pitting oedema. Mild hepatomegaly. Decreased pinprick sensation in stocking distribution.

### 8.3 Diagnosis

HCV-associated Type II mixed cryoglobulinemic GN (CryoGN) with systemic vasculitis (purpura, neuropathy, arthralgia)

### 8.4 Management

Glecaprevir/pibrentasvir 8 weeks. Rituximab 375 mg/m2 IV weekly x4. Methylprednisolone 500 mg IV x3 then prednisone 40 mg daily taper. Lisinopril 10 mg daily. PCP prophylaxis.

### 8.5 Outcome

Month 3: HCV RNA undetectable, C4 8 mg/dL, UPCR 1.2, cryoglobulins reduced. Month 6: C4 14 mg/dL, UPCR 0.4, cryocrit 1%, RF 1:160, purpura resolved. Month 12: SVR12 confirmed, C4 normal, UPCR 0.2, Cr 1.2 (eGFR 52). Remission maintained at 24 months.

### 8.6 Teaching Points

1. See text
2. Key diagnostic features: HCV-associated Type II mixed cryoglobulinemic GN (CryoGN) with systemic vasculitis (purpura, neuropathy, arthralgia)
3. Treatment based on severity and aetiology
