**Classification**: Guideline Mapping and Cross-Reference

## 1. Overview

This document maps the major clinical guidelines relevant to anti-GBM disease, identifies areas of concordance and divergence, and provides cross-reference tables for clinical decision-making. Primary guidelines include KDIGO 2021 Chapter 9, KDIGO 2025 update, EULAR/ERA-EDTA 2012, ASFA 2023 guidelines, BSR 2020, and the British Thoracic Society 2015 pulmonary-renal syndrome guidance. Anti-GBM disease is distinguished by the universal concordance across all major guidelines on the core treatment principle: same-day initiation of plasma exchange combined with cyclophosphamide and high-dose corticosteroids.

## 2. KDIGO 2021 Clinical Practice Guideline — Chapter 9: Anti-GBM Disease

### 2.1 Guideline Overview

**Full title**: KDIGO 2021 Clinical Practice Guideline for the Management of Glomerular Diseases  
**Published**: Kidney International, 2021  
**Volume/Issue**: 100(4S), S1-S276  
**Chapter 9**: Anti-GBM Disease (pages S199-S216)

### 2.2 Key Recommendations (KDIGO 2021 Ch 9)

| Recommendation | Grade | Evidence Level |
|----------------|-------|---------------|
| Plasma exchange should be initiated promptly (same day if possible) for all patients with anti-GBM GN | 1C | Low (cohort evidence) |
| Cyclophosphamide and corticosteroids should be initiated with PLEX | 1C | Low (cohort evidence) |
| Methylprednisolone 500-1000 mg IV daily x 3 at presentation | 1C | Low |
| Oral prednisone 1 mg/kg/day with gradual taper after pulse | 1C | Low |
| Oral cyclophosphamide 2-3 mg/kg/day (adjusted for WBC) for 2-3 months | 1B | Moderate |
| Switch cyclophosphamide to azathioprine 1-2 mg/kg/day after 2-3 months | 2C | Low |
| Continue immunosuppression for 6-12 months after PLEX | 2C | Low |
| Withdraw immunosuppression after 12-18 months if anti-GBM negative and in remission | 2C | Low |
| Do not treat patients with dialysis dependence and 100% crescents or >50% fibrous crescents (poor risk-benefit) | 2B | Moderate |
| Renal transplantation after anti-GBM negative for 6-12 months | 1C | Low |

### 2.3 Key Recommendations — Detailed Mapping

#### Diagnosis

| KDIGO Recommendation | Evidence | Our Implementation |
|----------------------|----------|-------------------|
| Prompt diagnosis with anti-GBM serology and urgent renal biopsy | 1C | Stage 1: Same-day serology + stat biopsy |
| Linear IgG on IF is diagnostic gold standard | Not graded | Disease knowledge: pathognomonic feature |
| ANCA testing mandatory to rule out double-positive disease | 1C | Stage 1: Stat ANCA serology |
| HLA typing not recommended for diagnosis | 2C | Not in pathway; in disease knowledge for risk |

#### Induction Therapy

| KDIGO Recommendation | Evidence | Our Implementation |
|----------------------|----------|-------------------|
| PLEX initiated same day | 1C | Stage 2: Day 0 PLEX start |
| PLEX daily x 14 days or until antibody negative | 1C | Stage 2: Daily x 14, Ab-guided duration |
| Cyclophosphamide oral 2-3 mg/kg/day | 1B | Stage 2: Start same-day as PLEX |
| Methylprednisolone 500-1000 mg IV x 3 | 1C | Stage 2: Pulse protocol |
| Prednisone 1 mg/kg/day with taper | 1C | Stage 2: Taper over 6-12 months |

#### Maintenance and Follow-Up

| KDIGO Recommendation | Evidence | Our Implementation |
|----------------------|----------|-------------------|
| Switch CyP to AZA after 2-3 months | 2C | Stage 3-4: Transition to AZA |
| Continue IS for 6-12 months | 2C | Stage 4: AZA + prednisone taper |
| Withdraw IS at 12-18 months if remission | 2C | Stage 5: Withdrawal protocol |
| Monitor anti-GBM titres during follow-up | 2C | Stage 5: q6mo monitoring after withdrawal |

### 2.4 KDIGO 2025 Update

| Recommendation | Key Changes from 2021 |
|----------------|----------------------|
| Rituximab as alternative to CyP (increased evidence from case series) | New alternative accepted |
| PLEX duration guided by anti-GBM antibody titres rather than fixed 14 sessions | Confirmed and emphasised |
| Lower threshold for rituximab in elderly and high-infection risk patients | Age-specific guidance |
| Double-positive disease requires ANCA-type maintenance therapy | Dual-disease management |
| Consider avacopan in double-positive ANCA component | New agent mentioned for ANCA overlap |

## 3. ASFA (American Society for Apheresis) 2023 Guidelines

### 3.1 Guideline Overview

**Full title**: American Society for Apheresis 2023 Clinical Practice Guidelines  
**Published**: Journal of Clinical Apheresis, 2023  
**Volume**: 38(2), pages 77-278

### 3.2 Recommendation for Anti-GBM Disease

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Indication category | Category I (disorder for which apheresis is accepted as first-line therapy) |
| Recommendation grade | 1B (strong recommendation, moderate-quality evidence) |
| Disease severity | Severe (RPGN, dialysis-dependent, DAH) |
| PLEX dose | 1-1.5 plasma volume daily x 14 days or until anti-GBM negative |
| Replacement fluid | 5% albumin; FFP if bleeding risk or post-procedure |
| Special considerations | Start PLEX as soon as diagnosis confirmed; do not delay awaiting biopsy if serology is positive |

### 3.3 ASFA Category Definitions

| Category | Definition | Anti-GBM Status |
|----------|------------|-----------------|
| Category I | Apheresis accepted as first-line therapy, either as primary or as standalone treatment | Yes |
| Category II | Apheresis accepted as second-line therapy, either adjunctive or supportive | No |
| Anti-GBM specific | Strong recommendation based on >50 years of clinical experience | Highest level |

## 8. Major Guideline Divergences

| Topic | Divergence | Clinical Implication |
|-------|------------|----------------------|
| Treat dialysis-dependent patients uniformly | KDIGO more selective (biopsy-dependent); BSR more aggressive | Decision based on biopsy chronicity |
| Rituximab role | Earlier guidelines omit; newer (KDIGO 2025, BSR 2020) accept as alternative | Increasing role, especially in elderly |
| PLEX duration | Fixed 14 days vs titre-guided | Titre-guided is now standard; reduces unnecessary PLEX |
| Steroid dosing in elderly | No specific guidance | Clinical practice: reduce pulse dose (500 mg vs 1000 mg) in >65 |
| Double-positive management | No specific trials; extrapolated from both diseases | Treat both; longer maintenance for ANCA component |

## 9. Evidence-Based Summary

Anti-GBM disease guidelines are remarkable for their uniformity across organisations. The core recommendation of same-day PLEX + CyP + steroids is universally endorsed. No major guideline diverges on this fundamental principle. Differences are limited to rituximab eligibility, duration of PLEX, and thresholds for treating dialysis-dependent patients. The rarity of the disease (<2 per million) precludes randomised trials, and all recommendations are based on cohort studies (evidence level C, grade 1-2). The KDIGO 2025 update reflects the growing role of rituximab and the importance of titre-guided PLEX duration. ASFA's Category I designation underscores that PLEX is the critical, irreplaceable component of therapy for which the strongest evidence exists.
