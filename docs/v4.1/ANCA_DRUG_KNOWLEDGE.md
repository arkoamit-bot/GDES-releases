**Document ID**: ANCA_DR

## 1. Overview

ANCA-associated vasculitis therapy has undergone a paradigm shift from cyclophosphamide-dominant regimens to targeted biologic therapy with rituximab and complement inhibition with avacopan. Corticosteroids remain integral but their role is being minimised through steroid-sparing strategies. This document covers the major therapeutic agents used in AAV with renal involvement: rituximab, cyclophosphamide, plasma exchange, avacopan, glucocorticoids, TMP-SMX prophylaxis, and azathioprine maintenance.

---

## 2. Therapeutic Agent Framework

### 2.1 Drug Categories

| Category | Agents | Role in AAV |
|----------|--------|-------------|
| Biologic (B-cell depleting) | Rituximab | First-line induction and maintenance |
| Alkylating agent | Cyclophosphamide | Alternative induction (resource-limited, special populations) |
| Complement inhibitor (C5aR antagonist) | Avacopan | Steroid-sparing induction |
| Corticosteroids | Methylprednisolone, Prednisone | Baseline therapy (limited by avacopan) |
| Plasma exchange (apheresis) | Not a drug | Severe DAH, double-positive disease |
| Antimetabolite | Azathioprine | Alternative maintenance therapy |
| Antibiotic prophylaxis | TMP-SMX | PJP prophylaxis mandatory during induction |

## 3. Rituximab

### 3.1 Drug Profile

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Drug class** | Chimeric monoclonal anti-CD20 antibody |
| **Mechanism** | B-cell depletion via CDC, ADCC, and apoptosis; reduces ANCA-producing plasma cell precursors |
| **Indications** | First-line induction (RAVE); superior maintenance (MAINRITSAN, RITAZAREM); relapsing disease |
| **Induction dose** | 375 mg/m2 IV weekly x 4 doses (RAVE protocol) OR 1000 mg IV day 1 and 15 |
| **Maintenance dose** | 500 mg IV q6mo x 4 doses over 24 months (MAINRITSAN protocol) |
| **Relapse dose** | Same as induction: 375 mg/m2 weekly x 4 or 1000 mg x 2 |
| **Premedication** | Acetaminophen 1000 mg, diphenhydramine 25–50 mg, methylprednisolone 100 mg IV |
| **Infusion rate** | Start at 50 mg/hour; escalate q30min if tolerated to max 400 mg/hour |
| **Key evidence** | RAVE (induction), MAINRITSAN (maintenance), RITAZAREM (relapsing) |

### 3.2 Monitoring

| Parameter | Frequency | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| CD19+ B-cell count | Pre-dose and q3–6mo | Measure B-cell depletion and reconstitution |
| Immunoglobulins (IgG, IgA, IgM) | Baseline and q3 months | Risk of hypogammaglobulinaemia |
| HBsAg, HBcAb, HCV serology | Baseline | HBV reactivation risk |
| Quantitative IgG if low | As needed | IVIG replacement if IgG <400 + infections |
| ANCA titre | At each visit | Predict relapse (especially PR3-ANCA) |

### 3.3 Adverse Effects

| Effect | Frequency | Management |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| Infusion reactions (fever, rigors, hypotension) | 5–10% | Slow infusion; premedication; antihistamines |
| Serum sickness | 1–2% | Steroids; discontinue; consider ofatumumab |
| Hypogammaglobulinaemia | 5–20% | Monitor IgG; IVIG replacement if recurrent infection |
| Late-onset neutropenia | 3–5% | Monitor CBC; transient, usually self-limiting |
| HBV reactivation | 1–5% (HBsAg+) | Screen all; antiviral prophylaxis if positive |
| PML (progressive multifocal leukoencephalopathy) | Extremely rare | Discontinue if neurological symptoms |
| Increased infection risk | 10–20% | TMP-SMX prophylaxis; vaccination |

### 3.4 Special Populations

| Population | Consideration |
|------------|--------------|
| Pregnancy | Category C; avoid (transplacental transfer; neonatal B-cell depletion) |
| Paediatric | 375 mg/m2 weekly x 4; effective in paediatric AAV |
| Dialysis-dependent | No dose adjustment; not cleared by dialysis |
| Elderly (>70 years) | Increased infection risk; monitor immunoglobulins |
| Hepatitis B | Antiviral prophylaxis mandatory; monitor HBV DNA |

## 4. Cyclophosphamide

### 4.1 Drug Profile

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Drug class** | Alkylating agent (nitrogen mustard) |
| **Mechanism** | Cross-links DNA; inhibits rapidly dividing cells including T and B cells |
| **Indications** | Alternative induction when rituximab unavailable or contraindicated |
| **IV dose** | 15 mg/kg (max 1.2 g) q3–4 weeks x 3–6 doses (CYCLOPS protocol) |
| **Oral dose** | 2 mg/kg/day (max 200 mg) for 3–6 months (older protocol) |
| **Cumulative limit** | Target <25 g lifetime; ideally <6 months total therapy |
| **Route** | IV preferred (lower cumulative dose); oral as alternative |
| **Key evidence** | CYCLOPS, RAVE (comparator arm), MEPEX |

### 4.2 Monitoring and Toxicity

| Toxicity | Frequency | Prevention |
|----------|-----------|------------|
| Haemorrhagic cystitis | 5–15% (oral), <5% (IV) | Mesna (20% of dose x 3); vigorous hydration |
| Leucopenia (WBC <3000) | 30–50% | CBC day 10–14 post-infusion; dose adjust |
| Ovarian failure | 10–50% (age-dependent) | Limit duration; GnRH agonist co-treatment |
| Azoospermia | 30–60% | Sperm banking before therapy |
| Malignancy (bladder, leukaemia, skin) | 2–5% (cumulative) | Limit total exposure; bladder cancer screening |
| Infections | 20–30% | TMP-SMX prophylaxis; G-CSF if severe neutropenia |

## 5. Plasma Exchange (PLEX)

### 5.1 Procedure Profile

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Mechanism** | Removal of circulating ANCA, cytokines, and inflammatory mediators |
| **Procedure** | Centrifugal or membrane separation; 1–1.5 plasma volume per session |
| **Replacement fluid** | 5% albumin (first-line); FFP if post-procedure bleeding risk |
| **Dose** | 7 sessions over 14 days (PEXIVAS, MEPEX protocols) |
| **Indications** | Severe DAH with hypoxaemia; double-positive ANCA+anti-GBM |
| **Non-indication** | Routine severe AAV without DAH (PEXIVAS: no renal benefit) |

### 5.2 Complications

| Complication | Frequency | Management |
|-------------|-----------|------------|
| Hypocalcaemia (citrate effect) | 5–15% | Calcium gluconate infusion |
| Hypotension | 3–5% | Volume replacement; rate adjustment |
| Anaphylactoid reaction (FFP) | <1% | Use albumin; premedicate |
| Line infection (central access) | 2–5% | Sterile technique; catheter care |
| Hypogammaglobulinaemia | Transient | IVIG if severe infection develops |

## 6. Avacopan

### 6.1 Drug Profile

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Drug class** | Oral C5a receptor (C5aR, CD88) antagonist |
| **Mechanism** | Blocks C5a-mediated neutrophil priming and activation; disrupts the amplification loop in AAV |
| **Indication** | In combination with rituximab or cyclophosphamide for induction of AAV |
| **Dose** | 30 mg (two 15 mg capsules) twice daily with food |
| **Duration** | 52 weeks (ADVOCATE protocol) |
| **Key evidence** | ADVOCATE trial: non-inferior to prednisone at 26 weeks; superior sustained remission at 52 weeks |

### 6.2 Clinical Trial Data (ADVOCATE)

| Endpoint | Avacopan | Prednisone | p-value |
|----------|----------|------------|---------|
| Remission at 26 weeks | 72.3% | 70.1% | Non-inferior |
| Sustained remission at 52 weeks | 65.7% | 54.9% | Superior (p=0.007) |
| Mean cumulative prednisone | 1,576 mg | 3,763 mg | — |
| Serious adverse events | 33.9% | 33.3% | NS |

### 6.3 Monitoring

| Parameter | Frequency | Rationale |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| LFTs (ALT, AST, bilirubin) | Monthly x 6, then q3mo | Hepatic toxicity (3-5% incidence) |
| Creatinine/eGFR | Monthly | Renal function monitoring |
| CBC, CRP | Monthly | Disease activity monitoring |
| Drug-drug interactions | Per interaction check | Avacopan is BCRP inhibitor |

### 6.4 Adverse Effects

| Effect | Frequency | Management |
|--------|-----------|------------|
| ALT/AST elevation (>3x ULN) | 3-5% | Monitor LFTs; hold if >5x ULN |
| Nausea, dyspepsia | 5-10% | Take with food; antiemetics |
| Headache | 7% | Symptomatic management |
| Leucopenia | 2% | Monitor CBC |
| No significant steroid toxicity | Avacopan advantage | No hyperglycaemia, osteoporosis, weight gain |

## 7. Glucocorticoids

### 7.1 Methylprednisolone (IV Pulse)

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Indication** | Severe organ-threatening disease (RPGN, DAH) |
| **Dose** | 500–1000 mg IV daily x 3 consecutive days |
| **Rationale** | Rapid anti-inflammatory effect; reduces neutrophil priming |
| **PEXIVAS insight** | Reduced-dose pulse (50% of standard) non-inferior |

### 7.2 Prednisone (Oral)

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Starting dose** | 0.5–1 mg/kg/day (max 80 mg) after pulse |
| **Taper** | Reduce by 5–10 mg every 1–2 weeks to 20 mg; slower taper thereafter |
| **Target** | 5–7.5 mg/day by 12–20 weeks; ideally off at 26 weeks with avacopan |
| **Adverse effects** | Hyperglycaemia, hypertension, osteoporosis, infection, cataracts, adrenal suppression |

### 7.3 Steroid-Related Monitoring

| Parameter | Frequency |
|-----------|-----------|
| Fasting glucose | Weekly x 4, then monthly |
| Blood pressure | Weekly x 4, then each visit |
| Bone mineral density | Baseline, annually |
| Ophthalmologic exam | Baseline, annually |
| Weight, BMI | Each visit |

## 8. Trimethoprim-Sulfamethoxazole (TMP-SMX)

### 8.1 Prophylaxis Profile

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Indication** | Pneumocystis jirovecii pneumonia (PJP) prophylaxis during induction |
| **Duration** | Minimum 6 months; continue throughout immunosuppressive therapy |
| **Dose** | 800/160 mg (single-strength) daily OR 800/160 mg (double-strength) three times weekly |
| **Alternatives** | Dapsone 100 mg daily (G6PD screen required); atovaquone 1500 mg daily |
| **Monitoring** | CBC, LFTs, creatinine at baseline and monthly |
| **Key note** | Mandatory; PJP mortality in AAV is 30-50% without prophylaxis |

## 9. Azathioprine

### 9.1 Maintenance Profile

| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Drug class** | Purine antimetabolite |
| **Mechanism** | Inhibits DNA synthesis in rapidly dividing T and B cells |
| **Indication** | Alternative maintenance therapy (MAINRITSAN: inferior to rituximab) |
| **Dose** | 1–2 mg/kg/day oral (start at 50 mg; titrate up) |
| **Duration** | 18–24 months minimum |
| **Key evidence** | MAINRITSAN (compared against rituximab); CYCAZAREM |
| **Monitoring** | CBC, LFTs q2 weeks x 8 weeks, then q1–3 months |
| **Key interaction** | Avoid allopurinol (fatal myelosuppression); reduce dose 75% if unavoidable |

### 9.2 TPMT Screening

| TPMT Activity | Action |
|---------------|--------|
| Normal (90% of population) | Full dose (2 mg/kg/day) |
| Intermediate (10%) | Start 50% dose; monitor closely |
| Deficient (0.3%) | Avoid azathioprine; consider alternative (MMF) |

## 10. Drug Interaction Summary

| Drug | Interacts With | Effect |
|------|---------------|--------|
| Rituximab | Live vaccines | Contraindicated; give vaccines before therapy |
| Cyclophosphamide | Allopurinol | Increased myelotoxicity |
| Avacopan | BCRP substrates (rosuvastatin, MTX) | Increased levels of co-administered drugs |
| Avacopan | CYP3A4 inducers (rifampin) | Decreased avacopan levels |
| Azathioprine | Allopurinol, febuxostat | Fatal myelosuppression; absolute contraindication |
| TMP-SMX | Warfarin | Increased INR |
| TMP-SMX | Methotrexate | Increased MTX toxicity |

## 11. Vaccination Recommendations

| Vaccine | Timing | Notes |
|---------|--------|-------|
| Influenza (inactivated) | Annually | During immunosuppression |
| Pneumococcal (PCV20/PCV21) | Pre-treatment | If not given, defer until stable |
| COVID-19 (mRNA) | According to schedule | During immunosuppression |
| HZV (Shingrix) | Before or during stable disease | Non-live recombinant vaccine; safe |
| Live vaccines (MMR, VZV, yellow fever) | Contraindicated | Avoid during immunosuppression |
| Hepatitis B | Before treatment if possible | Double dose may be needed |
