**Document ID**: FSGS_CP

**Domain**: Focal Segmental Glomerulosclerosis (FSGS)  
**Classification**: Clinical Pathway — 6-Stage Model  

---

## 1. Overview

This document defines the complete 6-stage clinical pathway for FSGS management, from initial diagnosis through ESKD and transplantation. The pathway emphasises a steroid-first approach for primary FSGS, incorporates genetic screening branches, and provides variant-specific management based on Columbia classification. Each stage specifies actions, duration, entry/exit criteria, and decision points.

## 3. Stage 1: Diagnosis and Classification

**Duration**: 30 days  
**Entry Criterion**: Clinical suspicion of FSGS (proteinuria ± oedema ± haematuria)  
**Exit Criterion**: Confirmed histological diagnosis with Columbia variant assignment  

### Actions

| # | Action | Timeline | Responsible |
|---|--------|----------|-------------|
| 1.1 | Perform percutaneous renal biopsy (minimum 8 glomeruli, ideally >20) | Day 0–7 | Nephrologist/Radiologist |
| 1.2 | Light microscopy assessment with Columbia classification | Day 7–14 | Pathologist |
| 1.3 | Immunofluorescence assessment (IgG, IgA, IgM, C3, C1q, kappa, lambda) | Day 7–14 | Pathologist |
| 1.4 | Electron microscopy assessment (foot process effacement, deposits, GBM changes) | Day 14–28 | Pathologist |
| 1.5 | Baseline laboratory assessment (UPCR, albumin, Cr/eGFR, lipids, CBC, LFTs) | Day 0–3 | Nephrologist |
| 1.6 | Columbia variant assignment (NOS, Perihilar, Cellular, Tip, Collapsing) | Day 14–28 | Pathologist |
| 1.7 | Initial differential diagnosis exclusion (MCD, membranous, lupus, etc.) | Day 0–14 | Nephrologist |
| 1.8 | Clinical context assessment (nephrotic vs non-nephrotic, severity) | Day 0–14 | Nephrologist |
| 1.9 | Documentation of baseline disease state for monitoring | Day 0–14 | Nephrologist |

### Decision Points

- **If <8 glomeruli obtained**: Consider repeat biopsy or clinical diagnosis with caution
- **If collapsing variant identified**: Prioritise genetic testing (APOL1, NPHS2) and consider early CNI therapy
- **If perihilar variant**: Investigate for adaptive/secondary causes before immunosuppression
- **If tip variant**: Favourable prognosis; may consider less aggressive initial therapy

## 4. Stage 2: Genetic and Secondary Screening

**Duration**: 21 days  
**Entry Criterion**: Confirmed FSGS diagnosis  
**Exit Criterion**: Aetiology determined (primary, genetic, or secondary)  

### Actions

| # | Action | Timeline | Responsible |
|---|--------|----------|-------------|
| 2.1 | APOL1 genotyping (all African American patients) | Day 0–7 | Genetics/Lab |
| 2.2 | Genetic panel if indicated (NPHS1/2, ACTN4, TRPC6, INF2): age <25, family history, resistant disease | Day 0–14 | Genetics |
| 2.3 | HIV serology | Day 0–3 | Lab |
| 2.4 | HBV/HCV serology | Day 0–3 | Lab |
| 2.5 | Syphilis serology (RPR/VDRL) | Day 0–3 | Lab |
| 2.6 | Obesity assessment (BMI, waist circumference) | Day 0–7 | Nephrologist |
| 2.7 | Hypertension evaluation (BP log, echocardiography if indicated) | Day 0–14 | Nephrologist |
| 2.8 | Drug history review (pamidronate, lithium, heroin, NSAIDs) | Day 0–7 | Nephrologist |

### Genetic Testing Indications (When to Pursue)

| Indication | Action |
|------------|--------|
| Age <25 years at presentation | Full genetic panel |
| Family history of FSGS or ESKD | Full genetic panel |
| Steroid-resistant disease | Genetic panel |
| African American ancestry | APOL1 genotyping |
| Collapsing variant | APOL1 genotyping + genetic panel |
| Congenital nephrotic syndrome | NPHS1/NPHS2 |

### Decision Points

- **If secondary cause identified**: Treat underlying cause; immunosuppression may not be needed
- **If genetic cause confirmed**: Do NOT initiate immunosuppression; proceed to supportive care
- **If primary (idiopathic) FSGS confirmed**: Proceed to Stage 3 (immunosuppression)
- **If APOL1 high-risk genotype**: Warn about poor prognosis; consider early CNI-based approach

## 5. Stage 3: Immunosuppressive Therapy

**Duration**: 120 days (approximately 16 weeks)  
**Entry Criterion**: Primary FSGS confirmed, secondary and genetic causes excluded  
**Exit Criterion**: Response assessment at 12–16 weeks  

### Actions

| # | Action | Timeline | Responsible |
|---|--------|----------|-------------|
| 3.1 | Initiate prednisone 1 mg/kg/day (max 80 mg) | Day 0 | Nephrologist |
| 3.2 | Baseline metabolic panel (glucose, lipids, bone density) | Day 0–7 | Nephrologist |
| 3.3 | Ophthalmological examination (cataract risk) | Day 0–14 | Ophthalmologist |
| 3.4 | Infection screening (TB, hepatitis) | Day 0–7 | Lab |
| 3.5 | Blood pressure monitoring (weekly initially) | Day 0–28 | Nephrologist |
| 3.6 | Blood glucose monitoring (fasting glucose weekly for 4 weeks) | Day 0–28 | Nephrologist |
| 3.7 | Proteinuria/albumin assessment at 4 weeks | Day 28 | Lab |
| 3.8 | Prednisone taper begins after 16 weeks if in remission | Week 16 | Nephrologist |
| 3.9 | Reassess proteinuria, eGFR, clinical status at 12–16 weeks | Week 12–16 | Nephrologist |
| 3.10 | Document response: complete remission, partial remission, or no response | Week 12–16 | Nephrologist |

### Response Definitions

| Response | Definition |
|----------|-----------|
| Complete Remission (CR) | UPCR <0.3 g/g or proteinuria <300 mg/d, normal eGFR, normal albumin |
| Partial Remission (PR) | UPCR decrease >50% from baseline, UPCR <3.5 g/g, stable/improved eGFR |
| No Response (NR) | Failure to achieve CR or PR after 16 weeks of adequate steroid therapy |

### Variant-Specific Approach

| Columbia Variant | Approach |
|------------------|----------|
| NOS | Standard prednisone course |
| Tip | Standard prednisone; often responsive; consider shorter course if CR achieved |
| Cellular | Standard prednisone; monitor closely; early CNI if partial response only |
| Perihilar | Investigate adaptive cause; immunosuppression often less effective |
| Collapsing | May require combination CNI + steroids; consider early CNI if no response by week 8 |

### Decision Points

- **Complete remission (CR)**: Begin prednisone taper; proceed to Stage 4 (monitoring)
- **Partial remission (PR)**: Continue steroids to 16 weeks; reassess
- **No response at 16 weeks**: Classify as steroid-resistant; proceed to Stage 5 (resistant management)
- **Steroid toxicity**: If intolerable side effects before 16 weeks, initiate CNI early

## 6. Stage 4: Monitoring and Remission Assessment

**Duration**: 365 days (1 year)  
**Entry Criterion**: Achievement of CR or PR with steroids  
**Exit Criterion**: Relapse, progression, or stable remission at 12 months  

### Actions

| # | Action | Timeline | Responsible |
|---|--------|----------|-------------|
| 4.1 | Prednisone taper protocol (reduce by 5–10 mg every 2–4 weeks) | Weeks 16–32 | Nephrologist |
| 4.2 | Proteinuria/albumin assessment (q1 month initially, then q3 months) | Ongoing | Lab |
| 4.3 | eGFR monitoring (q1–3 months) | Ongoing | Lab |
| 4.4 | Blood pressure monitoring (q1 month) | Ongoing | Nephrologist |
| 4.5 | Glucose monitoring during steroid taper | During steroids | Nephrologist |
| 4.6 | Lipid profile assessment (q3 months) | Ongoing | Lab |
| 4.7 | Bone density assessment (if prolonged steroid use >6 months) | q6–12 months | Endocrinologist |
| 4.8 | Infection surveillance (q1–3 months) | Ongoing | Nephrologist |
| 4.9 | Steroid-sparing agent introduction if dependent (MMF, CNI) | If relapse on taper | Nephrologist |
| 4.10 | Relapse assessment and management | If relapse occurs | Nephrologist |
| 4.11 | Transition to maintenance immunosuppression if needed | Ongoing | Nephrologist |
| 4.12 | Long-term prognosis assessment at 12 months | Month 12 | Nephrologist |

### Steroid Taper Protocol

| Week | Prednisone Dose | Notes |
|------|-----------------|-------|
| 16 | 60–80 mg/day | Begin taper if in CR/PR |
| 20 | 40–50 mg/day | Reduce by 10 mg q2–4 weeks |
| 24 | 20–30 mg/day | Continue taper |
| 28 | 10–15 mg/day | Slow taper to avoid relapse |
| 32 | 5–10 mg/day | Final taper |
| 36 | 0–5 mg/day | Discontinue or maintain low dose if dependent |
| 40+ | Off steroids | Monitor for relapse |

### Decision Points

- **Successful taper to off steroids**: Continue monitoring; proceed to long-term follow-up
- **Relapse on taper**: Restart prednisone at last effective dose; add steroid-sparing agent
- **Steroid dependency**: Defined as ≥2 relapses during taper or within 6 months of discontinuation; initiate MMF or CNI
- **Progressive eGFR decline**: Investigate; consider re-biopsy if unexplained

## 7. Stage 5: Resistant and Relapse Management

**Duration**: 365 days (1 year)  
**Entry Criterion**: Steroid-resistant (no response at 16 weeks) OR relapsing/dependent disease  
**Exit Criterion**: Response to second-line therapy, clinical trial enrolment, or progression to ESKD  

### Actions

| # | Action | Timeline | Responsible |
|---|--------|----------|-------------|
| 5.1 | Initiate cyclosporine (3–5 mg/kg/day, target trough 100–200 ng/mL) | Day 0 | Nephrologist |
| 5.2 | OR initiate tacrolimus (0.05–0.1 mg/kg/day, target trough 5–10 ng/mL) | Day 0 | Nephrologist |
| 5.3 | Low-dose prednisone (0.15–0.2 mg/kg/day) with CNI | Day 0 | Nephrologist |
| 5.4 | CNI trough level monitoring (weekly for 4 weeks, then q2–4 weeks) | Ongoing | Lab/Nephrologist |
| 5.5 | Serum creatinine monitoring (weekly for 4 weeks, then monthly) | Ongoing | Lab |
| 5.6 | Proteinuria assessment at 3 months on CNI | Month 3 | Lab |
| 5.7 | Reassess response at 6 months; if no response, consider re-biopsy | Month 6 | Nephrologist |
| 5.8 | If CNI-resistant: consider MMF, rituximab, or clinical trial | Month 6+ | Nephrologist |
| 5.9 | If relapsing/dependent: add MMF or CNI as steroid-sparing agent | Ongoing | Nephrologist |
| 5.10 | Prepare for ESKD/transplant if all therapies fail | Ongoing | Nephrologist/Transplant team |

### CNI Response Monitoring

| Timepoint | Assessment | Action |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| 3 months | Proteinuria, eGFR, CNI trough | If PR/CR: continue; if NR: re-biopsy |
| 6 months | Proteinuria, eGFR, CNI trough | If NR: change therapy; consider trials |
| 9 months | Proteinuria, eGFR | If improving: continue; if stable/declining: prepare for ESKD |
| 12 months | Full reassessment | Define long-term trajectory |

### Variant-Specific Considerations

| Variant | CNI Response | Notes |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| Collapsing | Often resistant | High-dose CNI may be needed; consider combination therapy |
| NOS | 60–70% respond | Standard CNI protocol |
| Tip | Often steroid-sensitive | May not need CNI |
| Cellular | Variable | Early CNI if partial steroid response |
| Perihilar | Often resistant | Focus on treating secondary cause |

### Decision Points

- **CNI response (CR/PR at 6 months)**: Continue CNI for 12–24 months; slow taper
- **CNI resistance**: Re-biopsy to confirm diagnosis; consider alternative agents
- **Progressive disease on all therapies**: Refer for transplantation evaluation
- **Collapsing variant with rapid decline**: Urgent transplantation evaluation

## 8. Stage 6: ESKD and Transplantation

**Duration**: 365 days (ongoing)  
**Entry Criterion**: eGFR <15 mL/min/1.73m² or initiation of renal replacement therapy  
**Exit Criterion**: Stable post-transplant function or ongoing dialysis  

### Actions

| # | Action | Timeline | Responsible |
|---|--------|----------|-------------|
| 6.1 | Renal replacement therapy initiation (dialysis or preemptive transplant) | When eGFR <15 | Nephrologist |
| 6.2 | Transplant evaluation and listing | 6–12 months pre-emptive if possible | Transplant team |
| 6.3 | Pre-transplant workup (cardiac, vascular, infection screening) | Pre-transplant | Transplant team |
| 6.4 | Living donor evaluation if applicable | Ongoing | Transplant team |
| 6.5 | Post-transplant recurrence risk assessment (APOL1, aetiology) | Pre-transplant | Nephrologist |
| 6.6 | Post-transplant monitoring for recurrence (proteinuria q1 week for first month) | Post-transplant | Transplant nephrologist |
| 6.7 | If recurrence suspected: protocol biopsy and plasmapheresis | Post-transplant | Transplant team |
| 6.8 | Immunosuppression optimisation (induction + maintenance) | Post-transplant | Transplant nephrologist |
| 6.9 | Cardiovascular risk management | Ongoing | Cardiologist/Nephrologist |
| 6.10 | Bone mineral disease management | Ongoing | Endocrinologist |
| 6.11 | Infection prophylaxis (CMV, BK virus, PJP) | Post-transplant | Infectious disease |
| 6.12 | Long-term graft function monitoring | Ongoing | Transplant nephrologist |

### Post-Transplant Recurrence Management

| Feature | Approach |
|---------|----------|
| Recurrence rate (primary FSGS) | 20–40% |
| Recurrence rate (collapsing) | ~50% |
| Early recurrence (within weeks) | Plasmapheresis (2–3x/week for 4–12 weeks) |
| Late recurrence | Consider CNI adjustment, plasmapheresis |
| Graft loss from recurrence | ~50% of recurrent cases |
| Prevention strategies | Peri-operative plasmapheresis (investigational) |

### Decision Points

- **Successful transplant without recurrence**: Standard post-transplant care
- **Post-transplant recurrence**: Initiate plasmapheresis; adjust immunosuppression
- **Graft loss**: Return to dialysis; re-list for re-transplantation
- **Ineligible for transplant**: Optimise dialysis and conservative management
