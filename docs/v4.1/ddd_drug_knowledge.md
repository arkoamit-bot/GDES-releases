## 3. Key Drugs

### 3.1 ACE Inhibitors (RAASi)
- Ramipril 2.5-10 mg daily. Start low, titrate to max tolerated dose
- Monitor K+, Cr 2-4 wks after initiation/dose change
- Indicated for all patients with proteinuria >=0.5 g/day

### 3.2 Corticosteroids
- Prednisone 1 mg/kg/day (max 80 mg) PO x8 wks then taper over 6-12 mo
- Pulse MP 500-1000 mg IV x3 for crescentic/RPGN
- Benefit primarily in children; limited evidence in adults

### 3.3 Eculizumab
- 900 mg IV weekly x4, then 1200 mg q2w
- Meningococcal vaccination mandatory (ACWY + MenB) — give at least 2 weeks before first dose
- Monitor CH50 (target <10%) for dosing adequacy
- Continue indefinitely if response seen

### 3.4 Plasma Exchange
- 1.5 plasma volume exchange daily x5 then QOD x5-10
- Replacement fluid: FFP or albumin
- Indications: anti-CFH antibody DDD, RPGN with crescents

### 3.5 MMF
- 1-2 g/day PO divided BID
- Monitor CBC, LFT monthly
- Level monitoring: target AUC 30-60 mg*h/L

### 3.6 Fresh Frozen Plasma (FFP)
- 30 mL/kg IV q2 weeks
- For CFH deficiency (factor H replacement)
- Monitor C3 levels to assess adequacy
- Risk of volume overload, transfusion reactions

## 4. Drug Safety

| Drug | Key Side Effects | Monitoring |
|---|---|---|
| ACEi | Cough, hyperkalemia, AKI, angioedema | K+, Cr at 2-4 wks, then Q3mo |
| Prednisone | Hyperglycemia, osteoporosis, infection, growth suppression | Glucose, bone density (annual), growth (paediatric) |
| Eculizumab | Neisseria infection, infusion reaction | CH50, meningococcal antibodies, fever surveillance |
| MMF | Myelosuppression, GI upset, teratogenicity | CBC, LFT monthly |
| Cyclophosphamide | Myelosuppression, hemorrhagic cystitis, infertility | CBC, UA, LFT |
| PLEX | Bleeding, hypocalcemia, anaphylaxis, line infection | Coags, Ca, vitals |
| FFP | Volume overload, transfusion reaction, infection | Volume status, vital signs |
| Furosemide | Electrolyte depletion, AKI, ototoxicity | K+, Cr |
