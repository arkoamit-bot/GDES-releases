## 2. Drug Classification
| Drug Class | Role | Evidence |
|---|---|---|
| Tacrolimus reduction | Cornerstone — reduce target to 4-6 ng/mL | AST 1B |
| MMF reduction/discontinuation | Reduce anti-proliferative | AST 1B |
| Leflunomide | Second-line for persistent BKVN | AST 2C |
| Cidofovir | Third-line for refractory BKVN (nephrotoxic) | AST 2D |
| IVIG | Adjunct — contains anti-BKV antibodies | AST 2D |
| mTOR inhibitors (Sirolimus/Everolimus) | Switch from CNI — reduces BKV in vitro | KDIGO 2D |
| Prednisone | Maintenance — continue during IS reduction | KDIGO 1B |
---
## 3. Stepwise Management
| Step | Intervention | Target |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Reduce tacrolimus to 4-6 ng/mL, reduce/stop MMF | BKV clearance (80% in 4-8 wks) |
| 2 | Leflunomide 20-40 mg daily (load 100 mg x3) | Level 50-100 mcg/mL |
| 3 | Cidofovir 0.25-1 mg/kg IV q2-4 wks | Reserve for refractory |
| 4 | IVIG 2 g/kg | Adjunct to steps 1-3 |
