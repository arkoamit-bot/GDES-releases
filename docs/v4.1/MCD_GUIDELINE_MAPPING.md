## 1. Document Purpose

This document provides a comprehensive cross-reference mapping of all major clinical practice guidelines relevant to Minimal Change Disease management. It aligns KDIGO 2021 (Chapter 5), KDIGO 2025 (Emerging), ISN 2023, ASN 2022, and IPNA 2023 recommendations with the BGDDR v4.1 disease knowledge schema, clinical pathways, and knowledge base rules. The mapping identifies areas of concordance, divergence, and gaps across guidelines.

## 5. Concordance Analysis

### 5.1 High Concordance (>90% agreement)

| Topic | Agreement Level | Details |
|---|---|---|
| Diagnosis criteria | Full | All 5 guidelines agree on LM, IF, EM, clinical, and exclusion criteria |
| First-line steroid therapy | Full | Prednisone at 60 mg/m2 (children) or 1 mg/kg (adults) |
| Steroid-resistant definition | High | Minor variation in timing (4 vs 8 weeks children) |
| Biopsy indication | High | Biopsy mandatory in adults; can defer in typical children |
| Supportive care | High | RAASi, diuretics, statins universally recommended |
| Long-term prognosis | High | Excellent renal survival for steroid-sensitive disease |

### 5.2 Moderate Concordance (70-90% agreement)

| Topic | Agreement Level | Variance |
|---|---|---|
| FR/SD first-line agent | High but evolving | KDIGO 2021: MMF/CNI equivalent; KDIGO 2025 and IPNA 2023: MMF preferred |
| Rituximab positioning | High but evolving | Post-2023: elevated to first-line for SD; KDIGO 2021 placed it as second-line |
| Steroid taper duration | Moderate | Ranges from 3 months (IPNA) to 6 months (KDIGO adult) |
| Cyclophosphamide role | Moderate | Paediatric guidelines retain as viable second-line; adult guidelines restrict |
| Adult steroid duration | Moderate | 4 weeks (KDIGO) to 16 weeks (ASN) for initial therapy |

### 5.3 Areas of Divergence (<70% agreement)

| Topic | Guidelines | Difference | Impact |
|---|---|---|---|
| Biopsy in children | G1/G5 vs G3 | G1/G5: defer if typical; G3: consider biopsy | Low — clinical equipoise |
| Genetic testing | G2 vs G1/G3/G4/G5 | G2 recommends APOL1 testing; others no routine | Low — emerging |
| Levamisole use | G5 only | Only IPNA 2023 recommends levamisole | Geographic variation |
| Rituximab dosing | All vary | 375 mg/m2 x 1-4 vs 1g x 2 | Moderate — practice variation |

## 6. KDIGO 2021 Chapter 5 — Detailed Breakdown

### 6.1 Chapter Scope

KDIGO 2021 Chapter 5 covers Steroid-Sensitive Nephrotic Syndrome (SSNS) in both children and adults. Key sections:

| Section | Topic | Recommendations |
|---|---|---|
| 5.1 | Diagnosis and classification | 5 recommendations |
| 5.2 | When to biopsy | 3 recommendations |
| 5.3 | Initial therapy | 4 recommendations |
| 5.4 | Management of initial episode | 6 recommendations |
| 5.5 | Management of relapses | 8 recommendations |
| 5.6 | Management of FR/SD | 7 recommendations |
| 5.7 | Supportive therapy | 4 recommendations |
| 5.8 | Immunisations | 3 recommendations |
| 5.9 | Outcomes | 2 recommendations |

### 6.2 Key KDIGO 2021 Recommendations Relevant to BGDDR

- **5.1.1:** Diagnosis of MCD requires biopsy in adults and atypical children
- **5.3.1:** Prednisone 60 mg/m2/day or 1 mg/kg/day (max 80 mg) for initial episode
- **5.4.1:** Taper prednisone after achieving remission over 3-6 months
- **5.5.1:** FR defined as 2 relapses in 6 months or 4 in 12 months
- **5.6.1:** MMF or CNI as first-line steroid-sparing agents for FR/SD
- **5.6.4:** Rituximab for patients who fail MMF and CNI
- **5.7.1:** ACEi or ARB for persistent proteinuria >1 g/day
- **5.8.1:** Vaccinate with inactivated vaccines during immunosuppression

## 8. ASN 2022 — Adult-Focused Mapping

### 8.1 Key ASN 2022 Adult Recommendations

- All adults with suspected MCD require biopsy (no empiric steroid trial)
- Steroid therapy: prednisone 1 mg/kg/day (max 80 mg) for 8-16 weeks
- AKI at presentation: manage with careful diuresis, dialysis if needed; full recovery expected
- FR/SD: MMF first-line; rituximab for refractory
- Elderly: lower steroid doses, closer monitoring for toxicity
- Pregnancy: plan preconception; steroids safe; MMF contraindicated

## 9. ISN 2023 — Consensus Mapping

### 9.1 ISN 2023 Consensus Statements

- MCD defined as a podocytopathy with no immune deposits
- T-cell dysregulation is central; B-cells contribute to relapse
- IL-13 and CD80 are key molecular mediators
- Biopsy essential for diagnosis but can be deferred in typical paediatric cases
- Steroid response is both diagnostic and prognostic
- Rituximab is the most effective steroid-sparing agent for SD
- Genetic testing emerging for steroid-resistant cases
