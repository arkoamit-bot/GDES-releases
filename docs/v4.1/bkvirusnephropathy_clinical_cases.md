### CASE-BKV-MIX-001: Mixed BKVN + TCMR Rejection
**Type:** atypical
**Source:** Mixed BKVN + TCMR
**History:**
A 55-year-old woman 8 months post-transplant with BKV 12,000 copies/mL. Creatinine rising 1.1 to 2.4. Biopsy: SV40+ in 20% of tubules BUT also i2, t2. C4d negative. DSA negative. Mixed picture.
**Examination:**
BP 138/84, graft tender, no fever.
**Laboratory Data:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 28 mL/min |
| Cr | 2.4 mg/dL |
| Plasma BKV | 12,000 copies/mL |
| Tacrolimus trough | 6.5 ng/mL |
| DSA | Negative |
| BK PCR urine | 5e7 |
**Biopsy Data:**
| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | SV40+ in 20% of tubules (BKVN Stage A) PLUS i2, t2 (TCMR Type I). No arteritis |
| SV40 | Positive (focal) |
| IF | C4d0 |
| EM | No glomerular or capillary changes |
**Diagnosis:**
Mixed BKVN (Stage A) + TCMR (Type I) — SV40+ and tubulitis
**Treatment:**
Difficult balance: pulse MP 250 mg x3 (treats TCMR gently). Reduce tacrolimus target 4-6. MMF 500 mg BID (reduce but continue). Weekly BKV + DSA. Avoid ATG.
**Outcome:**
Week 2: Cr 1.6, BKV 8000. Week 4: Cr 1.2, BKV 2000. Month 3: Cr 1.1, BKV 300. Resolution of both. Illustrates balanced approach: treat rejection cautiously, maintain some IS reduction for BKV.
**Differential Diagnosis:**
| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| tCellMediatedRejection | 0.3 | Both BKVN (SV40+) and TCMR (tubulitis in SV40- areas) are present. Pure TCMR would be SV40 negative. This requires dual management. |

### CASE-BKV-RET-001: Re-Transplant After BKVN Graft Loss
**Type:** atypical
**Source:** Re-transplant BKVN
**History:**
A 52-year-old man lost prior graft to BKVN Stage C (graft failed 18 months ago). BKV PCR now negative for 6 months. Seeking re-transplant. No DSA. Low immunologic risk.
**Examination:**
BP 130/78, no oedema, HD fistula functional.
**Laboratory Data:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | On HD (anuric) |
| Plasma BKV | Negative (x3 tests over 6 months) |
| DSA | Negative |
| Tacrolimus | N/A (off IS) |
**Biopsy Data:**
| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | N/A (native kidneys) |
| SV40 | N/A |
| IF | N/A |
**Diagnosis:**
ESKD from BKVN — cleared virus, candidate for re-transplant
**Treatment:**
Re-transplant with BKV-appropriate IS: tacrolimus target 5-8, MMF 1.5 g/day, prednisone. Monthly BKV PCR. Low threshold for IS reduction if BKV detected.
**Outcome:**
Re-transplant successful. BKV negative at 6 months. No BKVN recurrence. Cr 1.2. Illustrates: re-transplant after BKVN is safe with appropriate IS management.
**Differential Diagnosis:**
| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| primary graft failure | 0.1 | Previous graft loss = BKVN, not immunologic. Re-transplant with appropriate IS has good outcomes |

### CASE-BKV-URO-001: BKV Ureteric Stenosis
**Type:** complications
**Source:** BKV ureteric stenosis
**History:**
A 40-year-old man 6 months post-transplant with BKV viraemia (18,000 copies/mL). Presents with rising Cr (1.0 to 2.2) and hydronephrosis on ultrasound. Biopsy: BKVN Stage A plus ureteric involvement.
**Examination:**
BP 130/80, graft non-tender, mild flank pain.
**Laboratory Data:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 32 mL/min |
| Cr | 2.2 mg/dL |
| Plasma BKV | 18,000 copies/mL |
| Hydronephrosis | Present (ultrasound — mild, no stones) |
| DSA | Negative |
**Biopsy Data:**
| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | SV40+ in 20% of tubules. PLUS ureter biopsy: SV40+ in ureteric epithelium, ulceration, inflammation |
| SV40 | Positive (kidney and ureter) |
| IF | C4d0 |
**Diagnosis:**
BKVN Stage A + BKV Ureteric Stenosis
**Treatment:**
Percutaneous nephrostomy (relieves obstruction). IS reduction (tac 4-6, reduce MMF). Weekly BKV. Leflunomide added early due to ureteric involvement. Stent placement after inflammation resolves.
**Outcome:**
Week 4: BKV 5000, Cr 1.4 (post-nephrostomy). Week 8: BKV 400. Month 3: BKV negative, Cr 1.1. Ureteric stent placed — stable. Illustrates rare but important BKV complication.
**Differential Diagnosis:**
| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| obstructive uropathy | 0.2 | BKV ureteric involvement causes ulceration and stenosis. SV40+ ureter biopsy confirms. Distinguish from surgical stricture by BKV PCR and biopsy. |

### CASE-BKV-STC-001: BKVN Stage C with Graft Loss
**Type:** complications
**Source:** BKVN Stage C
**History:**
A 65-year-old man 18 months post-transplant. No BKV screening. Presents with Cr 4.5, UPCR 1.5, and BKV >10,000 copies/mL. eGFR 14. Long-standing BKVN undetected.
**Examination:**
BP 156/92, 2+ oedema, graft non-tender, fatigue.
**Laboratory Data:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 14 mL/min |
| Cr | 4.5 mg/dL |
| Plasma BKV | 55,000 copies/mL |
| Tacrolimus trough | 7.0 ng/mL |
| DSA | Negative |
| BK PCR urine | >1e8 |
**Biopsy Data:**
| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | Extensive SV40+ in remaining tubules, severe tubular atrophy, IFTA 70%, interstitial fibrosis. Inflammation minimal (burnt-out) |
| SV40 | Positive (few remaining tubules) |
| IF | C4d0 |
| EM | Extensive tubular loss, glomerulosclerosis 40% |
**Diagnosis:**
BKVN Stage C — late presentation, advanced IFTA, irreversible
**Treatment:**
Supportive: continued IS reduction but limited expectation. Avoid nephrotoxic adjuncts (cidofovir). CKD management, prepare for dialysis. Re-transplant evaluation.
**Outcome:**
Month 3: Cr 5.2, BKV 8000 (slow clearance). Month 6: started HD. BKV negative. Re-transplant evaluation. Illustrates consequence of no screening: advanced BKVN with irreversible graft loss.
**Differential Diagnosis:**
| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| cniToxicity | 0.2 | SV40+ confirms BKVN. CNI toxicity would show arteriolar hyalinosis, not SV40. IFTA is from BKVN, not CNI. Both can co-exist but SV40+ = BKVN |

### CASE-BKV-REF-001: Refractory BKVN — Cidofovir Rescue
**Type:** complications
**Source:** Refractory BKVN
**History:**
A 48-year-old woman 12 months post-transplant. BKVN Stage B on biopsy. Failed IS reduction + leflunomide (BKV still 15,000 at 12 weeks). Cr 1.8 to 2.6. Cidofovir considered.
**Examination:**
BP 144/88, mild oedema, graft non-tender.
**Laboratory Data:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 26 mL/min |
| Cr | 2.6 mg/dL |
| Plasma BKV | 15,000 copies/mL (persistent 12 weeks) |
| Tacrolimus trough | 4.8 |
| Leflunomide level | 68 mcg/mL (therapeutic) |
| DSA | Negative |
**Biopsy Data:**
| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | Re-biopsy: SV40+ 30%, IFTA 35% (progression), no rejection |
| SV40 | Positive |
| IF | C4d0 |
**Diagnosis:**
Refractory BKVN — persistent despite IS reduction + leflunomide
**Treatment:**
Cidofovir 0.5 mg/kg IV q2 weeks x3 doses. Monitor Cr, urine protein. Hydrate before. Continue IS reduction.
**Outcome:**
Month 1: BKV 8000. Month 2: BKV 2000. Month 3: BKV negative. Cr 2.2 (partial recovery). No cidofovir nephrotoxicity. Illustrates rescue therapy for refractory BKVN, but increased toxicity risk.
**Differential Diagnosis:**
| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| antibodyMediatedRejection | 0.2 | C4d0, DSA negative, SV40+ = BKVN only. Re-biopsy essential to exclude mixed rejection before escalating therapy |

### CASE-BKV-STG-001: BKVN Stage A with Rising Viraemia
**Type:** typical
**Source:** BKVN Stage A
**History:**
A 50-year-old woman 6 months post-transplant. Plasma BKV rising from 5000 to 18,000 over 3 weeks. Cr rising slowly from 0.9 to 1.3. Biopsy performed.
**Examination:**
BP 134/82, graft non-tender, no oedema.
**Laboratory Data:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 48 mL/min |
| Cr | 1.3 mg/dL |
| Plasma BKV | 18,000 copies/mL (rising from 5K) |
| Tacrolimus trough | 7.8 ng/mL |
| DSA | Negative |
| BK PCR urine | 6e7 |
**Biopsy Data:**
| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | SV40+ in 15% of tubules. Mild interstitial inflammation with plasma cells. IFTA 10%. No arteritis. No glomerulitis |
| SV40 | Positive (nuclear staining in tubular epithelial cells) |
| IF | C4d0 |
| EM | Normal glomeruli, viral particles in tubular nuclei |
**Diagnosis:**
BKVN Stage A — SV40 positive, <25% tubules, minimal IFTA
**Treatment:**
Reduce tacrolimus target 4-6. Stop MMF. Prednisone 5 mg. Weekly BKV PCR.
**Outcome:**
Week 4: BKV 6000. Week 8: BKV 800. Month 3: BKV negative, Cr 1.0. Month 6: repeat biopsy — SV40 negative, no inflammation. Complete histologic resolution. Illustrates: Stage A is reversible with IS reduction.
**Differential Diagnosis:**
| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| tCellMediatedRejection | 0.3 | Tubulitis with SV40+ = BKVN not TCMR. If SV40 negative, would be TCMR. Critical distinction: SV40 stain essential on all transplant biopsies with tubulitis |

### CASE-BKV-STB-001: BKVN Stage B with Persistent Viraemia
**Type:** typical
**Source:** BKVN Stage B
**History:**
A 60-year-old man 9 months post-transplant. BKV 25,000 copies/mL for 6 weeks despite IS reduction. Cr 1.2 to 2.0. Biopsy shows Stage B.
**Examination:**
BP 142/86, graft mildly tender, 1+ oedema.
**Laboratory Data:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 34 mL/min |
| Cr | 2.0 mg/dL |
| Plasma BKV | 25,000 copies/mL (persistent) |
| Tacrolimus trough | 5.2 (on reduced dose) |
| DSA | Negative |
| BK PCR urine | >1e8 |
**Biopsy Data:**
| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | SV40+ in 35% of tubules. Dense interstitial inflammation (lymphocytes, plasma cells). Tubular necrosis foci. IFTA 30% |
| SV40 | Positive — extensive |
| IF | C4d0 |
| EM | Viral particles in tubular cell nuclei, tubular basement membrane thickening |
**Diagnosis:**
BKVN Stage B — persistent despite IS reduction
**Treatment:**
Continue IS reduction (tac 4-6, MMF stopped). Add leflunomide 100 mg x3 days then 40 mg daily. Target level 50-100. Weekly BKV PCR.
**Outcome:**
Week 4: BKV 15,000. Week 8: BKV 4000. Week 12: BKV 600. Month 6: BKV negative, Cr 1.6 (incomplete recovery due to IFTA). Leflunomide helped clear. Illustrates leflunomide as second-line.
**Differential Diagnosis:**
| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| tCellMediatedRejection | 0.2 | SV40+ = BKVN. If persistent despite IS reduction, need to re-biopsy to exclude concurrent rejection. DSA negative, C4d0 = no ABMR |

### CASE-BKV-EAR-001: Early BK Viraemia Detected on Screening
**Type:** typical
**Source:** Early viraemia
**History:**
A 45-year-old man 4 months post-deceased donor kidney transplant (tacrolimus/MMF/prednisone). Routine monthly BKV PCR: 8500 copies/mL. Cr stable at 1.0. No symptoms. No graft tenderness. Tacrolimus trough 8.5.
**Examination:**
BP 128/76, graft non-tender, no oedema, well.
**Laboratory Data:**
| Parameter | Value |
|---|---|
| eGFR | 62 mL/min |
| Cr | 1.0 mg/dL |
| Plasma BKV | 8500 copies/mL |
| Tacrolimus trough | 8.5 ng/mL |
| DSA | Negative |
| BK PCR urine | 2.5e7 copies/mL |
| CMV PCR | Negative |
**Biopsy Data:**
| Feature | Finding |
|---|---|
| LM | Not performed (viraemia without Cr rise + <10K) |
| SV40 | N/A |
| IF | N/A |
**Diagnosis:**
BK Viraemia — significant level (8500 copies/mL). Pre-biopsy stage.
**Treatment:**
Reduce tacrolimus target 4-6 ng/mL (decrease dose). MMF 1 g/day (from 2 g). Maintain prednisone 5 mg. Weekly BKV PCR. Biopsy if persistent or rising.
**Outcome:**
Week 2: BKV 3200. Week 4: BKV 600. Week 6: BKV negative. Cr 0.9. Illustrates: IS reduction alone clears 80% of viraemia. No biopsy needed if Cr stable and BKV <10K.
**Differential Diagnosis:**
| Disease | Score | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| tCellMediatedRejection | 0.1 | Stable Cr, BKV+ = BKV viraemia, not TCMR. IS reduction is correct — ATG would worsen BKV. SV40 negative if biopsied |
