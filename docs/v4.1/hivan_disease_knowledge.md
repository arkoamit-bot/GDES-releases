## 2. Disease Record — Full 21-Field Schema

### 2.1 Definition

HIV-Associated Nephropathy (HIVAN) is a collapsing form of focal segmental glomerulosclerosis (FSGS) caused by direct HIV infection of renal epithelial cells, occurring predominantly in individuals of African ancestry with APOL1 risk alleles. It is characterised by nephrotic-range proteinuria, rapid progression to ESKD within months if untreated, and the histologic triad of collapsing glomerulopathy, tubular microcystic dilatation, and tubuloreticular inclusions on EM. The introduction of combined antiretroviral therapy (ART) has dramatically reduced incidence and improved renal outcomes.

### 2.2 Epidemiology

Incidence: declined from 10-15% of HIV patients pre-ART to <2% in the ART era. Race: African ancestry (90% of cases) — APOL1 G1/G2 risk alleles confer 10-20x increased risk. Gender: male predominance (3:1). Age: mean 35-45 years at diagnosis. Geography: most common in Sub-Saharan Africa and African diaspora. In the US: HIVAN accounts for 0.5-1% of ESKD among HIV patients. Pre-ART: ESKD within 6-12 months. Post-ART: renal outcomes significantly improved, but 30-40% still progress to ESKD if advanced at diagnosis. APOL1 association: 70-80% of HIVAN patients have two APOL1 risk alleles.

### 2.3 Aetiology

DIRECT CAUSE: HIV infection of renal epithelial cells (podocytes, tubular cells) — distinct from HIV-associated immune complex disease. HIV enters cells via non-CD4 mechanisms (probably via lipid rafts). The virus does not productively replicate but expresses viral proteins (Nef, Vpu, Tat, Rev) that dysregulate cellular pathways. APOL1 RISK ALLELES: G1 (rs73885319, rs60910145) and G2 (rs71785313) — present in 30-40% of African ancestry individuals. Two risk alleles (G1/G1, G1/G2, or G2/G2) increase HIVAN risk 10-20x. APOL1 is an innate immune factor that lyses Trypanosoma brucei but the risk variants cause podocyte injury. MODIFIERS: CD4 count <200 cells/uL (higher risk), viral load >100,000 copies/mL (higher risk), no ART (highest risk), co-infection with HCV or HBV.

### 2.4 Pathophysiology

HIV infects renal epithelial cells (not via CD4 — alternative entry mechanisms). Within podocytes, HIV expresses Nef protein which activates Stat3 and drives podocyte dedifferentiation and proliferation (collapsing phenotype). Podocytes lose expression of podocyte-specific markers (nephrin, podocin, WT1) and re-enter the cell cycle, leading to podocyte proliferation and detachment. APOL1 risk variants, when expressed under inflammatory conditions (IFN-gamma), form ion channels in intracellular membranes that cause podocyte cytotoxicity and cell death. The collapsing glomerulopathy results from podocyte depletion and denudation of the GBM. Tubular microcysts form due to tubular epithelial cell proliferation and dysregulation. Tubuloreticular inclusions on EM are composed of IFN-induced proteins and are characteristic of HIVAN (and also seen in lupus and interferon therapy). Interstitial inflammation and fibrosis develop secondary to proteinuria and viral protein-mediated injury.

### 2.5 Clinical Presentation

MOST COMMON: Nephrotic-range proteinuria (UPCR >3.5) — 90%. Edema (50-60%). Rapidly progressive CKD — eGFR decline of 10-20 mL/min/month without ART. Hypertension (30-40% — less common than other CKD). Microhematuria (30-40%). Normal-sized or enlarged kidneys on ultrasound (due to tubular microcysts). Advanced: oliguric ESKD within months if untreated. ASSOCIATED: Low CD4 count (<200), high viral load, no ART, HIV wasting syndrome, opportunistic infections (CMV, PCP, TB). Extra-renal: none specific. Asymptomatic HIV: may be first presentation of HIV in some patients.

### 2.7 Differential Diagnosis

drugInducedGn, fsgs, lupus, membranous, irgn

### 2.8 Laboratory Findings

- Nephrotic-range proteinuria: UPCR >3.5 g/g (90%)
- Microscopic hematuria (30-40%)
- Rapidly declining eGFR: 10-20 mL/min/month decline without ART
- Serum creatinine: rising rapidly
- HIV ELISA + Western blot: positive
- CD4 count: often <200 cells/uL
- HIV viral load: often >100,000 copies/mL
- Albumin: low (nephrotic range)
- APOL1 genotyping: G1/G1, G1/G2, or G2/G2 risk alleles
- Renal ultrasound: enlarged kidneys (10-12 cm) due to microcysts
- Serology: rule out HBV, HCV, syphilis co-infection

### 2.9 Biopsy Findings

- LM: COLLAPSING FSGS — segmental or global collapse of glomerular capillary loops with overlying podocyte hypertrophy and hyperplasia (pseudocrescents)
- LM: TUBULAR MICROCYSTS — marked dilatation of proximal tubules up to 5x normal diameter
- LM: Tubular epithelial cell degeneration and regeneration
- LM: Interstitial fibrosis and inflammation (variable, proportional to CKD stage)
- LM: No significant immune complex deposition (distinguishes from HIV-IC GN)
- IF: Negative or nonspecific IgM and C3 in sclerotic segments
- IF: No immune complex deposits (rule out HIV-immune complex GN)
- EM: TUBULORETICULAR INCLUSIONS — 20-30 nm interanastomosing tubular structures in endothelial cell cytoplasm (characteristic but not pathognomonic)
- EM: Podocyte foot process effacement (extensive)
- EM: Glomerular capillary collapse with wrinkling and folding of GBM
- EM: No electron-dense immune deposits

| No ART at presentation | Rapid progression to ESKD within 6-12 months | High |

| African ancestry | APOL1-dependent risk | Moderate |

### 2.12 Treatment Overview

Cornerstone: combined antiretroviral therapy (ART) — suppresses HIV viremia, reduces podocyte injury, and can reverse HIVAN. Start ART immediately regardless of CD4 count. RAASi (ACEi/ARB): reduce proteinuria by 40-60% and slow CKD progression. Corticosteroids: limited role — may benefit collapsing GN with rapid decline despite ART (KDIGO 2D). No role for other immunosuppression (may worsen HIV). Dialysis: ESKD requires HD or PD — HIV patients have acceptable survival on dialysis. Kidney transplant: excellent outcomes in well-controlled HIV (undetectable viral load, CD4 >200). HIV is NOT a contraindication to transplant in the ART era. ESKD preparation should include transplant evaluation. NSAIDs: contraindicated (AKI risk).

### 2.13 Treatment Algorithm

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1 | Confirm diagnosis: renal biopsy (collapsing FSGS, microcysts, TRI on EM). HIV serology (if not known) |
| 2 | Start ART immediately: INSTI-based regimen (dolutegravir, bictegravir) + dual NRTI backbone. TDF/TAF are nephrotoxic alternatives |
| 3 | Start RAASi: ACEi/ARB for proteinuria >=0.5 g/day. Titrate to max tolerated dose |
| 4 | BP management: target <130/80. Add CCB/thiazide if needed |
| 5 | Consider prednisone 1 mg/kg/day x8 wks for rapidly progressive collapsing GN despite ART (limited evidence) |
| 6 | ESKD preparation: RRT planning when eGFR <20. HIV patients acceptable for HD, PD, and transplant |
| 7 | Kidney transplant evaluation: undetectable viral load, CD4 >200 for >6 months. Excellent graft and patient survival |
| 8 | Monitor: HIV viral load, CD4, eGFR, UPCR — Q1-3 months |

### 2.14 Monitoring Protocol

Active HIVAN: monthly: HIV viral load, CD4, eGFR, UPCR, BP. On ART: Q3 months if stable. On RAASi: K+, Cr at 2-4 wks then Q3 months. Post-transplant: standard transplant monitoring + HIV viral load Q1-3 months. Nephrology + HIV/infectious disease co-management. ACEi/ARB monitoring: K+, Cr in 2-4 wks.

### 2.15 Complications

- ESKD — rapid progression within months without ART
- ART nephrotoxicity (TDF: tubular toxicity; TAF: safer; ATV: stones; tenofovir: AKI)
- Immune reconstitution inflammatory syndrome (IRIS) on starting ART
- HIV-associated immune complex GN (different entity)
- Co-infection with HBV, HCV, TB — worsened by immunosuppression
- Cardiovascular disease — HIV-related inflammation + CKD
- Steroid-induced immunosuppression — worsens HIV (CD4 decline) when combined with low CD4

### 2.16 Relapse Information

HIVAN is not relapsing-remitting. With ART initiation, proteinuria may decrease by 50-60% within 3-6 months and eGFR may stabilise or improve. However, if scarring is advanced (IFTA >30%), renal function rarely recovers — the goal is to slow further decline. ART non-adherence leads to virologic rebound and worsening proteinuria. HIVAN can recur post-transplant only if HIV is uncontrolled — transplant in well-controlled HIV has NO recurrence risk (donor kidney is not infected by HIV).

### 2.17 Long-Term Prognosis

Pre-ART: ESKD within 6-12 months — almost universal. Post-ART: 5-year renal survival 50-70% if eGFR >30 at diagnosis. If eGFR <30 at diagnosis: 50% ESKD at 1 year despite ART. ART adherence is the single most important factor. On dialysis: HIV patients have 2-year survival 75-85% (worse than non-HIV if advanced AIDS). Transplant: 5-year graft survival 70-80%, patient survival 85-90% — excellent in selected patients with controlled HIV.

### 2.18 Evidence Summary

No RCTs specific to HIVAN — all evidence from observational cohorts. ART: landmark studies showing HIVAN incidence decline from 10% to 2% with ART era; ESKD risk reduced 60-80%. RAASi: observational data showing 40-60% proteinuria reduction and ESKD delay. APOL1: discovery by Genovese et al. (2010, Science) revolutionised understanding of HIVAN and collapsing GN genetics. Transplant outcomes: NIH HIV-Tx study (n=150 HIV+ kidney transplant recipients) — 5-year graft survival 70%, patient survival 88%.
