### Case 1: Primary Membranous with PLA2R Positivity (Typical)

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Case ID** | CASE-MEMB-TYP-001 |
| **Presentation Type** | `typical` |
| **Gold Standard** | Yes |
| **Source** | Seed file `seed_clinical_cases.py` |

**Summary:**

- 48-year-old male presenting with progressive lower extremity edema over 6 weeks, frothy urine
- BP 132/78, UPCR 6.2, serum albumin 2.1 g/dL, eGFR 82
- Anti-PLA2R positive at 420 RU/mL; anti-THSD7A negative
- Biopsy: Stage II membranous with granular IgG4+ deposits on IF, subepithelial deposits on EM
- Negative secondary workup (ANA, HBV/HCV, HIV, malignancy screen)
- KDIGO moderate risk; treated with rituximab 1g IV x2 2 weeks apart
- Outcome: Complete remission by 9 months (UPCR 0.2, albumin 3.8, anti-PLA2R undetectable)

**Key Teaching Points:**

- Classic primary MN presentation with nephrotic syndrome
- Anti-PLA2R titer correlation with disease activity and treatment response
- Rituximab as first-line therapy per MENTOR trial evidence
- Secondary workup negative confirming primary MN

### Case 2: Progressive Membranous with High PLA2R Titres (Rapid)

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Case ID** | CASE-MEMB-RAP-002 |
| **Presentation Type** | `rapid` |
| **Gold Standard** | Yes |

**Summary:**

- 52-year-old male with rapid onset nephrotic syndrome over 4 weeks
- BP 138/85, UPCR 12.5, albumin 1.6 g/dL, eGFR 45
- Anti-PLA2R extremely elevated at 1800 RU/mL
- Biopsy: Stage III membranous with spike formation, moderate tubular atrophy (T1)
- High KDIGO risk (eGFR<60, UPCR>8, albumin<2.0)
- Treated with modified Ponticelli regimen (CyP + prednisolone alternating monthly)
- Required anticoagulation for VTE prophylaxis (albumin <2.0)
- Outcome: Partial remission at 12 months (UPCR 2.8, albumin 3.0, eGFR 52); anti-PLA2R decreased to 85 RU/mL

**Key Teaching Points:**

- Ultra-high PLA2R titers predict low spontaneous remission and aggressive course
- High KDIGO risk justifies more intensive immunosuppression
- Modified Ponticelli regimen as alternative when rapid disease control needed
- VTE prophylaxis mandatory with severe hypoalbuminemia

### Case 3: Relapsing Membranous After Rituximab (Relapse)

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Case ID** | CASE-MEMB-REL-003 |
| **Presentation Type** | `relapse` |
| **Gold Standard** | Yes |

**Summary:**

- 44-year-old female initially achieved complete remission after rituximab 1g x2
- Relapsed 30 months post-rituximab: UPCR increased from 0.3 to 4.8, albumin dropped to 2.5
- Anti-PLA2R became detectable again (280 RU/mL) 4 months before clinical relapse
- CD19/20 count: reconstituted at 22 months post-rituximab
- Re-treated with rituximab 375mg/m2 x4 weekly doses
- Outcome: Partial remission at 6 months, anti-PLA2R declining

**Key Teaching Points:**

- Rising anti-PLA2R titers predict clinical relapse by 3-6 months
- Relapse after rituximab occurs in 25-40% at 5 years
- Repeat rituximab effective in majority of relapsers
- PLA2R-guided monitoring enables early intervention

### Case 4: Treatment-Resistant Membranous Post Cyclophosphamide (Resistant)

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Case ID** | CASE-MEMB-RES-004 |
| **Presentation Type** | `resistant` |
| **Gold Standard** | Yes |

**Summary:**

- 56-year-old male with persistent nephrotic syndrome despite modified Ponticelli regimen
- Baseline: anti-PLA2R 920 RU/mL, UPCR 8.5, eGFR 55
- Completed 6-month CyP+prednisolone protocol; minimal response (UPCR 6.0, anti-PLA2R 650)
- Additional course of rituximab 1g x2 attempted; anti-PLA2R persisted at 520
- Repeat biopsy: Stage III-IV changes, 40% interstitial fibrosis (T2), no active cellular infiltrates
- High anti-PLA2R continued despite 2 lines of immunosuppression
- eGFR declining to 32 over 18 months
- Transitioned to supportive care; started transplant evaluation; continued MMF as steroid-sparing

**Key Teaching Points:**

- Treatment resistance defined as failure after >=2 lines of immunosuppression
- Re-biopsy critical to distinguish active disease from chronic irreversible damage
- High anti-PLA2R despite treatment predicts poor renal outcome
- Early transplant referral when eGFR declining despite therapy
- 50% of resistant MN progress to ESKD within 5-10 years

### Case 5: Membranous Secondary to Malignancy (Atypical)

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Case ID** | CASE-MEMB-ATY-005 |
| **Presentation Type** | `atypical` |
| **Gold Standard** | Yes |

**Summary:**

- 68-year-old male presenting with nephrotic syndrome, 8kg weight loss, fatigue
- UPCR 7.2, albumin 2.2, eGFR 65
- Anti-PLA2R and anti-THSD7A both negative
- Biopsy: Stage I-II membranous with IgG1/IgG2 dominant deposits (atypical IgG subclass)
- Age-appropriate malignancy workup: colonoscopy revealed adenocarcinoma of the colon
- Diagnosis: Paraneoplastic MN secondary to colon adenocarcinoma
- Treated with surgical resection (right hemicolectomy) and oncologic follow-up
- Nephrotic syndrome improved post-resection: UPCR declined to 1.6 at 6 months without immunosuppression

**Key Teaching Points:**

- Anti-PLA2R negative MN in an older patient should raise suspicion for malignancy
- IgG1/IgG2 dominant subclass suggests secondary etiology
- Paraneoplastic MN may remit after tumor treatment alone
- THSD7A-associated MN particularly associated with malignancy (10-20%)

### Case 6: Pediatric Membranous with HBV Association (Special/Pediatric)

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Case ID** | CASE-MEMB-PED-006 |
| **Presentation Type** | `special` |
| **Gold Standard** | Yes |

**Summary:**

- 12-year-old male from HBV-endemic region presenting with edema, frothy urine
- UPCR 5.5, albumin 2.0, eGFR 95
- Anti-PLA2R negative; anti-THSD7A negative
- HBsAg positive, HBeAg positive, HBV DNA 6.5 log10 IU/mL
- Biopsy: Stage I-II membranous with full-house IF pattern (IgG, IgM, IgA, C3, C1q)
- EM: subepithelial deposits with tubuloreticular inclusions
- Diagnosis: Secondary MN due to chronic HBV infection
- Treated with entecavir antiviral therapy; no immunosuppression given
- Outcome: UPCR improved to 0.8, albumin normalized at 12 months with HBV DNA suppression

**Key Teaching Points:**

- HBV-associated MN is the most common cause of pediatric MN in endemic regions
- Full-house IF pattern characteristic of HBV-associated MN
- Tubuloreticular inclusions on EM suggest viral etiology
- Antiviral therapy is the cornerstone; immunosuppression avoided in HBV-MN

### Case 7: Membranous with Renal Vein Thrombosis (Complications)

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Case ID** | CASE-MEMB-COM-007 |
| **Presentation Type** | `complications` |
| **Gold Standard** | Yes |

**Summary:**

- 42-year-old male with known primary MN (anti-PLA2R+, UPCR 8.0, albumin 1.8)
- Developed acute-onset left flank pain, macroscopic hematuria, worsening edema
- CT angiogram: left renal vein thrombosis with extension to IVC
- D-dimer markedly elevated; Doppler confirmed thrombus
- Diagnosed with nephrotic syndrome-associated renal vein thrombosis
- Treated with therapeutic LMWH bridged to warfarin (target INR 2-3)
- Anticoagulation continued indefinitely given persistent nephrotic syndrome
- Concurrently treated with rituximab for underlying MN
- Outcome: Symptomatic improvement, thrombus stable at 3 months

**Key Teaching Points:**

- MN has highest VTE risk among all glomerulonephritides (5-15%)
- Renal vein thrombosis is a classic complication of MN
- Serum albumin <2.5 g/dL is the strongest predictor of VTE in MN
- Anticoagulation for established VTE: minimum 6 months, indefinite if persistent nephrotic syndrome
- Acute presentation with flank pain and hematuria should prompt VTE evaluation

### Case 8: Spontaneous Remission of Membranous (Remission)

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| **Case ID** | CASE-MEMB-REM-008 |
| **Presentation Type** | `remission` |
| **Gold Standard** | Yes |

**Summary:**

- 38-year-old female with incidental nephrotic-range proteinuria found on routine labs
- Asymptomatic aside from mild ankle edema; UPCR 4.2, albumin 2.8, eGFR 95
- Anti-PLA2R moderately elevated at 180 RU/mL
- Biopsy: Stage I membranous with small subepithelial deposits
- KDIGO low-moderate risk (eGFR>60, UPCR<4.5, albumin>2.5)
- Decision: Conservative management with RAASi alone, active surveillance
- Over 18 months: UPCR gradually declined to 0.4, albumin normalized to 3.8
- Anti-PLA2R became undetectable at 14 months
- Outcome: Complete spontaneous remission without immunosuppression

**Key Teaching Points:**

- Spontaneous remission occurs in 20-30% of primary MN within 2 years
- Lower PLA2R titers (<200 RU/mL) predict higher likelihood of spontaneous remission
- Low-risk patients can safely undergo active surveillance for 6-12 months
- PLA2R titer decline precedes clinical remission
- KDIGO risk stratification accurately identifies candidates for watchful waiting
