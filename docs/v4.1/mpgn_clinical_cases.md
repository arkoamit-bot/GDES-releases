## 1. C3 Glomerulopathy with Progressive CKD

### 1.1 Presentation

A 28-year-old man with known hypocomplementemic GN (diagnosed 3 years ago, C3G on biopsy) now presents with worsening proteinuria and declining eGFR despite RAASi. Previous steroid trial was ineffective.

### 1.2 Examination

BP 138/86 mmHg. 1+ bilateral edema. No rash. Fundoscopy: normal. No neuropathy.

### 1.3 Diagnosis

C3 Glomerulopathy (C3G) with progressive CKD — C3NeF positive

### 1.4 Management

Empagliflozin 10 mg added. Losartan 100 mg. Eculizumab 900 mg IV weekly x4 then 1200 mg q2w. Meningococcal vaccination (ACWY + MenB). Prednisone 40 mg daily (short course during eculizumab induction).

### 1.5 Outcome

Month 1: UPCR 3.2, eGFR 38. Month 3: UPCR 2.8, eGFR 40. Month 6: UPCR 1.5, eGFR 42. C3 improved to 52. Month 12: UPCR 0.8, eGFR 44. CKD progression slowed. Continuing eculizumab.

### 1.6 Teaching Points

Key diagnostic features: C3 Glomerulopathy (C3G) with progressive CKD — C3NeF positive

## 2. Lupus Nephritis Class III/IV (IC-MPGN Pattern)

### 2.1 Presentation

A 26-year-old woman with known SLE (malar rash, arthritis, ANA positive) presents with edema, hypertension, and proteinuria. Not on immunosuppression.

### 2.2 Examination

BP 144/90 mmHg. Malar rash. Oral ulcers. 2+ edema. Joint tenderness in hands and knees.

### 2.3 Diagnosis

Lupus Nephritis Class IV-G (A) with IC-MPGN pattern — active disease

### 2.4 Management

Methylprednisolone 1 g IV x3 then prednisone 60 mg daily taper. MMF 1.5 g BID. Hydroxychloroquine 400 mg daily. Ramipril 5 mg. Atorvastatin 20 mg.

### 2.5 Outcome

Month 1: UPCR 2.8, Cr 1.2, C3/C4 improving. Month 3: UPCR 1.2, Cr 1.0, C3 normalising. Month 6: UPCR 0.4, Cr 0.9, anti-dsDNA negative. Renal remission achieved. Prednisone tapered to 5 mg. Continuing MMF, HCQ.

### 2.6 Teaching Points

Key diagnostic features: Lupus Nephritis Class IV-G (A) with IC-MPGN pattern — active disease

## 3. Crescentic IC-MPGN Presenting as RPGN

### 3.1 Presentation

A 45-year-old man presents with 10-day history of oliguria, dark urine, and rapidly rising Cr from 1.0 to 4.5. No identified underlying cause. No HCV, ANA negative, SPEP normal.

### 3.2 Examination

BP 168/102 mmHg. Pulmonary edema on CXR. 2+ edema. Confused (possible hypertensive encephalopathy).

### 3.3 Diagnosis

Crescentic IC-MPGN presenting as RPGN — 55% crescents

### 3.4 Management

Pulse methylprednisolone 1 g IV x3 days. IV cyclophosphamide 750 mg/m2. Prednisone 60 mg daily taper. Plasma exchange x5 sessions. Haemodialysis initiated (3 sessions). Ramipril 5 mg.

### 3.5 Outcome

Week 2: dialysis discontinued (urine output returned). Month 1: Cr 2.8, UPCR 3.5. Month 3: Cr 2.0, UPCR 1.2, C3 improving. Month 6: Cr 1.8 (eGFR 38), UPCR 0.6. Month 12: Cr 1.6 (eGFR 42), UPCR 0.4. Stage 3b CKD.

### 3.6 Teaching Points

Key diagnostic features: Crescentic IC-MPGN presenting as RPGN — 55% crescents

## 4. Long-Term Remission After Treated HCV-MPGN

### 4.1 Presentation

A 60-year-old woman now 4 years after DAA therapy for HCV-associated IC-MPGN. She received glecaprevir/pibrentasvir 12 weeks and achieved SVR12 with complete renal remission (UPCR 0.2, eGFR 65). Now on annual follow-up.

### 4.2 Examination

BP 122/76 mmHg (on losartan 50 mg). No edema. No purpura. HCV RNA undetectable. LFT normal.

### 4.3 Diagnosis

Sustained remission of HCV-associated IC-MPGN 4 years post-DAA therapy

### 4.4 Management

Losartan 50 mg daily. No immunosuppression. Annual screening: Cr, UPCR, BP, HCV RNA, LFT. Liver ultrasound for HCC surveillance (resolved cirrhosis on FibroScan).

### 4.5 Outcome

Stable UPCR <0.2, eGFR 65-70, Cr 0.9-1.0 over 4 years. HCV RNA undetectable. Cryoglobulins negative. No relapse. Annual surveillance ongoing.

### 4.6 Teaching Points

Key diagnostic features: Sustained remission of HCV-associated IC-MPGN 4 years post-DAA therapy

## 5. IC-MPGN Secondary to Monoclonal Gammopathy

### 5.1 Presentation

A 68-year-old woman presents with progressive leg edema, foamy urine, and fatigue. Known IgG kappa MGUS (M-spike 1.2 g/dL) monitored for 3 years. No HCV, no SLE. Hb 11.0 g/dL.

### 5.2 Examination

BP 140/84 mmHg. 2+ bilateral pitting edema to thighs. No purpura or neuropathy. Mild splenomegaly.

### 5.3 Diagnosis

IC-MPGN secondary to IgG kappa monoclonal gammopathy of renal significance (MGRS)

### 5.4 Management

Daratumumab + bortezomib + dexamethasone (Dara-Vd) 4 cycles. Ramipril 10 mg. Furosemide 40 mg PRN. Zoledronic acid for bone protection.

### 5.5 Outcome

Month 3: M-spike reduced 60%, UPCR 2.5, eGFR 50. Month 6: M-spike 0.3 g/dL (VGPR), UPCR 0.8, eGFR 52. Month 12: UPCR 0.4, eGFR 50, haemato logic VGPR maintained. Renal remission.

### 5.6 Teaching Points

Key diagnostic features: IC-MPGN secondary to IgG kappa monoclonal gammopathy of renal significance (MGRS)

## 6. HCV-Associated IC-MPGN (Cryoglobulinemic)

### 6.1 Presentation

A 55-year-old man with chronic HCV (genotype 1b, untreated) presents with purpura, arthralgia, proteinuria, and declining renal function. He has peripheral neuropathy in his feet.

### 6.2 Examination

BP 146/90 mmHg. Palpable purpura on lower legs. 1+ edema. Decreased pinprick in stocking distribution. Mild hepatomegaly.

### 6.3 Diagnosis

HCV-Associated IC-MPGN (Cryoglobulinemic MPGN)

### 6.4 Management

Glecaprevir/pibrentasvir 12 weeks. Rituximab 375 mg/m2 weekly x4 for severe vasculitis. Methylprednisolone 500 mg IV x3 then prednisone taper. Ramipril 5 mg.

### 6.5 Outcome

Month 3: HCV RNA undetectable, UPCR 2.0, eGFR 48, purpura resolved. Month 6: SVR12, UPCR 0.8, Cr 1.4, C4 normalising. Month 12: UPCR 0.3, eGFR 52, C4 normal. Renal remission.

### 6.6 Teaching Points

Key diagnostic features: HCV-Associated IC-MPGN (Cryoglobulinemic MPGN)

## 7. Idiopathic IC-MPGN with Nephrotic Syndrome

### 7.1 Presentation

A 32-year-old woman presents with 3-week history of progressive leg and periorbital edema, foamy urine, and fatigue. No history of HCV, SLE, or monoclonal gammopathy. No rash or arthralgia. No recent infections.

### 7.2 Examination

BP 142/88 mmHg. Bilateral 2+ pitting edema to thighs. Periorbital edema. Ascites (mild). JVP normal. No purpura or synovitis.

### 7.3 Diagnosis

Idiopathic Immune Complex-Mediated MPGN (IC-MPGN) with nephrotic syndrome

### 7.4 Management

Prednisone 60 mg daily (0.9 mg/kg). MMF 1 g BID. Ramipril 5 mg. Atorvastatin 20 mg. Furosemide 40 mg for edema. TMP/SMX for PCP prophylaxis.

### 7.5 Outcome

Month 1: UPCR 3.8, edema improved. Month 3: UPCR 1.8, eGFR 68, C3 62. Month 6: UPCR 0.6, eGFR 72, C3 normal. Prednisone tapered to 20 mg. Month 12: Remission (UPCR 0.3, eGFR 75). Prednisone 5 mg. Continuing MMF.

### 7.6 Teaching Points

Key diagnostic features: Idiopathic Immune Complex-Mediated MPGN (IC-MPGN) with nephrotic syndrome

## 8. Paediatric C3G with C3NeF

### 8.1 Presentation

A 10-year-old boy presents with 2-week history of periorbital edema, foamy urine, and reduced urine output. No prior renal history. No family history of kidney disease.

### 8.2 Examination

BP 128/76 mmHg (P95). Periorbital edema. 1+ bilateral leg edema. Normal growth parameters.

### 8.3 Diagnosis

Paediatric C3 Glomerulopathy (C3G) with C3NeF — moderate severity

### 8.4 Management

RAASi (enalapril 10 mg). Prednisone 40 mg/m2/day x8 weeks then taper. MMF 600 mg/m2 BID. Statin not indicated (age). Monitor C3, C3NeF, UPCR monthly.

### 8.5 Outcome

Month 1: UPCR 3.0, C3 28. Month 3: UPCR 1.2, C3 45. Month 6: UPCR 0.5, C3 62, C3NeF persisting. Prednisone off. MMF continued. Month 12: UPCR 0.2, eGFR 82. Remission. Long-term monitoring for relapse.

### 8.6 Teaching Points

Key diagnostic features: Paediatric C3 Glomerulopathy (C3G) with C3NeF — moderate severity
