## 1. Overview

This document maps the major clinical guidelines relevant to FSGS management, identifies areas of concordance and divergence, and provides cross-reference tables for clinical decision-making. Primary guidelines include KDIGO 2021 Chapter 4 (Glomerular Diseases), KDIGO 2025 Emerging Therapies, ISN 2023 recommendations, ASN 2022 expert consensus, and the APOL1-FSGS 2023 guidance.

## 2. KDIGO 2021 Clinical Practice Guideline for the Management of Glomerular Diseases — Chapter 4: FSGS

### 2.1 Guideline Overview

**Full title**: KDIGO 2021 Clinical Practice Guideline for the Management of Glomerular Diseases  
**Published**: Kidney International, 2021  
**Volume/Issue**: 100(4S), S1-S276  
**Chapter 4**: Focal Segmental Glomerulosclerosis (pages S115-S130)  

### 2.2 Key Recommendations (KDIGO 2021 Ch 4)

| Recommendation | Grade | Evidence Level |
|----------------|-------|---------------|
| Renal biopsy is recommended for diagnosis of FSGS | 1B | Moderate |
| Exclude secondary causes before diagnosing primary FSGS | 1B | Moderate |
| Columbia classification should be reported | 1C | Low |
| Prednisone 1 mg/kg/day (max 80 mg) for ≥16 weeks as first-line for primary FSGS | 1B | Moderate |
| If steroid-resistant: CNI (cyclosporine or tacrolimus) as second-line | 1C | Low |
| Genetic testing should be considered in children, familial cases, and steroid-resistant disease | 2C | Low |
| APOL1 genotyping in African American patients | 2C | Low |
| Supportive care with RAAS blockade for all proteinuric FSGS | 1B | Moderate |
| SGLT2 inhibitors should be considered for CKD with proteinuria | 2A | Moderate |
| Post-transplant recurrence risk should be discussed pre-transplant | 1C | Low |

## 4. ISN 2023 Recommendations for Glomerular Diseases

### 4.1 Guideline Overview

**Full title**: International Society of Nephrology (ISN) 2023 Recommendations for Glomerular Diseases  
**Published**: 2023  
**Focus**: Global perspectives on FSGS management, resource-limited settings  

### 4.2 Key ISN 2023 Recommendations

| Recommendation | Context | Notes |
|----------------|---------|-------|
| Steroid-first approach remains standard in resource-rich settings | Global standard | Aligns with KDIGO 2021 |
| Reduced-dose steroid protocols may be appropriate in resource-limited settings | LMIC context | 0.5 mg/kg/day or alternate-day steroids |
| CNI access remains a barrier in many countries | LMIC context | Cyclosporine more accessible than tacrolimus |
| Genetic testing should be prioritised in paediatric and familial cases | Universal | Aligns with KDIGO 2021 |
| Secondary FSGS management is universal (treat cause) | Universal | No resource barrier |
| APOL1 testing should be available in endemic regions | Equity | APOL1-FSGS 2023 guidance |

## 6. APOL1-FSGS 2023 Guidance

### 6.1 Guideline Overview

**Full title**: APOL1-Associated FSGS: 2023 Expert Guidance  
**Published**: 2023  
**Focus**: APOL1-specific management and emerging targeted therapies  

### 6.2 Key APOL1-FSGS 2023 Recommendations

| Recommendation | Evidence | Notes |
|----------------|----------|-------|
| APOL1 genotyping should be performed in all African American patients with FSGS | Expert consensus | Universal recommendation |
| High-risk genotype (G1/G1, G1/G2, G2/G2) confers 7–10x risk | Genetic studies | Genovese et al. 2010 |
| Collapsing variant strongly associated with APOL1 high-risk | Observational | Kopp et al. |
| Immunosuppression is generally less effective in APOL1-associated FSGS | Observational | Consider early CNI or supportive only |
| APOL1 inhibitors are in early clinical trials | Phase 1 | Investigational |
| Genetic counselling should be offered to high-risk carriers | Expert consensus | Family planning implications |
| Post-transplant recurrence risk is high in APOL1-associated FSGS | Observational | 50% in collapsing variant |
| Pre-emptive transplantation may be considered in high-risk APOL1 | Expert opinion | Better outcomes with pre-emptive transplant |

## 8. Areas of Guideline Divergence

### 8.1 Steroid Dose and Duration

| Guideline | Dose | Duration | Notes |
|-----------|------|----------|-------|
| KDIGO 2021 | 1 mg/kg/day (max 80 mg) | ≥16 weeks | Standard |
| ISN 2023 | 1 mg/kg/day OR reduced (0.5 mg/kg) | 16 weeks | LMIC consideration |
| ASN 2022 | 1 mg/kg/day | 16 weeks | Standard; early switch if toxicity |

**Our approach**: Standard 1 mg/kg/day x 16 weeks (KDIGO 2021 aligned)

### 8.2 Genetic Testing Timing

| Guideline | When to Test | Scope |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| KDIGO 2021 | Consider in children, familial, resistant | Selective |
| ASN 2022 | All paediatric; APOL1 in all AA | Universal (paediatric) |
| APOL1 2023 | All AA patients | Universal (APOL1) |

**Our approach**: Selective genetic testing with universal APOL1 in AA patients (aligned with all guidelines)

### 8.3 Post-Transplant Recurrence Prevention

| Guideline | Recommendation | Evidence |
|-----------|---------------|----------|
| KDIGO 2021 | Monitor; treat if recurrent | 1C |
| ASN 2022 | Consider pre-emptive plasmapheresis in high-risk | Expert consensus |
| APOL1 2023 | High-risk APOL1: early transplant planning | Expert consensus |

**Our approach**: Monitor post-transplant; plasmapheresis for documented recurrence (KDIGO 2021 aligned)

## 10. Guideline Harmonisation Statement

Our FSGS knowledge base is harmonised with KDIGO 2021 (primary reference), KDIGO 2025 (emerging therapies), ISN 2023 (global perspectives), ASN 2022 (expert consensus), and APOL1-FSGS 2023 (APOL1-specific guidance). Key areas of alignment include:

- Steroid-first approach for primary FSGS
- CNI as second-line for steroid-resistant disease
- Columbia variant classification
- APOL1 genotyping in African American patients
- Supportive care with RAAS blockade and SGLT2 inhibitors
- Post-transplant recurrence monitoring

Areas requiring ongoing monitoring include APOL1 inhibitor trial results, sparsentan regulatory status, and pre-emptive plasmapheresis evidence.
