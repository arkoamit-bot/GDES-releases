## 1. Document Purpose

This document provides the complete drug knowledge specification for C3 Glomerulopathy pharmacotherapy. It covers immunosuppressive agents, complement inhibitors, plasma therapy, supportive medications, adverse effect profiles, drug monitoring requirements, and vaccination guidance. C3G has no FDA-approved therapy; all agents are used off-label.

## 3. Corticosteroids

### 3.1 Indication
Active/proliferative C3GN; induction therapy combined with MMF or cyclophosphamide.

### 3.2 Dosing

| Formulation | Dose | Route | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| Methylprednisolone | 0.5-1 g/day | IV | Daily x 3 days (pulse) |
| Prednisone | 0.5-1 mg/kg/day (max 80 mg) | PO | Daily, then taper over 6-12 months |

### 3.3 Adverse Effects

| System | Effect | Management |
|---|---|---|
| Metabolic | Hyperglycaemia, diabetes | Glucose monitoring; insulin if needed |
| Musculoskeletal | Osteoporosis, avascular necrosis | DEXA scan; calcium/vitamin D; bisphosphonates |
| Ophthalmic | Cataracts, glaucoma | Annual eye exam |
| Cardiovascular | Hypertension, fluid retention | BP monitoring; sodium restriction |
| Immunologic | Increased infection risk | PCP prophylaxis; vaccination |
| Growth (children) | Growth retardation | Growth velocity monitoring |

## 4. Mycophenolate Mofetil (MMF)

### 4.1 Indication
First-line immunosuppression for active C3GN; steroid-sparing agent.

### 4.2 Dosing

| Dose | Route | Frequency | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1-2 g/day (renal-adjusted) | PO | Divided BID | 6-24 months |

### 4.3 Adverse Effects
GI (nausea, diarrhoea), cytopenias (leucopenia, anaemia), increased infection risk (BK virus, CMV), teratogenicity.

### 4.4 Monitoring
CBC monthly; consider MPA level monitoring in transplant setting.

---

## 5. Cyclophosphamide

### 5.1 Indication
Induction therapy for crescentic C3G / RPGN.

### 5.2 Dosing

| Regimen | Dose | Route | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| IV pulse | 500-750 mg/m2 | IV | Monthly x 3-6 |
| Oral | 2 mg/kg/day (max 100 mg) | PO | Daily x 3 months |

### 5.3 Adverse Effects
Haemorrhagic cystitis, bone marrow suppression, gonadal toxicity (cumulative), malignancy risk (cumulative).

### 5.4 Monitoring
CBC weekly; urinalysis for haematuria; cumulative dose tracking (max 36 g lifetime).

---

## 6. Rituximab

### 6.1 Indication
C3NeF-positive C3G; autoantibody-mediated disease.

### 6.2 Dosing

| Regimen | Dose | Route | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| Standard | 375 mg/m2 | IV | Weekly x 2-4 doses |

### 6.3 Adverse Effects
Infusion reactions, serum sickness, hypogammaglobulinemia, HBV reactivation, PML (rare).

### 6.4 Monitoring
CD19/20 counts, IgG levels, HBV screening (HBsAg, HBcAb).

---

## 7. Eculizumab (Anti-C5)

### 7.1 Indication
Refractory C3G with evidence of terminal complement activation.

### 7.2 Dosing

| Phase | Dose | Route | Frequency |
|---|---|---|---|
| Induction | 900 mg | IV | Weekly x 4 |
| Maintenance | 1200 mg | IV | Every 2 weeks |

### 7.3 Adverse Effects
Meningococcal infection (black box warning), Neisseria infections, infusion reactions.

### 7.4 Monitoring
CH50 (target <10%), meningococcal vaccination mandatory, amoxicillin prophylaxis considered.

---

## 8. Iptacopan (Factor B Inhibitor)

### 8.1 Indication
Refractory C3G; targets alternative pathway at factor B.

### 8.2 Dosing
200 mg PO BID (investigational).

### 8.3 Adverse Effects
Upper respiratory infection, headache, GI disturbance; meningococcal risk (theoretical).

---

## 9. Pegcetacoplan (C3 Inhibitor)

### 9.1 Indication
Refractory C3G; proximal complement blockade.

### 9.2 Dosing
1080 mg SC twice weekly (investigational).

### 9.3 Monitoring
Meningococcal vaccination; re-biopsy for histologic response assessment.

---

## 10. Plasma Therapy

### 10.1 Fresh Frozen Plasma (FFP)
- **Indication:** CFH mutation with factor H deficiency
- **Dose:** 10-20 mL/kg IV every 1-2 weeks
- **Rationale:** Replaces deficient complement regulatory factors

### 10.2 Plasma Exchange (PLEX)
- **Indication:** Anti-CFH autoantibody positive, severe disease
- **Dose:** 1.5 volume exchanges x 5-7 sessions
- **Rationale:** Removes pathogenic autoantibodies

## 12. Vaccination Guidance

| Vaccine | Timing | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Meningococcal (quadrivalent ACWY) | Before complement inhibitor | Mandatory; boost every 5 years |
| Meningococcal B | Before complement inhibitor | Mandatory; 2-dose series |
| Pneumococcal (PCV20 + PPSV23) | Before immunosuppression | Follow CDC schedule |
| Influenza (inactivated) | Annual | Safe on immunosuppression |
| COVID-19 | Per guidelines | Reduced response on MMF/rituximab |
| Live vaccines | Contraindicated | During active immunosuppression |
