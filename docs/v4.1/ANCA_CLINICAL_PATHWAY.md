**Document ID**: ANCA_CP  
**Version**: 1.0  
**Date**: 2026-07-10  
**Domain**: ANCA-Associated Pauci-Immune Glomerulonephritis  
**Classification**: Clinical Pathway — 6-Stage Model  

---

## 1. Overview

This document defines the complete 6-stage clinical pathway for ANCA-associated vasculitis with renal involvement, from initial diagnosis through ESKD and transplantation. The pathway integrates serotype-specific considerations, histological classification (Berden system), PLEX branching, rituximab versus cyclophosphamide decision points, avacopan integration, and evidence-based maintenance strategies. Each stage specifies actions, duration, entry/exit criteria, decision points, and responsible parties.

## 3. Stage 1: Diagnosis and Classification

**Duration**: 7 days  
**Entry Criterion**: Clinical suspicion of AAV (RPGN, pulmonary-renal syndrome, systemic vasculitis)  
**Exit Criterion**: Confirmed AAV diagnosis with serotype, histological class, and disease activity assessment  

### Actions

| # | Action | Timeline | Responsible |
|---|--------|----------|-------------|
| 1.1 | Urgent renal biopsy (minimum 8 glomeruli, ideally >20 with medulla) | Day 0–3 | Nephrologist/Interventional Radiologist |
| 1.2 | ANCA serology by immunoassay (PR3, MPO) and IIF (c-ANCA, p-ANCA) | Day 0–1 | Clinical Immunology |
| 1.3 | Light microscopy with crescent quantification and Berden classification | Day 3–7 | Renal Pathologist |
| 1.4 | Immunofluorescence for IgG, IgA, IgM, C3, C1q, kappa, lambda | Day 3–7 | Renal Pathologist |
| 1.5 | Electron microscopy (exclude immune complexes, evaluate GBM) | Day 5–7 | Renal Pathologist |
| 1.6 | Baseline disease activity (BVAS) and damage (VDI) assessment | Day 0–3 | Nephrologist |
| 1.7 | Extrarenal assessment: chest imaging, ENT exam, neurology screen | Day 0–7 | Multidisciplinary |

### Decision Points

- **If PR3/c-ANCA positive**: GPA phenotype; higher relapse risk; plan extended maintenance
- **If MPO/p-ANCA positive**: MPA phenotype; higher renal chronicity at diagnosis
- **If ANCA-negative by both immunoassay and IIF**: Consider biopsy review; re-test in reference lab; EGPA evaluation
- **If double-positive (ANCA + anti-GBM)**: Treat as both diseases; urgent PLEX; worst renal prognosis
- **Berden sclerotic class (>50% globally sclerotic glomeruli)**: Discuss realistic recovery expectations; prepare for ESKD pathway

## 4. Stage 2: Induction Therapy

**Duration**: 120 days  
**Entry Criterion**: Confirmed AAV diagnosis requiring immunosuppression  
**Exit Criterion**: Clinical response (improved renal function, stable or falling creatinine, reduced proteinuria, BVAS zero)  

### Actions

| # | Action | Timeline | Responsible |
|---|--------|----------|-------------|
| 2.1 | Methylprednisolone 1g IV daily x 3 pulses if severe/RPGN/DAH | Day 0–1 | Nephrologist |
| 2.2 | Rituximab 375 mg/m2 IV weekly x 4 (4 doses) OR | Day 0–28 | Nephrologist/Infusion Unit |
| 2.2a | Cyclophosphamide IV 15 mg/kg (max 1.2g) q3–4wk x 3–6 (if rituximab unavailable or contraindicicated) | Day 0–84 | Nephrologist |
| 2.3 | Prednisone 1 mg/kg/day (max 80 mg) with taper over 12–20 weeks | Day 0–120 | Nephrologist |
| 2.4 | Avacopan 30 mg BID oral x 52 weeks (consider from day 1 for steroid-sparing) | Day 1–365 | Nephrologist |
| 2.5 | PLEX: 60 mL/kg exchanges x 7 sessions over 14 days if severe DAH with hypoxaemia or anti-GBM double-positive | Day 0–14 | Apheresis Unit |
| 2.6 | TMP-SMX 800/160 mg daily or 3x/week for PJP prophylaxis | Day 0+ | Nephrologist |
| 2.7 | Proton pump inhibitor for stress ulcer prophylaxis during high-dose steroids | Day 0+ | Nephrologist |
| 2.8 | Calcium and vitamin D supplementation; bisphosphonate if high fracture risk | Day 0+ | Nephrologist |
| 2.9 | Weekly clinical assessment with BVAS; weekly creatinine/eGFR, urinalysis, CRP | Day 0–28 | Nephrology Team |
| 2.10 | Immunoglobulin monitoring: IgG, IgA, IgM at baseline and monthly (rituximab patients) | Day 0–120 | Clinical Immunology |

### Decision Points: Rituximab vs Cyclophosphamide

| Factor | Favour Rituximab | Favour Cyclophosphamide |
|--------|------------------|------------------------|
| Age < 18 years | Preferred | Not first-line |
| Reproductive age | Preferred (fertility preservation) | Gonadotoxic |
| PR3-ANCA | Preferred (higher relapse risk) | Acceptable |
| Organ-threatening disease | Acceptable | Acceptable |
| Severe DAH | Acceptable | Acceptable |
| Resource-limited setting | May be unavailable | Cost-effective |
| History of malignancy | Preferred | Contraindicated |
| Hepatitis B core Ab positive | May reactivate HBV | Acceptable |

### Decision Points: PLEX Indication

- **Severe DAH with hypoxaemia**: PLEX recommended (7 sessions over 14 days)
- **Anti-GBM double-positive**: PLEX recommended (minimum 7–10 sessions)
- **Dialysis-dependent at presentation without DAH**: PLEX not routinely indicated (PEXIVAS)
- **Severe RPGN (Cr >5.7 mg/dL) without DAH**: Consider PLEX case-by-case

### Decision Points: Avacopan Integration

- **Clinical trial-eligible patients**: Strongly consider avacopan for steroid-sparing
- **Glucocorticoid intolerance or toxicity**: Avacopan first-line adjunct
- **Newly diagnosed AAV with eGFR >30 mL/min**: ADVOCATE-eligible population
- **Dialysis-dependent**: Limited avacopan data; use with caution

## 5. Stage 3: Remission and Maintenance

**Duration**: 180 days  
**Entry Criterion**: BVAS = 0 or clinical remission achieved within Stage 2  
**Exit Criterion**: Stable remission on maintenance; transition to long-term monitoring  

### Actions

| # | Action | Timeline | Responsible |
|---|--------|----------|-------------|
| 3.1 | Initiate rituximab 500 mg IV (first maintenance dose) if rituximab used for induction | Day 0–30 | Infusion Unit |
| 3.1a | Initiate azathioprine 1–2 mg/kg/day if CyP used for induction or if rituximab maintenance chosen | Day 0–30 | Nephrologist |
| 3.2 | Continue corticosteroid taper to lowest effective dose (target 5–7.5 mg/day prednisone) | Day 1–180 | Nephrologist |
| 3.3 | Continue avacopan 30 mg BID if initiated (total course 52 weeks) | Day 1–365 | Nephrologist |
| 3.4 | Continue TMP-SMX PJP prophylaxis (at least 6–12 months post-remission) | Day 1–180+ | Nephrologist |
| 3.5 | Monthly clinical and laboratory assessment (BVAS, Cr, eGFR, urinalysis, UPCR, CRP) | Monthly x 6 | Nephrology Team |
| 3.6 | ANCA titre monitoring at each visit (especially PR3-ANCA for relapse prediction) | Monthly x 6 | Clinical Immunology |
| 3.7 | Immunoglobulin monitoring (rituximab patients): reinfuse IVIG if IgG <400 mg/dL with infections | Monthly x 6 | Clinical Immunology |
| 3.8 | Vaccination update: influenza, pneumococcal, COVID-19, HZV (avoid live vaccines) | Day 1–90 | Primary Care/Nephrology |

### Decision Points

- **If relapse during maintenance**: Return to Stage 5 (Relapse Management)
- **If stable remission at 6 months**: Transition to Stage 4 (Long-term Monitoring)
- **If azathioprine intolerance (GI, LFT elevation)**: Switch to mycophenolate mofetil 1–2 g/day
- **If TMP-SMX intolerance**: Switch to dapsone or atovaquone for PJP prophylaxis

## 6. Stage 4: Long-Term Monitoring

**Duration**: 730 days (extendable for PR3-AAV)  
**Entry Criterion**: Sustained remission for ≥6 months  
**Exit Criterion**: Completion of 2-year maintenance; transition to indefinite monitoring or Stage 5/6  

### Actions

| # | Action | Timeline | Responsible |
|---|--------|----------|-------------|
| 4.1 | Ongoing rituximab 500 mg q6mo (total 4 doses over 2 years) or continued AZA 2 mg/kg/day | q6mo | Infusion Unit |
| 4.2 | Clinical and laboratory assessment q3–6 months | q3–6mo | Nephrologist |
| 4.3 | Renal function monitoring: Cr, eGFR, urinalysis, UPCR | q3–6mo | Nephrologist |
| 4.4 | ANCA titre monitoring: every 3–6 months (PR3-AAV) | q3–6mo | Clinical Immunology |
| 4.5 | Immunoglobulin levels and B-cell count (rituximab patients) | q6mo | Clinical Immunology |
| 4.6 | Blood pressure, BMI, cardiovascular risk assessment | q6mo | Nephrologist |
| 4.7 | Steroid toxicity surveillance if on low-dose prednisone | Annually | Nephrologist |
| 4.8 | Skin cancer screening, age-appropriate malignancy surveillance | Annually | Multidisciplinary |

### Decision Points

- **PR3-ANCA with prior relapse**: Consider extended maintenance beyond 2 years (RITAZAREM protocol: rituximab q4mo or q6mo continued)
- **MPO-ANCA in sustained remission**: Consider stopping rituximab after 2 years with close monitoring
- **ANCA titre rising significantly (>2-fold)**: Increase monitoring frequency; consider pre-emptive rituximab if high risk
- **B-cell reconstitution (CD19 >10 cells/uL) after rituximab**: Consider pre-emptive redosing

## 7. Stage 5: Relapse Management

**Duration**: 90 days  
**Entry Criterion**: Confirmed relapse (major or minor) during any stage  
**Exit Criterion**: Return to remission; transition back to appropriate stage  

### Actions

| # | Action | Timeline | Responsible |
|---|--------|----------|-------------|
| 5.1 | Confirm relapse: clinical assessment (BVAS); repeat ANCA; consider re-biopsy | Day 0–7 | Nephrologist |
| 5.2 | Classify as major (organs-threatening) or minor (limited) relapse | Day 0–3 | Nephrologist |
| 5.3 | **Major relapse**: Re-induction with rituximab 375 mg/m2 weekly x 4 (or 1000 mg x 2) + steroid pulse | Day 0–28 | Nephrologist/Infusion |
| 5.4 | **Major relapse**: PLEX if severe DAH or RPGN with dialysis dependence | Day 0–14 | Apheresis Unit |
| 5.5 | **Minor relapse**: Increase/maintain immunosuppression; consider avacopan; steroid pulse | Day 0–14 | Nephrologist |
| 5.6 | Reassess avacopan candidacy if not on it | Day 0–14 | Nephrologist |
| 5.7 | Exclude infection mimicking relapse (especially if on PLEX/institution) | Day 0–7 | Nephrologist/Infectious Disease |
| 5.8 | Extend rituximab maintenance to q4mo for patients with relapsing PR3-AAV | Day 28+ | Nephrologist |
| 5.9 | Repeat renal biopsy if persistent proteinuria/creatinine rise without clear serological activity | Day 7–14 | Nephrologist |
| 5.10 | Transition back to Stage 3 or Stage 4 with adjusted maintenance plan | Day 30–90 | Nephrologist |

### Relapse Definitions

| Type | Definition | Action |
|------|------------|--------|
| Major relapse | New/worsening organ-threatening disease: renal (Cr rise 30%, active sediment, crescent), pulmonary (DAH), CNS, or severe neurological | Re-induction with rituximab + steroids + PLEX |
| Minor relapse | Skin, joint, limited ENT, or mild constitutional symptoms without organ threat | Adjust maintenance; possibly avacopan |

## 8. Stage 6: ESKD and Transplantation

**Duration**: 365 days (ongoing per cycle)  
**Entry Criterion**: Irreversible ESKD (eGFR <15 mL/min, dialysis-dependent or pre-emptive transplant candidate)  
**Exit Criterion**: Successful transplantation or sustained dialysis with AAV in remission  

### Actions

| # | Action | Timeline | Responsible |
|---|--------|----------|-------------|
| 6.1 | Initiate or continue renal replacement therapy (HD, PD) | Day 0+ | Nephrologist |
| 6.2 | Ensure AAV in complete remission before transplant listing (BVAS = 0, off immunosuppression or minimal) | Pre-transplant | Nephrologist/Transplant Team |
| 6.3 | Transplant evaluation: cardiac, infectious, malignancy, urological assessment | Day 30–180 | Transplant Team |
| 6.4 | ANCA monitoring pre- and post-transplant | q3–6mo | Clinical Immunology |
| 6.5 | Maintain low-dose immunosuppression or resume maintenance if relapsing | Ongoing | Transplant Nephrology |
| 6.6 | Monitor for recurrence: ANCA, urinalysis, protocol biopsies if indicated | Post-transplant | Transplant Nephrology |
| 6.7 | Cardiovascular risk management (ACEi/ARB, statin, aspirin, BP control) | Ongoing | Nephrologist |
| 6.8 | Infection surveillance and prophylaxis adjusted for transplant immunosuppression | Ongoing | Transplant Team |

### ESKD Prognosis

| Factor | Implication |
|--------|-------------|
| Renal recovery after induction possible | 50% of dialysis-dependent at diagnosis become independent within 12 weeks |
| Persistent dialysis >12 weeks | Unlikely renal recovery; plan for ESKD pathway |
| Transplantation outcomes | Patient and graft survival comparable to non-AAV ESKD |
| Post-transplant recurrence | 10-20%; higher in PR3-AAV; usually responsive to rituximab |
| ANCA positivity at transplant | Does not contraindicate transplant; does not predict recurrence |
